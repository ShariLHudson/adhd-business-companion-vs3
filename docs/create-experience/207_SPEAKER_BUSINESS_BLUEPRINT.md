# 207 — Speaker Business Blueprint

## Blueprint ID

`business.speaker`

## Purpose

Help a speaker build, organize, operate, and grow a sustainable speaking business—not merely create a presentation.

## Primary outcomes

The user can:

- define their speaker positioning
- identify audiences and buyers
- develop signature talk architecture
- package speaking offers
- determine pricing
- create a speaker marketing system
- build outreach and follow-up
- manage event logistics
- connect speaking to books, coaching, consulting, courses, or other offers
- track leads, bookings, revenue, and outcomes
- create repeatable operating rhythms

## Best-fit users

- keynote speakers
- workshop facilitators
- trainers
- authors adding speaking
- coaches using speaking for lead generation
- consultants using speaking for authority
- emerging speakers
- experienced speakers building a stronger business structure

## Business DNA adaptation

Adapt for:

- first-time versus experienced speakers
- paid versus unpaid speaking
- local versus national markets
- virtual versus in-person delivery
- corporate, association, nonprofit, education, faith-based, or community audiences
- solo speaker versus speaker with team
- speaking as primary income versus supporting channel

## Core blueprint sections

### 1. Speaker Business Foundation

- lived experience
- topics
- values

Outputs:

- Speaker Business Snapshot

### 2. Audience and Buyer Architecture

Separate:

- audience
- event organizer
- sponsor
- decision-maker
- budget owner
- referral partner

Questions:

- Who benefits from the talk?
- Who pays for it?
- Who recommends speakers?
- What outcome does each stakeholder need?
- What concerns prevent booking?
