# 230 — Product-Based Business Blueprint

## Blueprint ID

`business.product_based`

## Purpose

Help a founder turn a product idea or existing product line into a viable, profitable, repeatable business with disciplined product development, sourcing, pricing, production, distribution, and growth.

## Best-fit users

- makers
- inventors
- consumer product founders
- craft businesses
- specialty brands
- product designers
- private-label sellers
- small manufacturers
- productized intellectual property businesses

## Primary outcomes

The user can:

- validate product demand
- define product-market fit
- build a product portfolio
- organize development and sourcing
- calculate full cost and margin
- plan production
- choose sales channels
- protect quality
- manage product lifecycle
- expand responsibly

## Core blueprint sections

### 1. Product Opportunity

Capture:

- customer
- problem or desire
- current alternatives
- product concept
- differentiation
- evidence
- constraints
- strategic role

Outputs:

- Product Opportunity Brief
- Customer Need Map
- Validation Questions

### 2. Validation

Include:

- interviews
- prototypes
- samples
- preorders
- landing pages
- retail feedback
- test sales
- willingness to pay
- use feedback

Outputs:

- Validation Plan
- Evidence Summary
- Go, Revise, or Stop Decision

### 3. Product Architecture

For each product:

- purpose
- customer
- features
- variants
- materials
- packaging
- requirements
- quality standard
- dependencies
- lifecycle stage

Outputs:

- Product Brief
- Product Portfolio Map
- Variant Strategy

### 4. Sourcing and Production

Possible models:

- handmade
- in-house
- contract manufacturing
- private label
- print-on-demand
- assembly
- local production
- international sourcing

Outputs:

- Sourcing Strategy
- Production Model

- Risk Map

- wholesale

- distributors
- events

## Required contributors

- Business Intelligence
- Finance
- Operations
- Marketing
- Sales
- Creative Studio
- Data and Analytics
- Risk and Resilience
- AI Intelligence
- Execution

## Guardrails

- no unsupported safety or regulatory claims
- require professional review for regulated products
- preserve supplier and quality documentation
- do not recommend scaling before unit economics are understood

## Certification requirements

- validation before scale
- full-cost pricing
- production and sourcing risk
- quality control
- lifecycle management
- canonical Work
- Project integration
- exact resume
