# 385 — Creation-to-Knowledge Candidate Handoff Standard

## Purpose

Identify potential reusable knowledge without duplicating the future Knowledge Intelligence architecture.

## Boundary

Creation Strategy Intelligence may identify and package a knowledge candidate.

It does not:

- declare information canonical
- store knowledge in a parallel library
- approve facts
- resolve contradictions
- govern knowledge lifecycle
- determine platform-wide retrieval authority

Those responsibilities belong to the approved Knowledge Intelligence or Intelligence Library authority.

## Candidate triggers

A creation may be a knowledge candidate when it contains:

- stable business facts
- approved operating principles
- reusable explanations
- repeatable methods
- standard decision criteria
- approved service expectations
- recurring process knowledge
- proprietary frameworks
- lessons validated through use

## Required user language

> “This appears to contain guidance you may want to reuse across your business. I can prepare it as a knowledge candidate for your review.”

Do not say:

> “I saved this as business truth.”

unless the canonical knowledge authority has approved and stored it.
