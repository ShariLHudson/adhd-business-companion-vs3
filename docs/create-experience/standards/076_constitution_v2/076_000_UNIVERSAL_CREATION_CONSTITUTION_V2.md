# 076 — Universal Creation Constitution, Version 2

## Status

**Highest-authority platform architecture for every experience in Spark Estate™ that creates, develops, researches, reviews, executes, shares, publishes, or reuses meaningful work.**

This Constitution governs Create, Projects, every Chamber Member, every Round Table Board Director, Founder Studio, Business Estate, Research, Knowledge, Cartography, Decision Compass, templates, strategies, plans, checklists, SOPs, events, frameworks, reports, campaigns, budgets, courses, presentations, and all future creation surfaces.

No destination may create a conflicting local creation model.

---

## 1. Constitutional Mission

Spark Estate must provide one coherent creation experience that protects:

1. the member’s intent;
2. the member’s ownership;
3. the identity of the work;
4. the meaning and context of the work;
5. the member’s progress;
6. the ability to pause and return;
7. the member’s confidence that nothing important was lost;
8. the ability to move from thinking to action without rebuilding the same information.

The platform must help people begin before they have everything figured out, receive useful support without surrendering authorship, and continue without unnecessary friction.

---

## 2. Foundational Promise

> Every meaningful piece of work deserves a thoughtful place to grow.

A work item is not a disposable chat response. It is living work that may move through discovery, drafting, development, research, review, execution, completion, publication, maintenance, reuse, and future revision.

Spark Estate must preserve the lineage of that work across all of those stages.

---

## 3. Constitutional Authority

This Constitution controls when another file, workflow, prompt, implementation, template, or feature conflicts with it.

Order of precedence:

1. Universal Creation Constitution
2. Universal Creation Principles
3. Platform runtime and identity standards
4. Shared capability contracts
5. Consumer integration standards
6. Specialized Chamber, Board, Project, or destination specifications
7. Local UI implementation details

A specialized feature may add domain expertise, views, templates, and workflows. It may not replace the shared identity, persistence, workspace, editing, research, lifecycle, output, or routing architecture.

---

## 5. The Member Owns the Work

The member owns the content, decisions, direction, and final approval.

Spark Estate may:

- recommend;
- draft;
- explain;
- organize;
- compare;
- research;
- identify gaps;
- rewrite selected material;
- propose structures;
- translate plans into execution;
- create linked derivatives;
- help the member finish.

Spark Estate may not:

- silently overwrite member-authored content;
- imply that AI-generated content is final;
- lock editable work without a documented reason;
- make consequential decisions on the member’s behalf;
- remove content without an intentional action;
- create hidden duplicates;
- claim completion without verified execution;
- force the member to repeat context already available and relevant;
- trap the member in a discovery loop.

---

## 6. One Work Item, One Identity

Every meaningful work item must have:

- one immutable Work ID;
- one authoritative original request;
- one current working intent;
- one authoritative classification;
- one human-readable title;
- one durable record;
- one lifecycle state;
- one version history;
- one lineage model;
- one set of permissions;
- one source of truth.

The same work may appear in Create, Projects, Chamber, Board, Welcome Home, Founder Studio, Business Estate, Search, Knowledge, and Cartography.

Those surfaces must resolve to the same Work ID.

A new Work ID is created only by:

- Start New;
- Duplicate;
- Branch;
- Convert to a linked derivative;
- a documented import process.

## 8. Universal Creation Lifecycle

```text
Intent
→ Classification
→ Identity
→ Context
→ Structure
→ Workspace
→ Thinking and Research
→ Development
→ Review
→ Execution
→ Output
→ Completion
→ Reuse or Maintenance
```

The lifecycle is not strictly linear.

Members may:

- skip;
- pause;
- return;
- revise;
- branch;
- convert;
- change direction;
- reopen completed work;
- preserve unresolved questions for later.

The platform must support non-linear progress without losing coherence.

---

## 9. Present Intent Governs Routing

The current work does not permanently own every future turn.

The platform must distinguish:

- continue current work;
- edit current work;
- start something new;
- switch work;
- general conversation;
- research;
- Chamber request;
- Board request;
- Project action;
- navigation;
- clarification.

Present intent must be resolved before stale workflow state or sticky continuity may claim the turn.

A failed handoff may not silently fall through to an incompatible runtime.

---

## 10. “I Don’t Know” Is Supported

“I don’t know” is a valid working state, not a failure.

The platform must respond by doing one or more of the following:

- offer a few plausible options;
- recommend a starting point;
- show a relevant example;
- explain why the decision matters;
- ask one simpler question;
- infer a provisional answer from known context;
- offer research;
- allow Skip for Now;
- preserve the unresolved item.

The platform must not repeat the same question unchanged.

---

## 11. “Give Me Ideas” Is Contextual

Ideas must use:

- current Work ID;
- current section;
- audience;
- goals;
- constraints;
- previous decisions;
- current content;
- relevant specialized intelligence;
- current research when requested.

Ideas should support actions such as:

- Insert;
- Adapt;
- Compare;
- Save for Later;
- Research;
- Turn Into Task;
- Dismiss.

Generic brainstorming without context does not satisfy the standard.

---

## 12. Research Lives Inside the Work

Research is not a separate place members must visit and manually carry back.

Every applicable section and task must be able to request contextual research.

Research must:

- understand intended use;
- preserve sources;
- distinguish fact, interpretation, and recommendation;
- communicate uncertainty;
- support direct write-back;
- create notes or tasks;
- refresh when stale;
- preserve research history.

---

## 13. Everything Applicable Is Editable

Every generated draft, outline, plan, section, table, checklist, summary, recommendation, and research synthesis must be editable when meaningful and safe.

Read-only content is an exception requiring:

- a documented reason;
- visible explanation;
- an available duplicate or adaptation path when appropriate.

---

## 14. Truthful System Behavior

The platform must never use reassuring language to conceal failure.

“Saved” requires verified durable persistence.

“Created” requires a real Work ID and authoritative record.

“Opened” requires a real destination.

“Printed” requires a print action or print-ready output.

“Exported” requires a generated file.

“Sent” requires confirmed delivery.

“Deleted” requires a completed mutation.

When an action fails, the member must know:

- what did not happen;
- whether the work is safe;
- what remains available;
- what can happen next.

---

## 15. No Dead Ends and No Dead Controls

Every visible action must:

1. complete;
2. ask one necessary clarification and wait;
3. explain why it cannot complete and offer the next best action.

A button, link, suggestion, status, menu item, or conversational promise that silently does nothing violates this Constitution.

---

## 16. ADHD-First Architecture

The platform must reduce executive-function load by design.

Required behaviors include:

- one clear next step;
- progressive disclosure;
- useful defaults;
- recommendations rather than undifferentiated options;
- safe pausing;
- return summaries;
- low-energy modes;
- interruption recovery;
- visible save state;
- flexible order;
- reversible actions;
- no shame;
- no completion theater.

The experience should feel calm, present, nonjudgmental, and steady—the therapy-dog lens applied to software behavior.

---

## 17. Shari Is Architectural Behavior

Shari’s voice is not a cosmetic layer.

Her way of supporting members must be expressed through:

- how questions are asked;
- when recommendations are offered;
- how uncertainty is handled;
- how errors are explained;
- how options are reduced;
- how progress is recognized;
- how the member’s ownership is protected.

Shari is warm, practical, honest, observant, and gently confident.

She does not overpraise, pressure, patronize, or take over.

---

## 18. Integration Boundaries

Universal Creation owns:

- the Work ID;
- work content;
- work structure;
- lifecycle;
- version history;
- source lineage.

Projects owns execution.

Research owns retrieval and synthesis.

Chamber Members own specialized guidance.

The Board owns perspective and decision support.

Knowledge owns published reusable knowledge.

Cartography owns visual projections.

These capabilities collaborate through contracts. They do not duplicate ownership.

---

## 19. Security and Privacy

Every creation capability must enforce:

- tenant isolation;
- permissions;
- data minimization;
- protected Founder Studio content;
- safe sharing;
- audit history;
- explicit approval for consequential external actions.

Context may be shared only when relevant and permitted.

---

## 20. Certification

No creation experience is production-ready until browser evidence proves:

- correct routing;
- correct classification;
- correct Work ID;
- correct structure;
- real editing;
- contextual ideas;
- useful “I don’t know” behavior;
- contextual research;
- durable saving;
- refresh and resume;
- version history;
- delete and restore;
- output actions;
- integration continuity;
- stale-session bypass;
- no silent fallthrough;
- no dead controls;
- truthful success and failure states.

---

## 21. Constitutional Anti-Patterns

The following are prohibited:

- local workspace forks;
- local save engines;
- local Work ID models;
- local research experiences that cannot write back;
- hidden duplicate creation;
- generic AI buttons without context;
- read-only AI outputs;
- stale session ownership;
- false success messages;
- repeated discovery loops;
- disconnected Projects;
- output files with no source lineage;
- UI controls that are not implemented;
- features certified only by unit tests.

---

## 22. Amendment Rule

A constitutional amendment requires:

- documented problem;
- cross-platform impact analysis;
- compatibility review;
- migration plan;
- certification update;
- explicit approval by the platform architecture owner.

Local implementations may not amend the Constitution by behavior.

---

## 23. Final Constitutional Test

A compliant experience allows a member to say:

> “I could begin before I knew everything. It understood what I was trying to do. My work stayed mine. I could change anything. I knew what was saved. I could stop and return. I always had a useful next step. And I never had to learn a different creation experience in each part of Spark Estate.”
