# 588 — Strategy, Positioning & Audience Framework

## Foundation decisions
Help the user define:
- who the community serves
- what members share
- what they want to solve, learn, experience, or accomplish
- why the group should exist
- what it will and will not provide
- how it differs from alternatives
- what success means for members and the founder

## Community types
Lead nurture, customer support, paid membership, course/program, peer, professional, local, event, alumni, cause, learning, accountability, interest, referral/partner, or private team.

## Positioning framework
**Audience:** This community is for [specific people] who are [shared stage or goal].

**Problem:** They are trying to [desired progress] but often experience [meaningful friction].

**Promise:** This group helps members [credible outcome] through [distinctive support or experience].

**Difference:** Unlike groups that [common weakness], this community emphasizes [important difference].

**Belonging:** Members should feel [three qualities].

## Promise quality test
Relevant, credible, understandable, sustainable, member-centered, not a guaranteed result, and consistent with founder capacity.

## Founder capacity check
Ask:
- How much time is realistic each week?
- Who moderates?
- Is there a VA or team?
- Does it support an existing offer?
- Is live participation comfortable?
- What will make room for this work?

## Viability assessment
Audience clarity, shared need, credibility, differentiation, content sustainability, moderation capacity, acquisition access, retention value, resources, and risk.

## Output
A Community Foundation Brief containing audience, purpose, promise, difference, type, goals, success definition, capacity assumptions, risks, and initial recommendation.
