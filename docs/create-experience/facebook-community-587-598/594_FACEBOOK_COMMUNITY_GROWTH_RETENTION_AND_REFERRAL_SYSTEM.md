# 594 — Growth, Retention & Referral System

## Principle
Growth should follow member value, belonging, participation, and retention. Growth without retention creates a larger empty room.

## Channels
Client onboarding, customer success, email, lead magnets, webinars, workshops, speaking, podcasts, collaborations, partners, website, social channels, challenges, events, referrals, alumni, associations, and local partnerships.

## Growth loop
Enter → receive value → participate → experience progress/belonging → refer → relevant person joins → community value increases.

## Retention drivers
Clear purpose, relevant conversation, consistent value, trust, recognition, relationships, progress, founder presence, predictability, events, contribution, low noise, and healthy moderation.

## Risks
Promotion-heavy feed, irrelevant posts, unanswered questions, founder disappearance, excessive notifications, conflict, repetition, no member voice, over-programming, unclear boundaries, inconsistent moderation.

## Referral system
Invitation moments, referral language, partner process, ambassador option, attribution, and thank-you. Avoid contests that reward low-fit invitations.

## Stage heuristics
0–25 conversation; 25–100 rhythm; 100–500 systems; 500–1,000 leadership/segmentation; 1,000+ governance and discovery. These are planning heuristics, not guarantees.

## Re-engagement
Listening question, re-entry post, survey, seasonal restart, reorientation, appropriate email, and community reset.

## Output
Growth strategy, channel priorities, referral system, retention plan, re-engagement, partnerships, monthly review, and experiment backlog.
