# 595 — Moderation, Safety & Governance

## Components
Rules, member expectations, admin/moderator authority, review, warnings, removal, reconsideration where appropriate, crisis escalation, confidentiality, documentation, and team communication.

## Principles
Be consistent, clear, proportionate, respectful, timely, documented, and protective. Avoid public humiliation and favoritism. Distinguish disagreement from abuse.

## Levels
**Level 1:** off-topic, duplicate, accidental promotion, misunderstanding. Use reminder, edit request, redirect, education.

**Level 2:** repeated promotion, hostile interaction, ignored requests, misleading claims, unsolicited messages. Use warning, removal, approval controls, temporary limits where available, documentation.

**Level 3:** harassment, threats, hate, fraud, doxxing, predatory behavior, dangerous misinformation, repeated serious violations. Remove content/member, preserve evidence, report to Facebook where appropriate, and escalate safety concerns.

## Moderator handbook
Role, tone, response windows, authority, escalation ladder, examples, private-message templates, documentation, conflicts of interest, and moderator wellbeing.

## Templates
Reminder, clarification, post removal, warning, final warning, temporary restriction, removal, appeal response, spam decline, unsolicited-DM warning, de-escalation, and safety follow-up.

## Privacy
Collect only what is needed. Do not reuse member stories without consent. Never promise absolute confidentiality on a third-party platform. Protect screenshots, exports, and admin notes.

## Output
Rules, Moderator Handbook, escalation matrix, response templates, incident log, safety checklist, and continuity plan.
