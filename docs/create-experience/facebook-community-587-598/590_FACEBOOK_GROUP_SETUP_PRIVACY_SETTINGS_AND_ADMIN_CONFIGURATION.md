# 590 — Setup, Privacy, Settings & Administration

## Setup sequence
1. Open Facebook Groups and choose Create.
2. Enter the approved name.
3. Choose privacy.
4. Choose visibility where available.
5. Upload cover image.
6. Add About description.
7. Configure membership.
8. Add rules.
9. Configure posting and moderation.
10. Add admins/moderators.
11. Review relevant features.
12. Test as a member.

## Privacy decision
**Public:** discoverability and publicly viewable content matter.

**Private visible:** content is member-only but the group should be discoverable.

**Private hidden:** invitation-based, paid, client, internal, cohort, or sensitive community.

## Privacy safeguard
Warn that privacy changes can be restricted or irreversible. Require the user to confirm current Meta rules before making a final choice.

## Membership questions
Create no more than three:
1. What interested you in joining?
2. What are you hoping to learn, solve, or experience?
3. Do you agree to the rules and no unsolicited promotion?

Do not request unnecessary sensitive information.

## Rules
Respect, relevance, promotion, confidentiality, claims, harassment, private solicitation, intellectual property, events/offers, reporting, moderation decisions, and consequences.

## Team roles
Owner, primary admin, backup admin, moderators, content lead, member-experience lead, technical support, escalation owner.

## Automated moderation
Where available, configure Admin Assist or similar controls for membership requests, spam, keyword alerts, suspicious links, repeated violations, and post/comment review. Human judgment remains required for sensitive cases.

## Feature review
Only recommend features relevant to the group and available in that group, such as guides, events, polls, files, live video, badges, anonymous participation, chats, approval controls, experts, and moderation alerts.

## Output
Group Configuration Workbook with privacy/visibility rationale, membership questions, rules, roles, permissions, automation choices, setup checklist, and member-view test checklist.
