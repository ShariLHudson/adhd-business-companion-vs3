# Estate Collection User Journey™

**How someone lives inside these rooms for ten years — not how they use them once.**

This document designs the complete visitor experience across months and years. It is **not UI** and **not code**. It describes what should *feel true* when the framework is mature.

## The ten-year promise

After years in the Estate, a visitor should feel:

- *"Spark remembers what matters — I don't have to."*
- *"I know where to rest a thought without thinking about software."*
- *"My story is here — growing, connected, honored — not scattered."*
- *"Nothing nags me. When Spark speaks, it helps."*

The rooms are **destinations**, not databases. The journey is **relational**, not transactional.

---

## Universal lifecycle (every room)

Every entry — regardless of room — shares this spine. Room-specific texture comes in the sections below.

| Stage | What happens | Visitor feels | Spark's posture |
|-------|----------------|---------------|-----------------|
| **1. Moment** | Something meaningful occurs in life or conversation | Seen, not judged | Listening |
| **2. Recognition** | Spark quietly classifies *what kind* of moment this is | Understood | Invisible work |
| **3. Permission** | One warm question: *Would you like to…?* | In control | Humble offer |
| **4. Acceptance** | Visitor says yes (or picks another room) | Agency | Opens with draft |
| **5. Arrival** | Room opens; atmosphere matches emotional need | *I'd like to stay here* | Silent hospitality |
| **6. Draft** | Prefill from conversation; visitor edits freely | Safety to change | No rush |
| **7. Save** | Visitor chooses to rest the entry | Certainty — nothing lost | Quiet confirmation |
| **8. Return** | Days, months, or years later — browse or Spark recalls | Continuity | Gentle memory |
| **9. Reference** | Spark mentions entry when genuinely relevant | *I'm glad I didn't re-explain* | Permission to recall |
| **10. Evolution** | Entry may link, deepen, or inspire another room | Story compounding | Suggest, never force |

**Non-negotiables across all stages:** no auto-save · no streaks · no guilt on absence · no surveillance language · one primary suggestion at a time.

## Journal Gazebo — lifecycle

**Room feeling:** A white gazebo in quiet light. Personal. Reflective. The page is the hero.

### Stages 1–10 (Journal-specific)

| Stage | Journal experience |
|-------|-------------------|
| **1. Moment** | A feeling surfaces — overwhelm, gratitude, an idea, a hard day, a dream, a family story. |
| **2. Recognition** | Spark hears *processing*, not *proving* or *finishing*. |
| **3. Permission** | *"That feels like a meaningful reflection. Would you like to save it in your Journal?"* |
| **4. Acceptance** | Visitor arrives at the Gazebo — writing surface already open, scene soft, noise low. |
| **5. Arrival** | Compose zone leads the page. Prompts and Spark line sit below — never competing. |
| **6. Draft** | Conversation text may prefill the page; title optional; category whisper-light. |
| **7. Save** | *"Rest this page"* — calm confirmation. Entry joins *Pages at rest*. |
| **8. Return** | Browse by search, kind of entry, or favorites. Reading old pages feels like a quiet book. |
| **9. Reference** | *"Last spring you wrote about feeling stretched thin — does any of that still feel true?"* |
| **10. Evolution** | A journal insight may become Greenhouse growth, Garden bloom, or seed for Evidence when a solve emerges. |

### Return visits — Journal

| Situation | Experience |
|-----------|------------|
| **Same week** | Continue writing on a related page; treasure a line. |
| **After absence** | *"I'm glad you're here."* — not *welcome back, you were gone 47 days*. |
| **Browse** | Scroll *Pages at rest* like reading old letters — large type, breathing room. |
| **Related entries** | Spark may quietly surface 1–2 pages with similar themes — *"You wrote about this before"* — never a list of ten. |
| **Similar thoughts** | When member voices recurring theme: *"This echoes something you explored in March."* |
| **Seasonal rhythm** | Gratitude entries cluster in autumn; stress before launches — Spark notices patterns, never lectures. |

### Years 1 → 10 — Journal

- **Year 1:** Sparse pages. Trust forming. Spark offers rarely, perfectly.
- **Years 2–3:** Richer browse. Favorites become *treasured* pages. Ideas in journal spawn Greenhouse entries.
- **Years 4–6:** Spark helps member see themes: creativity, family, faith, business courage — as observations, not identity labels.
- **Years 7–10:** Journal becomes personal archive. Member asks *"What was I thinking when we started the business?"* — Spark retrieves meaning, not filenames.

## Growth Greenhouse — lifecycle

**Room feeling:** Warm glass, patient light. Hope without deadline. Nothing checklist-driven.

### Stages 1–10 (Greenhouse-specific)

| Stage | Greenhouse experience |
|-------|----------------------|
| **1. Moment** | Something is *becoming* — patience, a habit, a skill, a relationship, an idea not ready. |
| **2. Recognition** | Spark hears *not yet*, *trying*, *learning to*, *early roots*. |
| **3. Permission** | *"That sounds like something still growing. Would you like to place it in the Growth Greenhouse?"* |
| **4. Acceptance** | Visitor enters hopeful atmosphere — organic copy, no progress bars. |
| **5. Arrival** | Compose invites *what is growing* and *what nurturing would look like*. |
| **6. Draft** | Season language optional (*early roots*, *midsummer*) — never percentage complete. |
| **7. Save** | *"Planted gently."* Entry joins *Still becoming*. |
| **8. Return** | Revisit growth entries like checking on seedlings — not overdue tasks. |
| **9. Reference** | *"You've been tending patience with your team for a while now."* |
| **10. Evolution** | When growth produces proof → Evidence. When it finishes → Achievement. Feelings about the journey → Journal. |

### Return visits — Greenhouse

| Situation | Experience |
|-----------|------------|
| **Weekly check-in** | Only if member invites or conversation naturally returns — *"How is that boundary practice feeling?"* |
| **Stalled growth** | No shame. *"Some seasons are dormant. Still worth keeping here."* |
| **Breakthrough** | Spark notices shift in conversation — *"This sounds like it moved from tender to real — want proof in the Vault?"* |
| **Browse** | Entries read like garden labels — what is growing, what season, what nurture looked like. |
| **Related growth** | *"This connects to the communication work you placed here last year."* |

### Years 1 → 10 — Greenhouse

- **Year 1:** A few tender seedlings. Member learns this room is for *becoming*, not *done*.
- **Years 2–4:** Habits and skills mature; some entries archived quietly when complete.
- **Years 5–7:** Greenhouse shows character arc — resilience, leadership, health — through seasons, not scores.
- **Years 8–10:** Member visits to plant new growth after major life chapters; old entries become origin stories for Achievements.

## Evidence Vault — lifecycle

**Room feeling:** Private, safe, important. Confidence without administration.

### Stages 1–10 (Evidence-specific)

| Stage | Evidence experience |
|-------|---------------------|
| **1. Moment** | A hard thing was handled — client, conflict, crisis, negotiation, family problem. |
| **2. Recognition** | Spark hears *solved*, *handled*, *prevented*, *figured out* — stakes matter. |
| **3. Permission** | *"This sounds like strong evidence. Would you like to preserve it in the Evidence Vault?"* |
| **4. Acceptance** | Dark vault atmosphere — warm text, private tone. |
| **5. Arrival** | Structured prompts feel like testimony, not forms: situation → problem → what moved forward → why it mattered. |
| **6. Draft** | Conversation may prefill situation and action; visitor adds truth and detail. |
| **7. Save** | Quiet lock-in. *"Preserved in the vault."* |
| **8. Return** | Browse feels like reviewing proof of capability — especially before hard days. |
| **9. Reference** | *"You solved something similar when the billing crisis hit — want to remember how you handled it?"* |
| **10. Evolution** | Strong evidence may become Achievement (case study, process, product). Pair with Garden when member wants to celebrate the win. |

### Return visits — Evidence

| Situation | Experience |
|-----------|------------|
| **Before hard meeting** | Spark may offer relevant vault memory — only when useful, never as prep homework. |
| **Confidence wavering** | *"You've handled difficult clients before. One example is in your vault from last winter."* |
| **Interview / talk prep** | With permission: *"You've preserved several stories of initiative — want to revisit one?"* |
| **Similar problem** | *"This reminds me of the conflict you navigated in March — different context, same steadiness."* |
| **Browse** | Records read as quiet proof — category as whisper, body as narrative. |

### Years 1 → 10 — Evidence

- **Year 1:** A handful of saves. Member learns vault is for *capability*, not bragging.
- **Years 2–4:** Vault becomes quiet arsenal for entrepreneurial doubt.
- **Years 5–7:** Patterns emerge — negotiation, calm under fire, customer rescue — fed to bios and talks with permission.
- **Years 8–10:** Member's children or successors might hear stories sourced from vault — always member-owned, never Spark-initiated exposure.

## Achievement Library — lifecycle

**Room feeling:** An elegant private library. Treasured volumes — not folders, not cards.

### Stages 1–10 (Achievement-specific)

| Stage | Achievement experience |
|-------|------------------------|
| **1. Moment** | Something was created or completed — book, course, business, site, certification, major project. |
| **2. Recognition** | Spark hears *finished*, *built*, *published*, *launched*, *created*. |
| **3. Permission** | *"That sounds like part of your life's work. Would you like a place on the shelf?"* |
| **4. Acceptance** | Library atmosphere — spines, optional cover keepsake, serif calm. |
| **5. Arrival** | Title on spine, story behind the volume, year optional. |
| **6. Draft** | Title and story prefill; achievement type gentle. |
| **7. Save** | *"Resting on the shelf."* Volume joins *Volumes on the shelf*. |
| **8. Return** | Browse feels like walking the stacks — one column, unhurried, beautiful at 500 entries. |
| **9. Reference** | *"This connects to the workshop you placed on the shelf in 2024."* |
| **10. Evolution** | Celebrate in Garden. Honor in Hall if landmark. Link Evidence if origin was a hard solve. |

### Return visits — Achievement

| Situation | Experience |
|-----------|------------|
| **Resume / bio** | *"Several volumes might speak to this — want to browse the shelf together?"* |
| **New project** | *"Your first course is still on the shelf — this new one might sit beside it someday."* |
| **Anniversary of launch** | *"Three years since you placed the membership site here."* — observation, not party unless member wants. |
| **Related accomplishments** | *"This accomplishment relates to the podcast season you shelved last year."* |
| **Browse for pleasure** | Member stays to read old stories — coffee-table book aesthetic. |

### Years 1 → 10 — Achievement

- **Year 1:** First volumes feel precious. Shelf mostly empty — room still beautiful.
- **Years 2–4:** Shelf fills; spines tell a career story without Spark narrating it unprompted.
- **Years 5–7:** Marketing, legacy planning, and talks draw from library — always permission.
- **Years 8–10:** *Body of work* is visible. Member's Estate tells an entrepreneurial life through volumes, not metrics.

## Celebration Garden — lifecycle

**Room feeling:** Joyful, peaceful, hopeful, elegant. Wins *planted* — never gamified.

### Stages 1–10 (Garden-specific)

| Stage | Garden experience |
|-------|-------------------|
| **1. Moment** | Something went well — small courage, health choice, email sent, family joy, client yes. |
| **2. Recognition** | Spark hears *win*, *went well*, *proud*, *brave*, *good moment*. |
| **3. Permission** | *"That sounds worth celebrating. Would you like to plant it in the Celebration Garden?"* |
| **4. Acceptance** | Garden atmosphere — bloom layout, stem mark, no badges or points. |
| **5. Arrival** | *What are we celebrating?* · *Why it matters?* · optional photo keepsake. |
| **6. Draft** | Win text prefill; gratitude and date optional. |
| **7. Save** | *"Planted in the garden."* Bloom joins *In bloom*. |
| **8. Return** | Stroll the garden — wins visible, mood lifts without toxic positivity. |
| **9. Reference** | *"One year ago today you planted a win about asking for help."* |
| **10. Evolution** | Proof-heavy wins → Evidence. Landmark joy → Hall (rare). Memory-rich joy → Journal link. |

### Return visits — Celebration Garden

| Situation | Experience |
|-----------|------------|
| **Hard season** | Spark may retrieve one bloom — *"Remember when you stayed calm that day?"* — one, not a slideshow. |
| **Anniversary bloom** | *"One year ago today…"* — gentle, optional reflection. |
| **Browse** | Reading blooms feels like walking a path — stems, quiet dates, no leaderboard. |
| **Minimized wins** | Member says *"it's not a big deal"* — Spark still offers: *"Small things count here."* |
| **Seasonal garden** | Spring energy vs winter blooms — atmosphere may shift; entries eternal. |

### Years 1 → 10 — Celebration Garden

- **Year 1:** Garden teaches that small wins matter. Density low, dignity high.
- **Years 2–4:** Hundreds of blooms; browse on hard days becomes self-care ritual.
- **Years 5–7:** Anniversaries compound — member sees years of courage in small moments.
- **Years 8–10:** Garden balances Hall's grandeur — daily joy alongside life's chapters.

## Celebration Hall — lifecycle

**Room feeling:** Rare. Museum quality. Slow down. Honor.

### Stages 1–10 (Hall-specific)

| Stage | Hall experience |
|-------|-----------------|
| **1. Moment** | A life chapter — wedding, retirement, first book, graduation, acquisition, grandchild. |
| **2. Recognition** | Spark hears *landmark*, *once in a lifetime*, *chapter*, *I'll tell this for years*. |
| **3. Permission** | *"That feels like a chapter worth honoring. Would you like to preserve it in the Celebration Hall?"* |
| **4. Acceptance** | Grand atmosphere — pace slows, typography breathes, museum layout. |
| **5. Arrival** | Chapter title, milestone date, story of the honored season. |
| **6. Draft** | Title and narrative prefill; dignity over detail overload. |
| **7. Save** | *"Honored in the hall."* Chapter joins *Honored chapters*. |
| **8. Return** | Visitor lingers — reads chapters like plaques, not posts. |
| **9. Reference** | *"Five years since you honored your business launch here."* |
| **10. Evolution** | Links to Achievement volumes, Garden blooms, and Journal reflections from the same era — woven, not duplicated. |

### Return visits — Celebration Hall

| Situation | Experience |
|-----------|------------|
| **Milestone anniversary** | *"Five years since this chapter."* — invitation to reflect, not perform. |
| **Life review** | Member browses Hall for perspective — Spark silent unless asked. |
| **Family sharing** | Member chooses to show a chapter — Spark never pushes sharing. |
| **New chapter** | Rare new entry — Hall stays sparse; scarcity preserves meaning. |
| **Timeline view** | Future: life chapters in order — a quiet timeline, not a dashboard. |

### Years 1 → 10 — Celebration Hall

- **Year 1:** Perhaps one chapter. Room feels open, dignified, unhurried.
- **Years 2–5:** Chapters accumulate slowly — wedding, launch, award — each distinct.
- **Years 6–8:** Hall becomes life map anchor; Achievements and Garden provide texture around chapters.
- **Years 9–10:** Member sees legacy arc — Spark helps only when member asks *"What have been my biggest chapters?"*

## Connections — meaningful pathways

Connections are **invitations**, not automation. Spark suggests the next room only when the story naturally wants another home.

```
                    ┌─────────────┐
                    │   Journal   │
                    │  (meaning)  │
                    └──────┬──────┘
                           │ reveals
                           ▼
                    ┌─────────────┐
                    │  Greenhouse │
                    │ (becoming)  │
                    └──────┬──────┘
                           │ matures
              ┌────────────┼────────────┐
              ▼                         ▼
       ┌─────────────┐           ┌─────────────┐
       │  Evidence   │           │ Achievement │
       │   (proof)   │           │   (work)    │
       └──────┬──────┘           └──────┬──────┘
              │                         │
              └────────────┬────────────┘
                           │ may bloom
                           ▼
                    ┌─────────────┐
                    │   Garden    │
                    │   (joy)     │
                    └──────┬──────┘
                           │ rare elevation
                           ▼
                    ┌─────────────┐
                    │    Hall     │
                    │  (chapter)  │
                    └─────────────┘
```

### Connection rules

| Path | When it becomes meaningful | Spark's line (example) |
|------|---------------------------|------------------------|
| **Journal → Growth** | Reflection names an ongoing arc | *"This sounds like something still growing — want to tend it in the Greenhouse?"* |
| **Growth → Evidence** | Growth produces a concrete solve | *"That patience paid off in a real situation — want proof in the Vault?"* |
| **Growth → Achievement** | Growth completes into finished work | *"Sounds like this graduated from growing to done — ready for the shelf?"* |
| **Evidence → Achievement** | Solve becomes case study, product, or published process | *"This story might sit beside your other work on the shelf."* |
| **Achievement → Garden** | Completion deserves joy | Often same conversation: *"…and plant a bloom?"* |
| **Achievement → Hall** | Work is life-defining scale | *"This feels like a Hall chapter — not just a volume."* |
| **Garden → Evidence** | Win was actually a solve | *"Want to preserve how you handled that — not just the win?"* |
| **Garden → Journal** | Win is really a memory | *"Want a quiet page for the fuller story?"* |
| **Hall → *(all)*`** | Chapter links to era | Cross-references in copy when member browses — not duplicate saves |

**Forced connection anti-patterns (never):**

- Auto-creating a second entry when the first saves.
- Showing "Related entries" as a busy widget on every card.
- Requiring links to save.
- Suggesting Hall for routine achievements.

## Spark's role — Estate Concierge

Spark is present across the ten-year journey — not in the UI chrome, but in conversation and quiet memory.

### Spark gently…

| Role | Behavior | Example |
|------|----------|---------|
| **Reminds** | Recalls when it saves effort — not to create obligation | *"You wrote about this before — want to see it?"* |
| **Connects** | Links ideas across rooms when story warrants | *"This bloom connects to the volume you finished that spring."* |
| **Encourages** | Names courage without hype | *"That took steadiness."* |
| **Celebrates** | Offers Garden — never throws confetti | *"Worth planting?"* |
| **Notices patterns** | Observations, not conclusions | *"You've mentioned boundaries several times this season."* |

### Spark never…

- Nags about incomplete entries or visit frequency.
- Pressures toward "more" saves, links, or rooms.
- Breaks room atmosphere with software language (*saved successfully*, *3 items*).
- Surfaces memory that would feel creepy (*"I noticed you've been sad"*).
- Interrupts support conversations with collection offers.

### Concierge timing

| When | Spark |
|------|-------|
| Member overwhelmed | Support only — no collection |
| Member sharing a win | One offer — Garden or better fit |
| Member returning after long absence | Warm presence — no inventory of what they missed |
| Member browsing a room | Silent unless asked — atmosphere does the work |
| Member asks *"where should this go?"* | One recommendation + permission — not a lecture on six rooms |

## Living in the Estate — year by year

### Years 1–2: Trust

- Few entries per room. Each save feels significant.
- Spark offers rarely, accurately.
- Visitor learns room *character* — Gazebo for heart, Greenhouse for becoming, Vault for proof, Library for work, Garden for joy, Hall for chapters.
- Connections almost never suggested — single rooms suffice.

### Years 3–5: Density

- Browse becomes pleasurable — especially Journal and Garden.
- Return visits common; Spark references grow more precise.
- Cross-room suggestions appear naturally — Achievement + Garden, Journal → Greenhouse.
- Evidence vault used before hard meetings and during doubt.

### Years 6–8: Story

- Entries form eras: early business, family chapter, health season.
- Spark helps member see narrative — *"A lot of your growth entries from this period were about delegation."*
- Achievement Library becomes legacy resource.
- Hall holds defining chapters; Garden holds the daily courage between them.

### Years 9–10: Legacy

- Member may ask big questions: *"What have I built?"* · *"How have I grown?"* · *"What am I proud of?"*
- Spark draws from collections conversationally — bios, reflections, family stories — always permission.
- New entries slow; refinement and revisiting dominate.
- The Estate feels like a **home visited over a lifetime** — not an app used and abandoned.

## Experience design tests (every room, every release)

Before shipping any change to collection rooms, ask:

1. **First visit:** Would someone want to stay five quiet minutes?
2. **Tenth visit:** Does browse still feel beautiful — not crowded?
3. **Hundredth entry:** Does the room still feel like a destination at scale?
4. **Return after absence:** Dignity preserved — no guilt?
5. **Spark reference:** Would Shari say this out loud — warm, not robotic?
6. **Connection offer:** Does a second room add *distinct* value — or noise?
7. **Ten-year view:** Would this entry still matter — and still be findable?

If any answer is no → refine experience, not features.
