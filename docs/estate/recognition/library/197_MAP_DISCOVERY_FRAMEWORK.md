## Purpose

Define the standard workflow every visual map follows.

Every map type has its own line of thinking.

The Discovery Interview must match the type of map selected.

---

## Core Principle

The map is the output.

The Discovery Interview is how Spark gathers the right information.

The member should not be forced to build from a blank canvas.

Spark asks useful questions, uses available context, creates the first draft, then lets the member refine it.

---

## Universal Map Flow

1. Choose Map
2. Choose or detect source context
3. Conduct map-specific Discovery Interview
4. Spark analyzes responses
5. Spark builds first draft
6. Member reviews
7. Member adds, deletes, renames, and reorders
8. Spark suggests improvements
9. Member saves, converts, exports, or continues

---

## Context Before Questions

Before asking questions, Spark should check for available context.

Possible sources:

- Current conversation
- Clear My Mind™ session
- Current project
- Journal entry
- Decision Compass™
- Uploaded notes
- Previous visual map

If Spark already knows the answer, it should not ask again.

---

## Discovery Interview Style

The Discovery Interview should feel conversational.

Avoid long forms.

Ask one clear question at a time unless the member asks to fill everything at once.

Spark should allow text, voice, pasted lists, rough thoughts, skipped answers, and added-later answers.

---

## First Draft Standard

Spark should not create an empty map unless the member explicitly asks to start blank.

The first draft should include:

- Main topic
- Initial sections
- Known items
- Suggested groupings
- Suggested connections
- Missing-information prompts

---

## Review Standard

After creating the first draft, Spark should ask:

"Does this look like the right starting point?"

Options:

- Yes, keep going
- Add something
- Remove something
- Change the structure
- Show this another way

---

## Map-Specific Interviews

### Decision Map
- What decision are you trying to make?
- What options are you considering?
- What criteria matter?
- What risks or concerns should we include?

### Relationship Map
- What relationships are you trying to understand?
- Who or what should be included?
- What known connections already exist?
- What hidden connections should Spark look for?

### Timeline Map
- What are we planning or reviewing over time?
- What dates or milestones already exist?
- What deadlines matter?
- What is unknown?

### Process Map
- What process are we mapping?
- Where does it start?
- Where does it end?
- What steps currently happen?
- Where does it get stuck?

### Mind Map
- What is the central topic?
- What ideas, branches, or related thoughts should be included?
- Should Spark organize them automatically?

---

## Member Customization

The member can always add, delete, rename, reorder, connect, disconnect, save, or transform map content.

---

## Success Criteria

The Discovery Framework succeeds when:

- Every map starts with the right kind of questions.
- Spark builds useful first drafts.
- The member can reshape the map freely.
- No member is trapped in a rigid template.
