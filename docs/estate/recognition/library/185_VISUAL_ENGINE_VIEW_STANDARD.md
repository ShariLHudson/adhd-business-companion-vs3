## Purpose

Define the approved visual views available inside the Spark Visual Engine™.

The Visual Engine should not be treated as one mind map feature. It is a shared visual layer that can show the same information in different ways depending on what the member is trying to understand.

---

## Core Principle

One set of information may have many valid visual views.

The member may choose the view directly.

Spark may recommend a view.

The member can switch views at any time without losing data.

---

## Approved Views

## 1. Thought Map

Best for:
- Clear My Mind™
- Brain dumps
- Mixed thoughts
- Overwhelm
- Random ideas

Purpose:
Show clusters, loose connections, repeated themes, and possible next steps.

---

## 2. Mind Map

Best for:
- A central topic
- Branching ideas
- Concepts
- Teaching
- Workshop planning
- Course planning

Purpose:
Start with one central idea and branch outward.

---

## 3. Project Map

Best for:
- Projects
- Work in progress
- Milestones
- Tasks
- Resources

Purpose:
Show how goals, milestones, tasks, and next actions connect.

---

## 4. Process Map

Best for:
- Workflows
- SOPs
- Systems
- Business processes
- Client journeys
- Automation planning

Purpose:
Show steps in order.

---

## 5. Relationship Map

Best for:
- How ideas connect
- People
- Roles
- Themes
- Patterns
- Business ecosystems

Purpose:
Show connections that are not necessarily step-by-step.

---

## 6. Timeline View

Best for:
- Dates
- Phases
- Launch plans
- Roadmaps
- Sequences
- “What happens first?”

Purpose:
Show order over time.

---

## 7. Priority View

Best for:
- Choosing what to do next
- ADHD overwhelm
- Quick wins
- Energy matching
- Urgency vs importance

Purpose:
Help the member decide where attention should go.

---

## 8. Decision View

Best for:
- Options
- Pros and cons
- Risks
- Unknowns
- Tradeoffs
- Next best step

Purpose:
Make decisions visible.

---

## 9. Possibility View

Best for:
- Expansion
- Brainstorming
- Offers
- Product ideas
- Creative options
- “What could this become?”

Purpose:
Help the member see potential paths without committing yet.

---

## 10. Journey View

Best for:
- Customer journey
- Member journey
- Learning path
- Transformation path
- Workshop experience

Purpose:
Show the experience from beginning to end.

---

## View Switching

Members may switch between views without losing information.

Example:

Thought Map
→ Project Map
→ Timeline
→ Priority View

The underlying data remains the same.

Only the perspective changes.

---

## Spark Recommendation Logic

Spark may recommend:

- Thought Map when input is messy or mixed.
- Mind Map when there is one central topic.
- Process Map when the content contains steps.
- Project Map when tasks and goals appear.
- Timeline when dates or sequence appear.
- Decision View when options appear.
- Possibility View when the member wants expansion.
- Priority View when the member is stuck choosing what to do next.

---

## Anti-Rules

Do not force the member into one view.

Do not ask unnecessary questions when the member requested a specific view.

Do not treat Visual Thinking™ as only a mind map.

Do not make the member re-enter information when switching views.

---

## Success Criteria

The member should feel:

“I can finally see this clearly.”

Not:

“I am using diagram software.”
