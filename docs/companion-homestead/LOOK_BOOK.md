## Overall Vision

The **Companion Homestead** is **Shari's home**.

- Every room belongs to Shari.
- Every room has a purpose.
- Every room makes an **Emotional Promise**.
- Every room should feel **warm, inviting, authentic, creative, colorful, and unmistakably Shari**.
- The user is always a **welcomed guest** — never a customer, never an operator, never an account.

### The Iowa Setting (Universal)

Shari's homestead sits on rolling Iowa land — not a coast, not a mountain retreat. Honest Midwest sky. Corn and soy fields at the horizon. Oak and maple in the yard. Barn red accents where architecture allows. White trim. Wide porches. Screen doors that remember summer. Winter that earns the fireplace. Spring mud and hope. Autumn that deserves a slow walk.

**Never:** coastal clichés, generic loft aesthetics, Silicon Valley glass boxes, corporate office parks disguised as "home."

### Companion Presence — Reference Key

| Level | Meaning | When to use |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| **Host** | Shari is fully present — conversation is the experience | Arrival, celebration, creative partnership |
| **Partner** | Working alongside — warmth without hovering | Making, building, co-creating |
| **Nearby** | Support within reach — not interrupting | Planning, strategy, end-of-day reflection |
| **Prepared** | Evidence she was here — room speaks first | Focus, learning, garden, library |
| **Invisible** | Intentional absence — attention is sacred | Deep focus, protected work |

### Restoration Promise — Reference

Every room must answer: *What is better when they leave?*

`lighter` · `calmer` · `clearer` · `more hopeful` · `more focused` · `more confident` · `more creative` · `more organized` · `more understood`

### UI Integration — Universal Rules

The room never competes with usability. Navigation (House Map + Toolbelt) stays stable. The environment lives in the **content canvas**.

| Layer | Rule |
|-------|------|
| **Hero environment** | Top 20–30% for work rooms; up to 95% for Living Room arrival only |
| **Soft focus overlay** | Warm gradient behind copy — never raw text on busy photography |
| **Workspace** | Legible, dominant in work rooms — cream/warm white surfaces, not glass-on-photo |
| **Cards** | Paper-like — subtle shadow, rounded corners, room-tinted borders |
| **Buttons** | Warm solid fills or soft outlines — never neon productivity UI |
| **Conversation** | Lower third or sidebar chip — never blocking the room's emotional center |
| **Navigation** | Persistent chrome — homestead does not replace menus |

## Front Porch
<a id="front-porch"></a>

### 1. Hero Concept

A wide Iowa farmhouse porch stretches across the front of Shari's homestead — painted white trim over weathered wood, barn-red rocking chairs spaced with intentional hospitality, and a screen door that remembers every summer arrival. The **welcome mat** sits centered on worn floorboards that have earned their soft gray patina from boots, rain, and honest Midwest seasons. A porch swing moves almost imperceptibly in the breeze. Oak and maple frame the yard beyond — rolling land dipping toward corn and soy at the horizon under an enormous, unapologetic sky. Wicker baskets hold a folded quilt and a pair of garden shoes. A galvanized bucket catches rainwater beside the steps. Porch light glows warm even in daylight — a promise, not a demand. The air smells like cut grass, distant soil after rain, and the faint cedar of the posts. This is not a stage set. It is the threshold where the outside world loosens its grip and home begins.

### 2. Emotional Promise

**When someone enters this room they should immediately feel...** that they made it home — safe to exhale before anything is asked of them.

### 3. Restoration Promise

**When they leave they should feel...** *transitioned* — the noise of the world left behind on the other side of the porch rail, body unclenched enough to step inside.

### 4. Signature Objects

- **Welcome mat** — hand-lettered, slightly faded, honest wear at the center where feet land
- Barn-red rocking chairs with cream cushions, sun-softened
- Porch swing with a braided rope and a folded quilt
- White-painted railings with geraniums in clay pots
- Screen door with a hydraulic sigh and a brass latch
- Galvanized rain bucket beside the steps
- Wind chimes in tarnished copper — present, never insistent
- A small side table holding a jar of wildflowers in season

### 5. Companion Presence

**Level:** Nearby — **Why:** Shari meets guests at the threshold without crowding it — warmth within arm's reach, not a performance at the door. She honors arrival as a private moment; her presence says *I heard you coming* without demanding conversation before the guest is ready.

### 6. Motion

- Porch swing drifting on a slow arc
- Wind chimes answering a passing breeze
- Curtain or flag fabric lifting and settling against the house
- Leaves turning their pale undersides in the yard
- Moths or fireflies crossing the porch light at dusk
- Screen door settling after someone passes through

### 7. Audio

- Distant neighborhood hush — a dog, a far lawnmower, life at polite distance
- Wind through porch posts and screen mesh
- Rocking chair wood creaking on a slow rhythm
- Wind chimes — single notes, not a song
- Screen door spring and soft latch
- Birds in the oak canopy — cardinals, robins, the honest Midwest chorus

### 8. Iowa Seasons

**Spring:** Mud on the boot scraper, crocuses along the walk, porch boards dark with rain. Light is pale and hopeful; the mat says welcome before the yard has fully decided to green. **Summer:** Heat shimmer off white trim, cicadas beyond the oak shade, geraniums fierce red. Late light stays gold until nine; the swing invites staying outside one more minute. **Autumn:** Dried leaves collect in porch corners; a plaid throw appears on the rocker. Afternoon sun slants low and amber; the air smells like apples somewhere and woodsmoke maybe. **Winter:** Frost on the railings, boot prints in light snow on the steps. Porch light burns steady against early dark; the mat is brushed clean — welcome still means welcome when it's cold.

### 9. Time of Day

**Sunrise:** Pale gold on the eastern horizon, dew on the mat, birds louder than traffic. **Morning:** Clear Iowa light, shadows short, the day still choosing its pace. **Afternoon:** Heat on the boards, shade tucked under the eave, ice tea condensation on the side table. **Golden Hour:** Everything amber — white trim turns honey, fields glow, the porch becomes a photograph. **Evening:** Porch light on, crickets beginning, sky bruised purple then deep blue. **Night:** Moths at the bulb, stars over open land, the house glowing behind the screen door.

### 10. Hospitality

- **Low-energy day:** Quilt folded on the rocker, mat brushed clean, no extra asks — just *you can sit here as long as you need*
- **High-energy day:** Fresh flowers in the jar, swing ready, wind chimes bright — arrival matches their spark without rushing them inside
- **Birthday:** A ribbon on the door, balloons tied quiet to the rail — celebration at the threshold, not overwhelming
- **Recovery day:** Extra shade, slower swing, mat still says welcome even if they only make it to the first chair
- **Creative day:** Sketchbook left on the side table, porch as breathing room before making begins
- **Planning day:** A single index card on the table — *three things today* — gentle structure before the day opens
- **Celebration:** String lights along the rail, two glasses waiting, porch becomes the first toast
- **Vacation countdown:** A small chalk tally near the door, travel guide peeking from the basket — anticipation without frenzy
- **Rainy afternoon:** Quilt on the swing, mat absorbing footprints, rain drumming the porch roof — dry enough to think
- **Snow day:** Boot scraper cleared, hot drink station on the side table, porch light against white yard — warmth visible from the drive

### 11. Visual Language

Threshold architecture, honest wear, barn-red accents on white, open sky composition. Invitational not ornamental — every object says *stay* or *go gently* without clutter. Photography favors wide shots that show land and sky; the porch is a frame, not the whole painting.

### 12. UI Integration

**Hero ~35%** environmental header — porch vista and welcome mat visible above the fold. Soft-focus warm gradient behind arrival copy; the threshold image must never compete with legibility. Workspace below carries transition content — today's gentle entry, not a dashboard. Cards paper-cream with barn-red accent borders. Conversation sits lower third or sidebar chip — Shari greets without blocking the porch swing, the emotional center of the frame. House Map and Toolbelt remain stable; the porch is atmosphere around the crossing, not a navigation replacement.

## Living Room
<a id="living-room"></a>

### 1. Hero Concept

Shari's Living Room is the heart of the homestead — a generous Iowa farmhouse room where morning light pours through tall windows and finds every warm surface on purpose. Plaster walls in cream and soft wheat, barn-red throw pillows against a deep sofa that has shaped itself to years of conversation. A farmhouse coffee table bears the **spark mug** — ceramic, handmade glaze, the kind of cup that means *someone was thinking of you*. Books stack with honest disorder. A braided rug anchors the seating. White trim frames windows that look out over rolling land — oak branches shifting, fields beyond, sky that owns half the frame. A stone fireplace waits without demanding fire. Lamps with linen shades pool evening light. Textures everywhere: knit, worn leather, cotton, wood grain. Color lives here — not loud, but alive: terracotta, sage, cornflower blue, the red of the accents echoing the barn. This room smells like coffee, wood polish, and something baking two rooms away. It feels like *I've been expecting you* made architectural.

### 2. Emotional Promise

**When someone enters this room they should immediately feel...** welcomed — as if Shari set the mug down when she heard the screen door, turned, and smiled like they were already part of the day.

### 3. Restoration Promise

**When they leave they should feel...** *steadier* — less alone with whatever they carried in, grounded enough to face the next hour.

### 4. Signature Objects

- **Spark mug** on the coffee table — handmade ceramic, warm even when empty
- Deep sofa with layered throws — barn red, cream, sage
- Farmhouse coffee table — scarred wood, honest rings from real use
- Stone fireplace with a basket of birch logs (seasonal)
- Braided rug in faded earth tones
- Floor lamp with linen shade, golden pool
- Windows with gauze curtains that move when the oak moves
- A low shelf of books, photos in mismatched frames, one small succulent thriving

### 5. Companion Presence

**Level:** Host — **Why:** The Living Room is arrival made full — Shari is here, present, conversational warmth is the product. This is the one room where her voice may lead before the guest finds theirs, because being welcomed is the work.

### 6. Motion

- Sunlight drifting across the floor as hours pass
- Curtain gauze lifting in a window breeze
- Dust motes in a sunbeam — slow, unhurried
- Oak branches shifting outside the glass
- Steam rising from the spark mug when filled
- Fire flicker in winter — optional, never required

### 7. Audio

- Soft birds beyond the windows
- Distant wind in the oak canopy
- Optional wind chimes from the porch — faint
- Floorboard settle, house breathing
- Fire crackle when lit — winter hospitality
- The hush of a room that doesn't need to fill silence

### 8. Iowa Seasons

**Spring:** Pale green through the windows, mud boots by the door visible in the entry beyond, light bright and forgiving. Throws fold lighter; the room feels like opening windows after a long winter. **Summer:** Fan turning slow, curtains billowing, late gold lasting forever. Fields beyond are full height; the room stays cool in shade while outside hums. **Autumn:** Throws multiply, fire more likely, light slants amber across the rug. Outside maples carry color the room echoes in pillows and candles. **Winter:** Fire encouraged, lamps on by four, frost etching window corners. The room contracts gently around warmth — mug, blanket, lamplight, Shari's voice.

### 9. Time of Day

**Sunrise:** Pink-gray light on the rug, house quiet, mug waiting empty or full. **Morning:** Full window light, clearest room in the homestead, coffee warmth. **Afternoon:** Sun across the sofa, heat pooled, good for honest conversation. **Golden Hour:** The room becomes amber — photography's favorite hour, Shari's too. **Evening:** Lamps only, fire optional, voices soften. **Night:** Low lamplight, windows black with occasional porch glow, intimacy without isolation.

### 10. Hospitality

- **Low-energy day:** Blanket already folded on the sofa arm, mug ready, lights soft — no agenda in the room
- **High-energy day:** Windows bright, spark mug charged with something good, space feels open for big talk or big rest
- **Birthday:** Banner subtle not corporate, cake plate on the coffee table, photos turned face-out on the shelf
- **Recovery day:** Softer light, quieter sounds, blanket dominant — the room holds without asking for performance
- **Creative day:** Sketchbook on the table beside the mug, colors in the room turned up one notch
- **Planning day:** One clear surface on the coffee table — planner open but not screaming — structure with sofa comfort
- **Celebration:** Spark mug for a toast, fire lit if season allows, room arranged for faces not screens
- **Vacation countdown:** Travel book on the shelf, calendar glanceable, excitement warm not frantic
- **Rainy afternoon:** Curtains drawn partway, rain on glass, lamp on — cocoon with windows to the weather
- **Snow day:** Fire going, mugs stacked, world outside white — inside deliberately warm red and cream

### 11. Visual Language

Farmhouse warmth, lived-in luxury, color with restraint, window-led light. Nothing staged for a catalog — everything staged for a person. Keywords: welcoming, steady, handmade, oak-framed, honest clutter, spark.

### 12. UI Integration

**Hero ~95%** — full arrival immersion; the Living Room is the only room permitted near-full environment share. Greeting, invite, and conversation entry float over a soft warm gradient — never raw text on the photograph. Working layer is not dominant here; the room is the experience until the guest chooses to move. Cards minimal at arrival — paper-warm when they appear. Conversation lower third, Shari's voice primary. House Map and Toolbelt persist but defer visually; this is *I've been expecting you* in pixels. Reference: `full-arrival` immersion profile in Companion Layout System.

## Kitchen Table
<a id="kitchen-table"></a>

### 1. Hero Concept

The Kitchen Table is the homestead's morning altar — a solid wood farmhouse table, honey-toned and marked by real meals, centered in a kitchen where function wears beauty without trying. White cabinets, barn-red hand towels, open shelving with mismatched plates that somehow belong together. Morning light cuts across the table surface and catches the **tea set** — pot, cup, saucer, a small jar of honey with a wooden dipper. Steam is a visible character here. The window above the sink frames the same rolling Iowa land as every room, but closer — bird feeder, herb pots on the sill, basil and rosemary. Oak flooring, braided rug under the table legs, a bench on one side for sliding in without ceremony. Copper kettle on the stove. Fruit in a bowl — apples in autumn, berries in summer. The air smells like toast, tea, and possibility before urgency. This is where *one bite at a time* is not philosophy but architecture — the table is long enough for spreading out but round enough in feeling to face each other.

### 2. Emotional Promise

**When someone enters this room they should immediately feel...** nourished before they eat — as if the day agreed to start gently because the table was already set.

### 3. Restoration Promise

**When they leave they should feel...** *ready* — not pumped, not pressured — gently begun, stomach and spirit fed enough to take the first real step.

### 4. Signature Objects

- **Tea set** — ceramic pot, cup and saucer, steam visible in morning light
- Farmhouse table — honey wood, knife marks forgiven
- Window above sink with herb pots and land beyond
- Copper kettle on the stove
- Barn-red hand towels on oven handle
- Fruit bowl — seasonal, real, sometimes imperfect
- Bench on one side, chairs with worn rush seats on the other
- Open shelf with everyday dishes — no display-only china

### 5. Companion Presence

**Level:** Host — **Why:** Morning hospitality is Shari's natural register — she pours before asking, sets the tone for the day with warmth not agenda. The table is shared space; her presence says *sit, breathe, we'll figure out today together*.

### 6. Motion

- Steam rising from kettle and cup
- Sunlight sliding across the table surface
- Curtain at kitchen window lifting slightly
- Herb leaves trembling in sill draft
- Honey dipper turning slow in jar
- Birds at the feeder — quick hops, small shadows

### 7. Audio

- Kettle heating — soft metal tick, then pour
- Morning birds at the feeder and oak beyond
- Refrigerator hum — domestic baseline
- Spoon on ceramic, unhurried
- Distant wind, screen door somewhere in the house
- Rain on kitchen window — optional, cozy not gloomy

### 8. Iowa Seasons

**Spring:** Herb starts on the sill, mud boots drying by the back door, light sharp and clean. Rhubarb might appear in the fruit bowl. **Summer:** Berries, tomatoes from the garden, window wide, fan moving air. Fields fully green; breakfast before heat claims the day. **Autumn:** Apples, squash on the counter, golden slant through the window. The table invites longer sits as mornings cool. **Winter:** Frost on glass, kettle always on, bread rising somewhere. The table becomes the warmest geography in the house before the sun catches up.

### 9. Time of Day

**Sunrise:** Gray-blue kitchen, first lamp, kettle first sound. **Morning:** Peak room — steam, sun, tea, today's gentle planning surface. **Afternoon:** Crumbs from lunch, slanting light, table cleared for next purpose. **Golden Hour:** Amber on wood grain, long shadows, dinner prep beginning. **Evening:** Pendant light over table, dishes done, surface clear for tomorrow's setup. **Night:** Dark windows, single under-cabinet glow, table holding the quiet center of the house.

### 10. Hospitality

- **Low-energy day:** Tea already steeped, toast simple, table cleared except comfort — no spread, no demand
- **High-energy day:** Fruit bright, sun full, space on the table for ideas to land alongside breakfast
- **Birthday:** Candle in something edible, banner on the window not the wall, favorite mug forward
- **Recovery day:** Soft foods implied, kettle ready, bench cushion extra — sitting is the main task
- **Creative day:** Notebook beside the tea set, colored pens in a jar — morning pages welcome
- **Planning day:** Today's Reality surface — one corner of table for three priorities, rest stays domestic
- **Celebration:** Something special in the bowl, pot replaced with something festive — still the same table
- **Vacation countdown:** Calendar on fridge visible from table, countdown treats small and fun
- **Rainy afternoon:** Tea renewed, window streaked, lamp on — indoor morning extended
- **Snow day:** Hot chocolate station, bread warm, world outside paused — table is headquarters

### 11. Visual Language

Farmhouse kitchen honesty, steam as texture, morning gold, white-and-red palette anchored in wood. Keywords: nourishing, gentle start, handmade ceramic, herb-green accents, unhurried domestic.

### 12. UI Integration

**Hero ~30%** environmental header — table, tea set, window light above Today's Reality workspace. Soft gradient behind morning copy. Workspace dominant below — cream surface for daily gentle planning, not glass on photo. Cards paper-warm with sage or wheat borders. Conversation sidebar or lower chip — Shari hosts morning without blocking the tea steam, the emotional anchor. House Map and Toolbelt stable. Reference: `environmental-header` immersion, working layer dominant.

## Window Seat
<a id="window-seat"></a>

### 1. Hero Concept

The Window Seat is a built-in alcove where the house leans toward the weather — wide glass, deep cushion, and a **window blanket** folded with intention: wool, soft plaid, barn-red and cream, the kind you pull over your knees when thoughts get heavy. Books nest in the side cubby but don't demand reading. The seat faces rolling Iowa land through rain or sun — oak branches near enough to feel personal, fields distant enough to offer perspective. Cushions in washed linen, pillows that have learned your shape. A small shelf holds a candle unlit, a stone from the garden path, a cup forgotten and forgiven. Light here is side-lit, soft, rain-muted when weather cooperates with the room's purpose. The glass may carry droplets — each one a tiny lens on the world outside. This is not a nook for productivity. It is architecture for *you don't have to carry this alone* — a physical place where the house holds the back of someone sitting forward, curled, or still.

### 2. Emotional Promise

**When someone enters this room they should immediately feel...** held — as if the house built this alcove specifically for the weight they brought in.

### 3. Restoration Promise

**When they leave they should feel...** *lighter* — not fixed, not analyzed — less alone with hard thoughts, carrying them at a lower volume.

### 4. Signature Objects

- **Window blanket** — wool plaid, folded but reachable with one hand
- Deep built-in cushion — linen slipcover, impression-welcoming
- Wide window with oak branches framing the view
- Side cubby with books, not assigned homework
- Small shelf: candle, garden stone, empty cup allowed
- Rain on glass — seasonal character, not defect
- Gauze curtain tied back, able to close for cocoon
- Floor pillow optional — for when the seat isn't enough

### 5. Companion Presence

**Level:** Nearby — **Why:** Clear My Mind requires a quiet listener, not a host performing empathy. Shari is within reach — voice soft, questions rare — but the blanket and the rain do most of the holding. She speaks when spoken to; silence is hospitality here.

### 6. Motion

- Rain tracing paths on glass
- Curtain gauze drifting when window cracks
- Oak leaves brushing the exterior pane
- Blanket fringe settling when someone shifts
- Cloud shadow crossing the fields beyond
- Candle flame if lit — single tremble, not flicker chaos

### 7. Audio

- Rain on glass — primary instrument
- Distant wind in oak branches
- House hush — intentional quiet
- Occasional bird between rain bands
- Fabric shift on cushion — the sitter's soundtrack
- No music unless the guest brings it

### 8. Iowa Seasons

**Spring:** Rain frequent, buds on oak visible inches from the glass, blanket lighter weight. Hope and heaviness share the seat equally. **Summer:** Thunderstorms that turn the window into percussion, then green everywhere after. Blanket pushed aside; seat becomes perspective perch. **Autumn:** Rain cold and honest, maple color at eye level, blanket essential. Fields gold-brown; the view says *cycles are normal*. **Winter:** Ice on corners of glass, frost ferns, gray sky endless. Blanket thick, seat warm from house breath, outside world simplified to texture.

### 9. Time of Day

**Sunrise:** Gray-pink light, rain optional, seat cool until cushion warms. **Morning:** Side light, Clear My Mind capture — thoughts still arriving. **Afternoon:** Bright or dim depending on weather; rain makes the room. **Golden Hour:** Rare here unless west-facing catch — brief amber on blanket fold. **Evening:** Lamp in cubby, window dark mirror, inside world smaller and safer. **Night:** Black glass, reflection of blanket and self, house sounds distant.

### 10. Hospitality

- **Low-energy day:** Blanket unfolded on the cushion, light dim, rain sound up — zero questions waiting
- **High-energy day:** Window open a crack, cool air, blanket available not imposed — movement allowed before stillness
- **Birthday:** Small card on the shelf, blanket festive not silly — acknowledgment without spotlight
- **Recovery day:** Thickest blanket, rain loop if needed, tea on the shelf — duration honored over progress
- **Creative day:** Notebook in cubby, not centered — capture without forcing feeling into product
- **Planning day:** Not this room's job — if they land here, planner stays hidden, permission to not plan
- **Celebration:** Window open to fireworks or stars, blanket for sitting long — witness mode
- **Vacation countdown:** Travel stone swapped for souvenir from last trip — gentle nostalgia
- **Rainy afternoon:** Peak hospitality — blanket, rain, Shari quiet — the room fully itself
- **Snow day:** Frost frame on glass, blanket doubled, hot drink on shelf — outside muted, inside held

### 11. Visual Language

Soft alcove architecture, rain as texture, side light, plaid and linen, oak-framed view. Keywords: held, listening, weather-honest, cocoon, Clear My Mind, no fixing.

### 12. UI Integration

**Hero ~35%** — Window Seat header with blanket and rain-visible glass above capture workspace. Soft-focus gradient essential — emotional copy never on raw storm glass. Workspace dominant below: Clear My Mind continuous capture surface, cream/warm white, paper cards. Conversation minimal — lower third whisper chip, Shari as quiet listener. Cards rounded, soft shadow, wheat borders. House Map and Toolbelt never hide; environment supports capture, doesn't perform it. Reference: `environmental-header` immersion for Clear My Mind.

## Planning Table
<a id="planning-table"></a>

### 1. Hero Concept

The Planning Table occupies a sun-room corner or a bright dining alcove — a round or oval table that softens the authority of planning, honey wood with a **leather planner** open at center like an honest contract with the day. Morning light falls clear and unsentimental across the surface. Chairs are comfortable enough for real thinking — rush seats, cushions tied on with barn-red twine. A cup of pens, a single analog clock on the wall (no tick, or soft tick only). Windows on two sides if architecture allows — Iowa land visible but not distracting, sky offering scale when the list feels too large. A low credenza holds folders, not filing-cabinet dread. Color palette: cream paper, wheat walls, sage accents, leather brown. The room smells like paper, coffee from nearby kitchen, and possibility trimmed to manageable. This is Plan My Day made spatial — structure without corporate conference room, boundaries without walls.

### 2. Emotional Promise

**When someone enters this room they should immediately feel...** that today can be manageable — the room already believes their list is finite even if they don't yet.

### 3. Restoration Promise

**When they leave they should feel...** *clearer* — less overwhelmed by the day, holding three true priorities instead of forty anxious ghosts.

### 4. Signature Objects

- **Leather planner** — open, pen ready, pages that expect handwriting
- Round/oval honey wood table — no head of table, democratic planning
- Analog clock — gentle presence, not urgency
- Cup of pens — good ones, not promotional freebies
- Two-window corner with land and sky visible
- Low credenza with labeled folders — human labels, not taxonomy
- Single task lamp — brass, adjustable, warm bulb
- Water carafe and one glass — hydration without leaving the room

### 5. Companion Presence

**Level:** Nearby — **Why:** Plan My Day needs a gentle guide, not a driver. Shari sits conceptually across the table — available for *what if we moved this?* but not hovering over every line. Structure is offered; agency stays with the guest.

### 6. Motion

- Sunlight moving across the planner pages
- Curtain slow drift in corner windows
- Pen roll when someone reaches
- Page corner lift in draft from window crack
- Shadow of oak branch crossing the table — periodic, not chaotic
- Steam from water glass when morning cold

### 7. Audio

- Soft clock — optional tick at low volume
- House quiet — protected planning hush
- Pen on paper — the primary sound when working
- Distant birds through closed windows
- Page turn — satisfying, analog
- Kitchen sounds far — kettle reminder that breaks exist

### 8. Iowa Seasons

**Spring:** Light bright early, planner pages feel like seed packets — small things that grow. Mud season outside; inside clarity. **Summer:** Morning cool before heat, blinds half against glare. Fields visible reminder: some things ripen on schedule, some don't. **Autumn:** Golden slant, planner and leaves share color story. School-year energy without school-year stress. **Winter:** Lamp on sooner, planner under task light, outside gray — inside decisions feel possible.

### 9. Time of Day

**Sunrise:** Too early for this room — planner closed, table waiting. **Morning:** Peak Plan My Day — clear light, coffee nearby, list honest. **Afternoon:** Review hour — what shifted, what stays, gentler light. **Golden Hour:** Close-the-day planning optional — warm, reflective not frantic. **Evening:** Lamp only, tomorrow's three things maybe — soft setup not full session. **Night:** Planner closed, table clear — permission to stop planning.

### 10. Hospitality

- **Low-energy day:** Planner open to one page only, three-line limit visible, water ready — micro-planning only
- **High-energy day:** Full surface available, pens sharp, windows bright — capture the spark before it scatters
- **Birthday:** Planner page with celebration block protected — joy scheduled, not squeezed
- **Recovery day:** Planner shows rest items as valid entries — nap, walk, nothing — structure includes recovery
- **Creative day:** One corner for idea spillover — color pens, separate sheet — plan and play adjacent
- **Planning day:** Room fully itself — leather planner centered, Shari guide-present, clock visible
- **Celebration:** Planner records wins first — what went right before what's next
- **Vacation countdown:** Trip tasks in separate folder — excitement organized, not dominating daily plan
- **Rainy afternoon:** Lamp on, rain white noise, planning feels cocooned not confined
- **Snow day:** Flexible schedule implied — weather exceptions pre-written on today's page

### 11. Visual Language

## Future Wings

### 1. Hero Concept

**Future Wings** is the homestead's unfinished east wing — framed but not drywalled, studs and rafters honest against **forward dawn light** through plastic sheeting windows. The feeling is **not yet built** — possibility without pressure. A **blueprint scroll** unrolls across a plywood table; vision boards lean against studs; launch timelines written in chalk on a bare crossbeam. You stand in what will be, not what is — dust motes in early light, birds outside the sheeting, the rest of the house comfortable behind you. This room holds **vision and launches** — the business wing, the course studio, the product shelf not yet installed. **Subtle** presence: the environment whispers forward, never shouts. Iowa dawn paints the studs gold; you are building the building while building the work. Hope without hurry. Room to grow.
