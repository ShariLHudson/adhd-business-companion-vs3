# Part I — First Arrival

## Where it is

**Rolling Henry County, Iowa** — not a resort corridor, not a suburb. County gravel road on the **north** boundary. Neighbor corn and soy visible south and west. Oak-hickory grove on the homestead itself. **Fourteen acres** — homestead scale, not estate scale.

## How you approach

You slow on the gravel road because the tires ask you to. A **white split-rail gate** stands open or latched-never-locked. You walk or drive a **curving lane** through native grasses and old maples — the house is not visible all at once. That is intentional.

## What you see first

Maples. Then roofline — white painted wood, **barn-red** door. Then **the porch swing** — still. Then garden color left of the house, **greenhouse glass** catching light right, **barn** silhouette west, a **horse** possibly at the far fence. South of everything: **fields to the horizon**.

## What you hear

Gravel underfoot. Wind in oak leaves. **Wind chimes** — one note, not a song. A cardinal if morning. Distant — a tractor maybe, miles away. Kinsey's nails on porch boards, then silence.

## What you smell

Cut grass. Soil after rain if recent. Something baking or coffee if Shari is home. Herb wall near the kitchen — thyme when you pass west.

## First emotional feeling

**Exhale.** Not excitement — relief. The world stops demanding. The property says: *You made it. You can land here.*

## Welcome before the door

The **Front Porch** is the first room — not the foyer. The swing. The welcome mat. The rocking chair angled toward you. Shari may appear — photograph-still, voice-warm — but the porch has already done the work. **Hospitality begins outdoors in Iowa.**

# Part IV — The Living Landscape

## Native Iowa trees

Bur oak · white oak · black walnut · sugar maple · red cedar at fence lines · river birch near pond

## Prairie & wildflowers

Little bluestem · coneflower · black-eyed Susan · butterfly weed · goldenrod (controlled) · sunflower at barn corners · wildflower meadow south fence — **never mowed mid-summer**

## Cultivated gardens

**Peonies** near porch · **hydrangea** north shade · **tulips** spring walk edge · **vegetable beds** south garden · **container gardens** on deck steps

## Hardscape

Crushed limestone paths (garden) · gravel service route (barn) · **stone bench** at pond · flagstone **fire circle** · no concrete patio — **wood deck** only

## Water

**Pond** — one-third acre feel, native edge, no fountain — dragonflies preferred · **Rain chains** at greenhouse corner — visible, heard

## Wildlife habitats

Brush pile northwest (rabbits) · owl box in barn peak · bat house · hummingbird garden east · coneflower for goldfinch · feeder stations three (porch, garden, meadow)

## Wind & light outdoors

Ornamental grasses move west pasture edge · wind chimes porch only · **outdoor lighting**: porch, deck stair, path to fire — **warm 2700K, never security flood**

## Views

Every major outdoor seat has **either** garden intimacy **or** field horizon — never neighbor garage dominant

# Part IX — ADHD Environmental Design

Every decision passes the **ADHD Filter**:

| Question | Fail signal |
|----------|-------------|
| Reduces overwhelm? | Visual noise, too many heroes |
| Reduces cognitive load? | Competing focal points |
| Supports executive function? | No clear next physical step |
| Reduces decision fatigue? | Too many equal choices |
| Provides orientation? | Guest doesn't know where they are in home |
| Creates calm? | Alert colors, urgency copy |
| Invites creativity? | Sterile perfection |
| Rewards arrival? | Feels like login, not welcome |
| Leaves guest better? | Productivity shame |

**Environmental psychology rules:**
- **Threshold before task** — porch, kitchen, then plan
- **Body before brain** — garden path before afternoon call
- **One surface ready** — planner open, not blank page terror
- **Escape valves** — window seat always available if plan floods
- **Closure built-in** — deck sunset ends day without app reminder
