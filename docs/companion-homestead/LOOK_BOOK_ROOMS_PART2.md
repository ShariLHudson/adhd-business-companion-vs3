## Library™
<a id="library"></a>

### 1. Hero Concept

Floor-to-ceiling oak shelves climb the north wall of Shari's library — not a showpiece collection, but a working archive of everything she has learned, saved, and intends to return to. A rolling **book ladder** rests on brass rails, its worn treads proof that knowledge here is meant to be reached, not admired behind glass. A **study lamp** with a green glass shade throws a disciplined pool of light across a broad walnut desk; everything outside that circle falls into respectful shadow. Leather armchairs sit at angles that invite reading without demanding performance. The windows face east across Iowa rolling land — corn stubble or green shoots depending on season — and afternoon light slants through half-drawn linen curtains, catching dust motes like slow constellations. Built-in cabinets hold labeled boxes, course binders, saved articles, and project references; the room smells of paper, cedar, and the faintest trace of coffee from a cup left on a coaster. **Library hush** lives here — not silence imposed, but the quiet that arrives when thoughts have a permanent address. Knowledge feels **organized**, findable, honored.

### 2. Emotional Promise™

**When someone enters this room they should immediately feel** that what they have learned and saved will not scatter — it has a home, and they are welcome to sit down and use it.

### 3. Restoration Promise™

**When they leave they should feel** **more organized** — their reference world sorted, accessible, and no longer pressing on the edges of memory.

### 4. Signature Objects™

- **Book ladder** — brass rails, oak treads, always within reach of the upper shelves
- **Study lamp** — green glass shade, warm focused pool on the desk
- Walnut writing desk with ink blotter and a single open notebook
- Floor-to-ceiling oak shelves with mixed spines — business, craft, fiction, field guides
- Leather armchair with a folded wool throw
- Cedar document boxes with handwritten labels
- East-facing window with linen curtains and a view of Iowa fields
- Ceramic mug on a coaster — evidence of a long session, not clutter

### 5. Companion Presence™

**Level:** Prepared — **Why:** Shari has arranged the shelves, left the lamp on, and chosen what sits on the desk before the guest arrives. She is not hovering over every search; the room itself communicates that learning is safe, stored, and respected.

### 6. Motion™

- Dust motes drifting through afternoon sunbeams
- Curtain hem lifting on a draft from the hall
- Page corner lifting slightly when a book lies open
- Shadow line creeping across spines as the sun moves
- The faintest sway of tree branches visible through the window

### 7. Audio™

- **Library hush** — muffled house sounds, no television bleed
- Soft creak of floorboards when someone shifts in the chair
- Page turn when a book is opened
- Distant wind against window glass
- Occasional bird call from the yard oak — far away, not intrusive

### 8. Iowa Seasons™

**Spring:** Mud boots by the door, a windowsill pot of seedlings visible through the glass, shelves holding seed catalogs and garden planning binders; light is pale and eager. **Summer:** Heavy green outside the east window, fan stirring the curtain hem, iced tea ring on the desk; afternoon heat held at bay by thick walls and drawn shades on the south side. **Autumn:** Maple gold through the glass, a basket of apples from the yard on the side table, wool throw unfolded on the chair; lamplight comes earlier. **Winter:** Frost feathering the panes, fire glow from the hall bleeding warm under the door, desk lamp the heart of the room; snow quiets the fields to white silence.

### 9. Time of Day™

**Sunrise:** Pale gold on the east shelves, long shadows, the room still cool. **Morning:** Clear working light on the desk, best hours for reference and search. **Afternoon:** Sunbeams and dust, the ladder's brass catching fire. **Golden Hour:** Warm amber on leather and wood, fields turning copper outside. **Evening:** Lamp pool dominant, window a blue rectangle of dusk. **Night:** Desk lamp and nothing else — deep focus, house asleep.

### 10. Hospitality™

- **Low-energy day:** Lamp already on, one book open to the right page, chair turned toward the light
- **High-energy day:** Fresh notebook on the desk, index tabs visible, nothing overwhelming on display
- **Birthday:** A wrapped book on the armchair, ribbon in homestead barn red
- **Recovery day:** Soft throw, rain optional on the window, kettle waiting in the kitchen
- **Creative day:** Sketchbooks pulled to the front shelf, reference images in an open folio
- **Planning day:** Leather planner beside the lamp, calendar within arm's reach
- **Celebration:** A small stack of "finished" binders with satisfaction, not ceremony
- **Vacation countdown:** Travel guides on a side table, library still calm — not frantic
- **Rainy afternoon:** Lamp bright, window streaked, perfect indoor learning weather
- **Snow day:** Frost patterns, extra blanket, cocoa implied from the kitchen

### 11. Visual Language™

Scholarly warmth without academia — oak, walnut, brass, leather, linen. Colors stay in the family of cream, forest green, burgundy, and honey. Nothing corporate, nothing sterile; this is a homestead library that works for a builder's life.

### 12. UI Integration™

**Hero:** 25% — warm workspace header; shelves and lamp visible, desk surface implied below. **Overlay:** Cream-to-warm gradient softening the upper shelf line so titles remain legible. **Workspace:** Dominant — reference cards, search results, and saved work on a warm white surface like the desk below the lamp. **Cards:** Paper-like, spine-colored left borders, subtle shadow. **Conversation:** Lower third chip or sidebar — quiet listener cadence, never covering the ladder or lamp.

## Back Deck™
<a id="back-deck"></a>

### 1. Hero Concept

The back deck runs the width of the house — wide boards, weathered gray with barn-red railing accents and white post caps. A **rocking chair** sits at the corner where you can see both the yard and the long Iowa sunset; its runners have polished the deck boards beneath them into a pale path. Potted herbs line the rail: basil, rosemary, mint releasing scent when the evening warms. String lights hang in a soft arc overhead, unlit until dusk. Below, the yard rolls away to garden beds and the distant tree line; fireflies rise from the grass as twilight deepens. **Crickets** begin their chorus; the day's heat lifts from the boards. This is where the homestead exhales — not the front-facing welcome of the porch, but the private release at day's end. A small side table holds a glass with condensation; a blanket is folded over the chair arm for when the air cools. The feeling is **evening reflection**: nothing left to prove, only the slow rock and the sky changing.

### 2. Emotional Promise™

**When someone enters this room they should immediately feel** that the day may end without being finished — they have permission to stop, rock, and let the evening arrive.

### 3. Restoration Promise™

**When they leave they should feel** **lighter** — released from the day's grip, body unclenched, mind allowed to wander.

### 4. Signature Objects™

- **Rocking chair** — painted wood or wicker, runners worn smooth
- **Side table** — small, deck-height, ringed from many glasses
- Barn-red railing with white post caps
- Potted herbs on the rail
- String lights overhead (off by day, warm at night)
- Folded blanket on the chair arm
- Wind chime at the corner — single, gentle
- View over garden beds to Iowa tree line

### 5. Companion Presence™

**Level:** Nearby — **Why:** Shari may settle into the second chair or lean on the rail without taking over the silence. She is present for end-of-day conversation when wanted, and comfortable saying nothing while the crickets speak.

### 6. Motion™

- Rocking chair slow arc — rhythmic, unhurried
- **Fireflies** rising and falling over the yard
- Curtain drifting through the screen door behind
- String lights swaying slightly on a breeze
- Cloud color shifting across the horizon
- Herb leaves trembling in the cooling air

### 7. Audio™

- **Crickets** — the primary evening chorus
- **Fireflies** implied in the hush between cricket phrases
- Rocking chair faint creak on the boards
- Distant wind in the maples
- Single wind chime — one note, not a song
- Screen door soft slap if someone steps out
- Far dog bark, once, across the fields

### 8. Iowa Seasons™

**Spring:** Cool evenings, blanket essential, peepers mixing with crickets, yard mud drying at the edges. **Summer:** Board warmth underfoot, fireflies thick as stars, humidity lifting at last, mint and basil sharp in the air. **Autumn:** Earlier sunset, golden light on the railing, leaves skittering across the boards, cocoa season begins. **Winter:** Deck chairs stored; this room may show snow on the rail, frost on the glass door — invitation to look, not sit, unless a brave blanket moment.

### 9. Time of Day™

**Sunrise:** Rare here — quiet, dew on the rail, birds not yet loud. **Morning:** Bright across the yard, herbs vivid, not the room's primary hour. **Afternoon:** Shade under the roof overhang, heat held off. **Golden Hour:** The room's true name — rocking, sky on fire. **Evening:** Crickets, fireflies, string lights considered. **Night:** Stars over Iowa dark, chair rocking, house glow behind the screen door.

### 10. Hospitality™

- **Low-energy day:** Blanket ready, chair already angled to the sunset, no questions waiting
- **High-energy day:** Two glasses on the table, invitation to talk the day out
- **Birthday:** String lights on, something fizzy on the table
- **Recovery day:** Extra quiet, no agenda, chair and sky sufficient
- **Creative day:** Notebook on the side table — optional, not required
- **Planning day:** Tomorrow can wait; tonight is for rocking
- **Celebration:** Lights on, a small toast, wins named gently
- **Vacation countdown:** Suitcase visible through the screen door — adventure tomorrow, rest tonight
- **Rainy afternoon:** Covered deck dry, rain on the roof, gray-green yard
- **Snow day:** Hot drink, wrapped in blanket, white yard stillness

### 11. Visual Language™

Weathered wood, barn red, white trim, soft evening gold. Outdoor-living Midwest — not coastal deck luxury, not urban rooftop. Intimate scale, honest materials, horizon wide.

### 12. UI Integration™

**Hero:** 35% — environmental header; railing, chair, and sky band across the top. **Overlay:** Warm dusk gradient protecting copy from busy sunset photography. **Workspace:** Reflection prompts, journal capture, end-of-day review on cream surface below. **Cards:** Soft edges, evening-warm borders. **Conversation:** Lower third — reflective friend cadence, sidebar preferred so the horizon stays visible.

## Outlook Point™
<a id="outlook-point"></a>

### 1. Hero Concept

Past the garden, the land rises to a low knoll where Shari placed a **bench at the edge** — not a cliff, but the place where the yard ends and the Iowa horizon opens. The bench is simple cedar, facing west; worn smooth by seasons of sitting. Grass bends in **wide wind**; **clouds drift** slow enough to watch them change shape. From here you see fields, tree lines, a distant barn silhouette, and sky that owns more of the view than any human structure. Wildflowers at the bench feet — not cultivated beds, but what grows where the mower stops. The feeling is **horizon perspective**: problems shrink to scale, the week becomes a story instead of a trap, and the future looks like land you can walk into. No walls, no ceiling — only the honest Midwest sky and the **perspective restored** that distance grants.

### 2. Emotional Promise™

**When someone enters this room they should immediately feel** that they can see further than today's urgency — the horizon is real, and they are part of something larger than the inbox.

### 3. Restoration Promise™

**When they leave they should feel** **clearer** — perspective restored, priorities reordered by distance and sky.

### 4. Signature Objects™

- **Bench at the edge** — cedar, facing the horizon, no back to the wind
- Worn path through grass leading up from the garden
- Wildflowers and native grasses at the bench feet
- Distant barn silhouette and field lines
- Single oak at the knoll's side — shelter without enclosure
- Optional: Shari's field journal left on the bench slat
- Wide sky — dominant visual element
- Hawk or turkey vulture distant, circling

### 5. Companion Presence™

**Level:** Prepared — **Why:** Shari has walked this path before and knows when someone needs the bench more than company. She may have left the journal, or she may join silently and sit at the other end — but the place itself does the widening work.

### 6. Motion™

- **Cloud drift** — slow, monumental, calming
- **Wide wind** in grass — waves across the slope
- Tree leaves on the knoll oak — heavier motion than the grass
- Hawk circle, distant and patient
- Shadow of cloud crossing the fields below
- Tall stems bending and recovering

### 7. Audio™

- **Wide wind** — open, not howling, constant
- Grass rustle, layered and soft
- Distant birds — red-winged blackbird, meadowlark
- Bench wood faint creak when weight shifts
- Silence between wind gusts — enormous
- Far combine or tractor — optional, seasonal Iowa truth

### 8. Iowa Seasons™

**Spring:** Green rolling forward, mud on the path, sky big with rain clouds and hope. **Summer:** Heat shimmer on distant fields, crickets from the valley, bench hot until evening. **Autumn:** Gold and bronze to the horizon, best season for this bench — harvest visible, air clean. **Winter:** Bench snow-dusted or cleared, fields pale, sky enormous and gray-blue — stark perspective, few visitors, honest.

### 9. Time of Day™

**Sunrise:** East light behind the sitter, fields waking — rare, worth it. **Morning:** Clear air, long sight lines, planning perspective. **Afternoon:** Heat, shade under the oak preferred. **Golden Hour:** Bench at its best — whole sky performance. **Evening:** Wind cooling, crickets rising from below. **Night:** Stars unobstructed, bench a dark shape, vast quiet.

### 10. Hospitality™

- **Low-energy day:** Path mowed, bench dry, nothing required but sitting
- **High-energy day:** Journal on the bench, invitation to think big
- **Birthday:** Wildflowers picked, laid on the bench — no fuss
- **Recovery day:** Blanket folded on the bench, wind not too strong
- **Creative day:** Sketchpad, horizon as prompt
- **Planning day:** Best use — quarterly vision, life map, "what matters" questions
- **Celebration:** Watch the sunset, name one win to the sky
- **Vacation countdown:** Look outward — adventure is part of a life, not an escape from one
- **Rainy afternoon:** Not this room — or brief walk to see storm front pass
- **Snow day:** Crisp air, tracks on the path, white fields to the tree line

### 11. Visual Language™

Horizon-forward, minimal objects, maximum sky. Cedar, grass green, field gold, cloud white. No clutter — the bench is the only furniture. Emotional keyword: **vast**.

### 12. UI Integration™

**Hero:** 40% — environmental header; sky and horizon band dominant. **Overlay:** Soft blue-gold gradient so vision prompts and copy float above the fields. **Workspace:** Strategy cards, vision exercises, long-view planning below — never blocking the horizon in the hero. **Cards:** Wide aspect hints, horizon-line dividers. **Conversation:** Sidebar chip — reflective friend, short lines; the view speaks first.

## Garden™
<a id="garden"></a>

### 1. Hero Concept

Shari's garden is not a magazine spread — it is Iowa ground made intentional. Raised beds of cedar bleed into **wildflower** margins where coneflowers, black-eyed Susans, and milkweed hold space for **birds and bees**. Paths of packed gravel curve between beds; tomato cages lean with honest use. A wooden gate opens from the yard, and beyond it the garden hums with life — not loud, but alive. Grounding happens here through contact with soil, season, and creature: a robin on the fence post, a bee working a blossom, your boots on real earth. The **homestead garden** feeds people and pollinators; it does not perform perfection. Colors are saturated — green, gold, purple, tomato red — against the soft gray of Iowa sky. You leave your screen voice at the gate; the garden speaks in slower time.

### 2. Emotional Promise™

**When someone enters this room they should immediately feel** rooted in something alive and cyclical — they belong to a season, not only a schedule.

### 3. Restoration Promise™

**When they leave they should feel** **calmer** — grounded, body remembered, attention widened to what is growing.

### 4. Signature Objects™

- **Wildflowers** — coneflower, black-eyed Susan, milkweed at the margins
- Cedar raised beds with rich soil
- Gravel paths between beds
- Tomato cages, bean poles, practical structure
- Wooden gate from the yard
- Bird bath — terra-cotta, water low
- Weathered bench at the garden's quiet end
- Hand tools in a bucket by the gate — trowel, gloves
- Bees and butterflies — motion, not decoration

### 5. Companion Presence™

**Level:** Prepared — **Why:** Shari has been in the garden; the gate is unlatched, the path clear. She may kneel in a bed nearby or point out what bloomed since last week — companion as fellow gardener, not instructor.

### 6. Motion™

- **Bees** working blossoms — purposeful, small
- **Birds** — goldfinch on seed head, robin on the fence
- Butterflies — monarch when milkweed cooperates
- Leaves lifting in breeze across the beds
- Tomato leaves trembling on the cage
- Cloud shadow crossing the beds — slow cooling

### 7. Audio™

- **Birds** — chickadee, robin, goldfinch chatter
- **Bees** — low hum in the wildflower margin
- Gravel crunch underfoot on the path
- Wind through bean poles and tall stems
- Distant lawnmower — neighbor, far off
- Water trickle if the bird bath is full

### 8. Iowa Seasons™

**Spring:** Mud and hope, first peas, cold frames, catalogs fulfilled in soil. **Summer:** Peak abundance, heat at noon, harvest basket weather, insects full chorus. **Autumn:** Frost warning, final tomatoes, seed saving, goldenrod at the wild edge. **Winter:** Beds mulched, structure visible, snow on the cedar frames — garden sleeping, plan dreaming.

### 9. Time of Day™

**Sunrise:** Dew on leaves, birds loud, best for quiet walking. **Morning:** Cool work light, harvesting herbs. **Afternoon:** Shade sought, colors vivid. **Golden Hour:** Wildflowers backlit, magic hour for photos and breath. **Evening:** Crickets from the beds, fireflies at the margin. **Night:** Moon on the paths, rare visit, peaceful.

### 10. Hospitality™

- **Low-energy day:** Bench ready, no tools required — looking is enough
- **High-energy day:** Bucket of weeds already started — join if you want
- **Birthday:** Bouquet tied with twine, from the garden not a florist

- **Celebration:** Harvest shared — tomatoes, flowers, something real
- **Vacation countdown:** Garden tended before leave — trust the season
- **Rainy afternoon:** Rain on leaves, greenhouse visible in the distance
- **Snow day:** Seed catalogs inside, beds under white — planning season

### 11. Visual Language™

Living color — green dominant, flower accents, cedar brown, sky gray-blue. Organic edges, no rigid geometry. Homestead productive, not estate formal. **Grounded** is the keyword.

### 12. UI Integration™

**Hero:** 40% — outdoor header; beds, wildflowers, sky. **Overlay:** Soft green-gold gradient for legibility. **Workspace:** Grounding exercises, seasonal notes, gentle prompts on warm cream below. **Cards:** Rounded, petal-soft borders, earth-tone tints. **Conversation:** Lower third — thoughtful companion, unhurried cadence.

## Garden Path™
<a id="garden-path"></a>

### 1. Hero Concept

The **garden path** is not the destination — it is the room between. Packed gravel and stepping stones wind from the back door through shade and sun, past the vegetable beds, along the **wildflower** edge, toward the greenhouse and the knoll beyond. **Dappled light** falls through apple and maple branches; the pace here is **unhurried** by design. A **garden basket** sits on a stump at the path's bend — sometimes empty, sometimes holding herbs or clipped blooms, an invitation to gather without goal. Ferns and hostas hold the shady sections cool; sunnier stretches warm your shoulders. The path is wide enough for two but pleasant alone; your thoughts walk with you, sorting themselves in the rhythm of steps. Iowa humidity, bird call, the crunch of gravel — nothing demands arrival. **Walking thoughts** are the product here.

### 2. Emotional Promise™

**When someone enters this room they should immediately feel** that they may take the long way — movement without destination is allowed, even encouraged.

### 3. Restoration Promise™

**When they leave they should feel** **calmer** — thoughts less tangled, body having moved, mind sorted by walking.

### 4. Signature Objects™

- **Garden basket** — wicker, on a stump at the path bend
- Stepping stones through the shady section
- Packed gravel main path — honest crunch
- Apple or maple arch — dappled shade
- Wildflower margin along one side
- Cedar bench halfway — optional pause, not required
- Hand-painted stone marker — whimsical, Shari's touch
- View through to greenhouse glass ahead

### 5. Companion Presence™

**Level:** Prepared — **Why:** Shari may walk ahead or fall behind, matching pace without filling silence. The basket is her signal — gathering is possible, not mandatory. She knows this path is for sorting, not solving.

### 6. Motion™

- **Dappled light** shifting as branches move
- Leaves drifting onto the path
- Butterfly crossing ahead — slow chase optional
- Basket handle stirring in the breeze

### 7. Audio™

- Birds in the canopy — layered, not loud
- Wind in leaves — the dappled sound

### 9. Time of Day™

**Sunrise:** Dapple just beginning, birds active, cool air. **Morning:** Best walking hour — light angled, garden waking. **Afternoon:** Shade welcome, slow loop. **Golden Hour:** Path lit like a corridor to the greenhouse. **Evening:** Fireflies at the wild edge, crickets starting. **Night:** Path by porch light only — rare, intimate.

### 11. Visual Language™

Path-forward, tunnel of green, light and shadow play. Wicker, stone, gravel, leaf gold. Motion implied in every frame — this room is never still. Keyword: **unhurried**.

### 12. UI Integration™

## Greenhouse™
<a id="greenhouse"></a>

### 1. Hero Concept

The **greenhouse** stands at the garden's sunny end — aluminum frame, **diffused glass** panels, a door that sighs when you open it. Inside, the world is warmer, greener, and softer than Iowa weather outside. **Condensation** beads on the glass and trails slowly down; **patient hope** lives on the potting bench in a **seedling tray** — tiny stems with their first true leaves, labels in Shari's hand. Shelves hold starts for tomatoes, peppers, herbs; tools hang on pegboard; a watering can waits. Light is even, shadowless, forgiving — **diffused glass** turns harsh noon into nurture. The air smells of soil, humidity, and growth. This is where long projects begin too small to see; where **slow growth** is honored, not rushed. You do not harvest here yet — you tend. The feeling is greenhouse quiet: humid hush, water drip, the almost-silent push of cells dividing.

### 2. Emotional Promise™

**When someone enters this room they should immediately feel** that slow growth is legitimate — what is small now is allowed to take its time.

### 3. Restoration Promise™

**When they leave they should feel** **more hopeful** — patient hope for what is growing, faith in processes longer than a week.

### 4. Signature Objects™

- **Seedling tray** — labeled cells, first true leaves
- Potting bench with soil bin and hand tools
- **Diffused glass** walls — light without burn
- **Condensation** on panels — trails, beads
- Watering can — copper or galvanized
- Pegboard with trowels, pruners, twine
- Shelves of starts in various stages
- Thermometer on the wall — gentle monitoring
- Stool for long tending sessions

### 5. Companion Presence™

**Level:** Prepared — **Why:** Shari has watered this morning; the seedlings are labeled. She may point to a tray and say "these take six weeks" — teaching patience by example, not lecture. Companion as fellow tender.

### 6. Motion™

- **Condensation** trailing on glass — slow paths downward
- Seedling leaves lifting almost imperceptibly — growth in real time
- Water drip from can to soil — single plop
- Diffused light shift as clouds pass outside
- Fan faint stir — air circulation, leaf tremor
- Shadow of a bird crossing the roof glass

### 7. Audio™

- Humid quiet — muffled outside wind
- Water **drip** from can or eave
- Fan low hum — circulation
- Glass faint creak in temperature shift
- Soil sound when fingers press a pot
- Distant garden birds — filtered, soft

### 8. Iowa Seasons™

**Spring:** Greenhouse full — trays crowded, frost still outside, this room essential. **Summer:** Vent open, shade cloth partial, starts hardened off and moved out. **Autumn:** Second wave of cool crops, cleanup, shelves thinning. **Winter:** Greenhouse as refuge — cold outside, green inside, planning and seed-starting for next year.

### 9. Time of Day™

**Sunrise:** Light building, condensation fresh, cool inside. **Morning:** Best tending hour — water, check labels. **Afternoon:** Warmest inside, vent may open. **Golden Hour:** Glass glows, seedlings backlit. **Evening:** Cooling, door closed, humidity held. **Night:** Dark glass, occasional night light for sensitive starts.

### 10. Hospitality™

- **Low-energy day:** Stool ready, one tray to look at — no work required
- **High-energy day:** Repotting station set up, soil bin full
- **Birthday:** New seeds as gift — variety chosen for joy
- **Recovery day:** Quiet tending — water one tray, breathe
- **Creative day:** Label making, garden journal at the bench
- **Planning day:** Long-project mapping beside the seedlings — parallel growth
- **Celebration:** First transplant — small ceremony, soil on hands
- **Vacation countdown:** Autowater setup implied, trust the system
- **Rainy afternoon:** Perfect greenhouse weather — outside wet, inside green
- **Snow day:** Maximum contrast — white outside, living green inside

### 11. Visual Language™

Diffused light, humid green, aluminum gleam, soil brown. Soft focus at the edges — glass diffusion as natural filter. Keyword: **patient hope**.

### 12. UI Integration™

**Hero:** 35% — glass glow, seedling tray visible on the bench line. **Overlay:** Soft white-green gradient, condensation texture optional subtle. **Workspace:** Long-horizon project cards, growth tracking, nurture metaphors on cream below. **Cards:** Sprout-green accents, growth-stage indicators. **Conversation:** Lower third — thoughtful companion, slow cadence, short lines.

## Barn™
<a id="barn"></a>

### 1. Hero Concept

The **barn** sits beyond the garden — classic Iowa red with white trim, board-and-batten siding, a sliding door that opens on honest hardware. Inside, **practical light through slats** stripes the floor: gold bars across worn concrete and packed earth. This is **big projects storage** — the place where long-horizon work lives without crowding the kitchen table. A **project board** covers one wall: index cards, timelines, lumber sketches, labeled bins. Shelves hold materials for builds not yet started; rafters show beams strong enough to trust. It smells of wood shavings, hay memory, and metal tool oil. The barn is not polished — it is capable. Work boots by the door, extension cords coiled right, a radio silent unless someone turns it on. **Big work can live here** — renovations, business launches in build phase, seasonal equipment, the projects that need square footage and patience.

### 2. Emotional Promise™

**When someone enters this room they should immediately feel** that their largest work has a dedicated home — it will not spill into every corner of life.

### 3. Restoration Promise™

**When they leave they should feel** **more confident** — big projects contained, next physical step visible, overwhelm partitioned into the barn.

### 4. Signature Objects™

- **Project board** — cork or plywood, cards, timelines, sketches
- Sliding barn door — red, white X trim optional
- **Light through slats** — defining visual rhythm
- Workbench with vise and tool rack
- Labeled bins on shelves — hardware sorted
- Lumber rack along one wall
- Extension cords, coiled correctly
- Work boots on a mat by the door
- Rafter beams — visible structure, trust

### 5. Companion Presence™

**Level:** Prepared — **Why:** Shari has updated the project board and cleared a path to the bench. She is not micromanaging the build — she is ensuring the barn is ready when someone is ready to work. Ambient competence.

### 6. Motion™

- **Light through slats** shifting as the sun moves — bar shadows crawling
- Dust motes in the light bars
- Hanging tool slight sway from door draft
- Project board card corner lifting in breeze from vent
- Spider web glint — rare, honest barn truth
- Swallow flight through high vent — summer

### 7. Audio™
