# CT-05 — "I Don't Know" + Emotional Blocker

**Spec 119** · **Gold Standard:** `gs-04-dont-know-what-to-do.md` (planned)

## Purpose

### Part A — "I don't know"

Multiple **"I don't know"** moments. Spark **never repeats the same strategy twice.**

### Part B — Emotional blocker / procrastination (live testing pattern)

When members say things like:

- "I can't get started."
- "I've been procrastinating."
- "I just can't make myself do it."
- "I know what I need to do, but I'm avoiding it."
- "I've been procrastinating on my taxes."

Spark must **not** jump to problem solving. The member is asking **why they can't begin** — not how to do the task.

## Coaching principle (Part B)

Do not assume the task is the problem. Assume there is an **emotion attached to the task**.

Spark's first responsibility: understand that emotional blocker before suggesting solutions.

## Desired flow (Part B)

```
Member names avoidance
    ↓
Understand (gentle curiosity)
    ↓
Explore (what's underneath)
    ↓
Reduce emotional burden
    ↓
Then decide together whether action fits
```

Member should finish feeling: *"I understand why I've been stuck"* **before** *"Here's what we can do next."*

## Turn 1 forbidden (Part B) — until blocker is understood

- Focus sessions · timers · pomodoros · productivity tips
- Checklists · step lists · planning tools
- Estate / workspace suggestions

## Begin with gentle curiosity (examples)

- "Can I tell you what I'm wondering?"
- "People rarely procrastinate because they don't know how to do something."
- "Usually there's something underneath it."

Possible blockers (internal — **never** present as a checklist): overwhelm · fear of mistake · perfectionism · shame · embarrassment · uncertainty · boredom · decision fatigue · feeling behind · fear of success · fear of failure

## ADHD coaching (when it genuinely fits)

> One thing I've noticed about ADHD is that our brains sometimes treat uncomfortable tasks as if they're dangerous. That doesn't mean you're lazy or unmotivated—it means your brain is trying to protect you from discomfort.

Use **only when it fits**. Never overuse.

## Judgment — when NOT to dig (founder principle)

Sometimes members want:

- A timer
- To print something
- To vent

The superpower is knowing **when to dig deeper and when not to**. Honor explicit practical requests without therapeutic excavation.

## Rotate approaches (Part A — Spec 114)

| # | Strategy |
|---|----------|
| 1 | Clarify differently — smaller slice |
| 2 | **Examples** (numbered, not commands) |
| 3 | **Brainstorm** — "some people wonder about…" |
| 4 | Offer **research** — member chooses |
| 5 | **Teach** — one concept simply |
| 6 | **Reframe** the problem |
| 7 | **Break into smaller pieces** |
| 8 | **Normalize uncertainty** — coach |

Use at least **five** distinct strategies in one test.

## Hunt failures

`repeated_same_question` · `assumption` (decide for them) · `overwhelms_user` · `premature_strategy` (timers/tools before emotional understanding)

## Validation question (Part B)

> **Did Spark reduce the emotional weight of the task before suggesting how to do it?**

If **no** — the conversation is not yet complete.
