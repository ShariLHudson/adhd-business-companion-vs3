# Reading Nook™ — Room Look Book

**Place ID:** `reading-nook`  
**Status:** Design authority — Phase 1 (no UI implementation)  
**Registry:** [`lib/companionObjects/companionObjectRegistry.ts`](../../lib/companionObjects/companionObjectRegistry.ts)

---

## Purpose

The Reading Nook™ is a **small room on purpose** — intimate, evening-native, built for long-form thinking, saved wisdom, and strategies that do not feel clinical.

It exists for **Reading Nook™**, **Strategies**, and quiet reference — learning that feels like a corner you chose, not a classroom you were assigned.

**Emotional need served:** "Nothing important gets lost — and I can think without being watched."

**ADHD challenge reduced:** shame about needing coping tools, fear that saved knowledge disappears, distraction from open-plan noise.

Hallway landing or transformed deep closet: armchair, floor-to-ceiling personal-scale shelves, brass reading lamp — **and** the reef.

---

## Emotional Promise™

**When someone leaves this room…** they feel **more understood** — with one strategy or passage held, not flooded by everything they should have remembered.

---

## Signature Feature™

### The living saltwater reef aquarium

One hero only. A **living saltwater reef aquarium** built into the nook wall — calm blue-green light, fish in slow orbit, anemone gentle in current. Not a screensaver. Not a looped GIF. A **living** system Shari maintains — honest evidence that beautiful things require steady care.

The reef says: *attention can rest on something that isn't you*. Bioluminescent calm without meditation-app voiceover. ADHD brains find regulation in gentle motion that asks nothing back.

The reading lamp and armchair support the reef — they do not compete.

## Companion Presence™

**Occasionally present — Prepared level.**

Shari may have left the **strategy card deck** angled for discovery or a book flagged — evidence she was here. She is not performing a lecture.

She explains strategies in plain language when asked. She does not clinicalize. She does not stack cards.

**Why not Host:** Intimacy requires lower volume than the Living Room™.

**Why not Absent:** Empty nook would feel like a storage closet, not a refuge.

---

## Kinsey™

**Not in this room.**

Saltwater and dog hair negotiate poorly. Kinsey stays beyond the hallway gate — may be heard softly elsewhere.

If guest needs animal comfort, they move to Living Room™ or Window Seat™.

## UI Language™

| Element | Rule |
|---------|------|
| **Cards** | Strategy and reading cards like index cards on wood — not clinical tiles |
| **Buttons** | "Open," "Save for later" — soft outline |
| **Spacing** | Single column; evening legibility; no dense grids |
| **Overlay** | Reef visible in hero band (~25–30%); warm gradient on text |
| **Typography** | Book-scale line length; strategy blurbs short |
| **Glass** | Avoid cold glass UI — warm paper surfaces |
| **Interaction** | Browse slowly; search available but not shouting |

Growth journey objects (`growth`, `my-journey` mapped to reading-nook in presence engine) may **visit** visually but primary objects stay reading + strategies.

## Never In This Room™

- Fluorescent clinic lighting
- Diagnostic worksheets aesthetic
- Gamified streak badges on strategy cards
- Auto-play video walls
- Open-office noise visual language
- Multiple aquariums or competing water features
- Cartoon emoji strategy icons
- Shame copy about "not coping well enough"
- Feature mall grid at entry
