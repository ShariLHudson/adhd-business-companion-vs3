# Window Seat — Room Look Book

**Place ID:** `window-seat`  
**Status:** Design authority — Phase 1 (no UI implementation)  
**Registry:** [`lib/companionObjects/companionObjectRegistry.ts`](../../lib/companionObjects/companionObjectRegistry.ts)

---

## Purpose

The Window Seat is architecture for **unloading** — where thoughts land before they are sorted, fixed, or turned into tasks.

It exists for **Clear My Mind**. Capture without commitment. Rain without verdict.

**Emotional need served:** "I don't have to carry this alone — I can put it down."

**ADHD challenge reduced:** working memory overflow, thought spirals, shame about messy thinking, pressure to organize before feeling.

Built-in alcove: deep cushion, wide glass, wool blanket reachable with one hand. Not a productivity nook.

---

## Emotional Promise

**When someone leaves this room…** they feel **lighter** — not fixed, not analyzed — carrying hard thoughts at a lower volume.

---

## Signature Feature

### The Iowa view

One hero only. **Rolling Iowa land through wide glass** — oak branches near enough to feel personal, corn and soy fields distant enough to offer perspective, sky owning half the frame.

Rain on glass is character, not defect. Each droplet a tiny lens. Frost ferns in winter. Thunder-green in summer. The view says: *cycles are normal; you are small in a good way; this will move*.

The blanket, journal, and cushion support the view — they do not replace it.

## Motion

Subtle only:

- Rain tracing paths on glass
- Curtain gauze drifting when window cracks
- Oak leaves brushing exterior pane
- Blanket fringe settling when someone shifts
- Cloud shadow crossing fields beyond

No typing animations demanding performance. No pulsing capture buttons.

---

## Companion Presence

**Nearby — quiet listener.**

Shari is within reach — voice soft, questions rare. The **view and the blanket** do most of the holding. She speaks when spoken to.

Silence is hospitality. She does not fix, pattern-diagnose, or turn capture into therapy theater on entry.

**Why not Host:** Performance empathy violates unload safety.

**Why not Absent:** Total absence would feel like shouting into a void on hard days.

---

## Kinsey

**Sleeping — occasionally present.**

Kinsey may be curled on the floor pillow at the foot of the seat — breathing audible, not demanding petting. She is **not** the hero.

- **Greeting:** Rare — soft tail, then settle; no barking at the window
- **Not in this room:** When guest needs zero sensory input — Kinsey stays elsewhere

## UI Language

| Element | Rule |
|---------|------|
| **Cards** | Soft wheat borders; capture cards feel like journal pages, not tickets |
| **Buttons** | Minimal — capture, park for later; no "process now" pressure |
| **Spacing** | Continuous capture surface; generous line height |
| **Overlay** | ~35% hero (view + blanket) then dominant cream workspace |
| **Typography** | Readable at a glance; no dense metadata on each thought |
| **Glass** | Gradient essential — never raw storm glass behind body text |
| **Interaction** | Type and release; organize is invited later, not forced |

Conversation **minimal** — lower-third whisper chip. Clear My Mind is the experience; chat supports, not leads.

---

## Never In This Room

- Kanban boards, priority matrices on capture screen
- AI "you seem stressed" pattern callouts on first keystroke
- Productivity scoring, task auto-creation without consent
- Bright alert red, notification stacks
- Split-screen feature mall
- Clinical diagnostic labels from user text
- Fixing before listening
- Making the blanket or Kinsey the hero over the view

---

## ADHD Rules

| Challenge | How the room helps |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **Executive function** | Capture without categorizing — defer organization |
| **Working memory** | Externalize immediately — journal holds what RAM cannot |
| **Overwhelm** | View offers perspective scale; blanket offers somatic safety |
| **Decision fatigue** | No "what bucket?" on entry — sort later |
| **Attention** | Single focal lane — type or gaze, not both demanded |
| **Emotional regulation** | Rain and land regulate; Shari quiet |

## Seasonal Notes

- **Spring:** Buds on oak inches from glass; lighter blanket; rain frequent
- **Summer:** Thunderstorms then green; blanket pushed aside; window may crack
- **Autumn:** Cold rain; maple at eye level; blanket essential
- **Winter:** Ice on corners; frost ferns; gray sky endless — inside warm

View changes; alcove architecture does not.
