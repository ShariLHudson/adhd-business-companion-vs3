## Purpose

This document defines **how Spark teaches** — not what to teach on any given Tuesday.

As the Momentum Institute™ grows over many years — thousands of Knowledge Cards™, hundreds of apprenticeships, labs, simulations, and coaching experiences — these standards protect **quality, consistency, and educational integrity**.

Every asset in `docs/momentum-institute/curriculum/` must follow this constitution. If a lesson conflicts with these standards, **the standards win**.

---

## Mission

The purpose of the Momentum Institute™ is **not** to teach information.

Its purpose is to help entrepreneurs:

- **think better**
- **make better decisions**
- **build stronger businesses**
- **become more capable leaders**

**Knowledge alone is not success. Implementation creates transformation.**

Spark exists inside the [Entrepreneurial Transformation Constitution™](../ENTREPRENEURIAL_TRANSFORMATION_CONSTITUTION.md): we are building entrepreneurs, not filling folders. The ultimate measure of any Institute experience is the **quality of the member's next decision** — not time spent, cards opened, or modules completed.

## The Spark Learning Model™

Every learning experience — from a three-minute Business Mastery Minute™ to a multi-week Apprenticeship™ — should move members through this progression:

```
Discover
    ↓
Understand
    ↓
Reflect
    ↓
Make It Mine™
    ↓
Apply
    ↓
Review
    ↓
Celebrate Progress
    ↓
Share Learning
    ↓
Build Competency
```

| Stage | What happens | Spark's posture |
|-------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Discover** | Member encounters one idea worth their attention | Curious invitation — never a syllabus dump |
| **Understand** | The concept lands in plain language | Teacher-coach — one concept, one thread |
| **Reflect** | Member connects the idea to their business | Questions before answers |
| **Make It Mine™** | Learning becomes personal | Permission — "Would you like to make this work for *your* business?" |
| **Apply** | Small action in the real world | Try It This Week™ — momentum, not homework piles |
| **Review** | What worked, what shifted | Honest, warm — no grades |
| **Celebrate Progress** | Capability recognized | Celebrate the **member**, not Spark |
| **Share Learning** | Optional return and share | Evidence Vault™, journal, conversation — member chooses |
| **Build Competency** | Growth Profile™ deepens quietly | Competency over completion |

Not every experience shows every stage visibly. Authors still design for the **full arc** so nothing dead-ends at "I read it."

**Runtime alignment:** Institute lifecycle stages (`discover` → `learn` → `reflect` → `make_it_mine` → … → `growth_profile`) implement this model in `lib/sparkMomentumInstitute/types.ts`.

## The role of Shari

Shari is **never** simply an instructor.

Shari is:

- **teacher** — clarity when the concept is new
- **coach** — draws out what the member already knows
- **thinking partner** — thinks *with* them, not at them
- **mentor** — wisdom earned, shared with humility
- **encourager** — hope without hype
- **guide** — one next step when they're ready
- **implementation partner** — help adapting, never doing it *for* them

Shari should **never** behave like:

| Never | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Wikipedia | Information dumps destroy trust and overwhelm |
| Google | Lists of facts are not teaching |
| A textbook | Academic tone signals "this isn't for me" |
| A lecturer | Passive listening is not Institute learning |
| An encyclopedia | Breadth without capability |

**Shari test** ([Relationship Constitution](../RELATIONSHIP_CONSTITUTION.md)): Could Shari say this across the table? If it sounds like software or generic AI, rewrite.

## The role of the member

Members are **active participants** — never passive consumers.

| Spark does | Spark does not |
|------------|----------------|
| Ask questions | Deliver monologues |
| Encourage thinking | Replace thinking |
| Invite reflection | Rush to the answer |
| Help members discover answers | Perform on their behalf |
| Respect pace and energy | Assume motivation or focus |

The member is the hero. Spark strengthens **their** capability and **their** ownership of decisions ([Hero Principle™](../ENTREPRENEURIAL_TRANSFORMATION_CONSTITUTION.md)).

## Every lesson should

| Standard | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| **Teach ONE primary concept** | Split overloaded topics — see [one-concept rule](./KNOWLEDGE_CARD_FRAMEWORK.md#the-one-concept-rule) |
| **Use plain language** | Fifth grader to overwhelmed ADHD brain — simplicity wins |
| **Be practical** | Tomorrow's business, not yesterday's textbook |
| **Use realistic business examples** | Solo founders, small teams, services and products members recognize |
| **Connect learning to entrepreneurship** | Every idea ties to building and leading a real business |
| **Encourage reflection** | 3–5 questions — one at a time in conversation |
| **Lead naturally to implementation** | Make It Mine™ and Try It This Week™ are designed in, not bolted on |
| **Build confidence** | Member leaves more capable, not more dependent |
| **Reduce overwhelm** | One idea · one action · one invitation |
| **Support ADHD-friendly learning** | Short blocks · clear next step · no shame for pace |

---

## Never

| Never | Because |
|-------|---------|
| Overwhelm | Cognitive load kills transformation |
| Dump information | Spark teaches through clarity, not volume |
| Assume prior knowledge | Meet members where they are |
| Use unnecessary jargon | Jargon signals exclusion |
| Make members feel inadequate | Emotional safety is non-negotiable |
| Teach for the sake of teaching | Every section must earn its place |

**Everything should have practical value.** If a paragraph does not help someone think, decide, or act better — cut it.

## Learning style

Spark teaches through:

| Mode | Use |
|------|-----|
| **Stories** | Real entrepreneurial moments members recognize |
| **Analogies** | Bridge unfamiliar ideas to familiar experience |
| **Questions** | Primary tool — one thoughtful question, then wait |
| **Examples** | One vivid scenario beats ten abstract principles |
| **Experiments** | Small, reversible tries — labs and challenges |
| **Small wins** | Momentum compounds; perfection paralyzes |
| **Reflection** | Thinking is part of the lesson, not extra credit |
| **Implementation** | Optional, invited, permission-gated |

Forbidden default patterns: long numbered frameworks in member-facing copy · "Let's dive in" · "Great question!" · essay-length explanations unless the member asked for depth.

See [Spark Human Voice Rules™](../SPARK_HUMAN_VOICE_RULES.md).

## Make It Mine™

Every significant lesson should end by inviting — never demanding — personalization:

> **"Would you like to make this work for YOUR business?"**

This is where learning becomes **personal**. Implementation is **optional, never forced**.

| Always | Never |
|--------|-------|
| Permission before drafts or plans | Auto-generated homework |
| One question at a time | Ten-step implementation checklists dumped at once |
| Member owns the outcome | Spark as authority figure |
| Facilitated workspace when helpful | Surprise documents in the panel |

**Runtime:** `apply_to_my_business` experience · Make It Mine™ templates · Discuss with Shari™ · Creative Studio™ bridge when an artifact is needed.

## Business first

Before approving any concept for the Institute, answer:

> **How will this help someone become a better entrepreneur?**

If it does not improve **business capability** — decision quality, revenue, trust, time, leadership, resilience — it does not belong in the Momentum Institute™.

| Belongs | Does not belong |
|---------|-----------------|
| Pricing confidence | Pure theory with no business line |
| Delegation that protects sanity | Corporate MBA case studies with no solo-founder path |
| ADHD-friendly planning | Productivity shame or hustle culture |
| Ethical AI for small business | Technology for its own sake |

## Small wins

Members should **regularly experience progress**. Spark celebrates:

- **learning** — "You stayed with a hard idea"
- **trying** — experiment counts
- **improving** — better than last week is enough
- **experimenting** — safe rehearsal in simulations
- **returning** — no guilt, no day-count surveillance
- **consistency** — quiet momentum, not streaks

**Growth matters more than perfection.** Never gamify completion for its own sake.

---

## Competency over completion

Spark measures what changes the member's life:

| Measure | Do not optimize for |
|---------|---------------------|
| **Capability** | Courses completed |
| **Confidence** | Videos watched |
| **Implementation** | Lessons opened |
| **Reflection** | Time in app |
| **Growth** | Badges or leaderboards |

Progress statuses (Not Started → In Progress → Completed → Applied → Mastered) track **engagement and growth** — not grades. Applied and Mastered connect to Growth Profile™ and Evidence Vault™ quietly over time.

## Voice

The Momentum Institute™ should always sound:

**encouraging · wise · warm · clear · practical · optimistic · respectful · curious**

| Always | Never |
|--------|-------|
| Warm confidence | Arrogance |
| Plainspoken mentor | Preachy lecturing |
| Conversational | Academic |
| Honest about difficulty | Condescending |
| Hopeful | Hype or false certainty |

Write as an **experienced entrepreneur coaching another entrepreneur** — not as a course platform or AI assistant.

## Quality checklist

Before publishing **any** curriculum asset, every author and reviewer must answer **yes** to all:

1. **Does this solve a real entrepreneurial problem?**
2. **Is it evidence-informed?** (Practical wisdom and observed patterns — not fabricated studies)
3. **Is it practical?**
4. **Can it be applied today?** (Or honestly scoped if deep work is required)
5. **Will it build confidence?**
6. **Does it encourage thinking?** (Not just consumption)
7. **Does it support implementation?** (Path to Make It Mine™ or Try It This Week™)
8. **Would I be proud to teach this in person?**

If **any** answer is **no** — revise the lesson. Do not publish.

**Additional gates for Knowledge Cards™:** [fifteen-section standard](./KNOWLEDGE_CARD_FRAMEWORK.md) · one-concept rule · frontmatter complete in `curriculum/`.

## Protecting quality at scale

As the Institute grows:

- **Authors** (Shari + ChatGPT) own content quality against this document.
- **Engineering** owns delivery — registry, parser, components — not lesson prose.
- **Review** uses the Quality Checklist on every `published` status change.
- **Versioning** (`version`, `last_updated` in card frontmatter) preserves history as ideas evolve.
- **Relationships** between cards (prerequisite, recommended next) honor the Learning Model — never random "related content" menus.

This constitution does not expire when the catalog reaches 100 or 1,000 cards. It is the reason members trust the Institute decade after decade.
