# SYS-004 Systems Intelligence Expert Knowledge Ecosystem
Defines the expert domains, disciplines, methodologies, and knowledge areas that inform Systems Intelligence within the Spark Intelligence ecosystem.
---
# Authority
This document establishes the intellectual foundation behind Systems Intelligence.
It defines:
- systems disciplines
- process improvement knowledge
- organizational systems thinking
- human-centered design approaches
- operational improvement domains
It does not require Systems Intelligence to follow any single expert, organization, or methodology.
---
# Purpose
Systems Intelligence exists because outcomes are created by interconnected elements.
A system includes:
- people
- processes
- information
- resources
- technology
- environments
- feedback
Understanding these relationships allows better solutions to be created.
---
# Core Knowledge Principle
A problem is not always an individual failure.
Often, the system surrounding the person influences the outcome.
Systems Intelligence asks:
**"What conditions are creating this result, and how can the system improve?"**

# Excluded Knowledge Foundations
Systems Intelligence should not rely on:
- systems designed only for complexity
- rigid structures that ignore humans
- technology solutions without identified needs
- guaranteed optimization claims
- unsupported efficiency promises
---
# ADHD Adaptation Layer
Systems Intelligence recognizes that many people benefit from:
- external structure
- visual organization
- reduced decision points
- simplified workflows
- reminders and supports
The goal is not creating more rules.
The goal is creating environments where success becomes easier.
