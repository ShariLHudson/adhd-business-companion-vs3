# SYS-003 Systems Intelligence Knowledge Ownership
Defines the knowledge domains, responsibilities, expertise areas, and boundaries owned by Systems Intelligence within the Spark Intelligence ecosystem.
---
# Authority
This document is the authoritative ownership definition for Systems Intelligence.
It establishes:
- what knowledge Systems Intelligence owns
- what problems it helps solve
- what areas belong to other Intelligence Libraries
It does not contain the complete expert ecosystem or framework library.
---
# Purpose
Systems Intelligence exists to understand how parts work together.
Many challenges are not caused by one isolated issue.
They are caused by:
- disconnected processes
- unclear responsibilities
- inefficient workflows
- missing structures
- systems that do not support human needs
Systems Intelligence helps create structures that improve outcomes.
---
# Core Ownership Statement
Systems Intelligence owns:
**The knowledge of systems thinking, process structures, workflows, relationships, dependencies, optimization, and designing environments where people and processes can work effectively together.**

# Primary Knowledge Domains
---
# 1. Systems Thinking
## Purpose
Understand how interconnected parts influence outcomes.
---
## Knowledge Areas
Includes:
- relationships
- dependencies
- patterns
- feedback loops
- system behavior
---
## Questions
- What parts influence this outcome?
- How are these parts connected?
- What happens when one part changes?

# Specialized Expertise
Systems Intelligence does not replace:
- legal systems
- financial systems
- medical systems
- technical systems
It supports understanding of how components work together.
---
# Systems Intelligence Questions
Systems Intelligence seeks to answer:
- How does this work today?
- What parts are involved?
- How do those parts connect?
- Where is friction occurring?
- What can be simplified?
- How can this become more repeatable?
- How can the system better support people?
---
# Systems Application Principle
Systems Intelligence does not ask:
"How do we add more structure?"
It asks:
**"What structure creates the best support with the least unnecessary friction?"**
---
# Permanent Ownership Rule
Systems Intelligence owns:
**the understanding of systems, structures, processes, workflows, relationships, dependencies, and optimization.**
It does not own:
- strategy
- decisions
- project execution
- technology implementation
- motivation
- personal identity
