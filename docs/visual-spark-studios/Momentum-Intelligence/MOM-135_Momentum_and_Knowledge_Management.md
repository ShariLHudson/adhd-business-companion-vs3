# MOM-135 Momentum and Knowledge Management

Defines the collaboration between Momentum Intelligence and Knowledge Management. This is a relationship document. It defines how the two libraries work together and never redefines either.

---

# Authority

This document describes a collaboration. It owns neither library's concepts. Momentum reasoning remains owned by the Momentum Intelligence Library; the preservation, connection, and lifecycle of information remain owned by Knowledge Management.

---

# Why These Libraries Collaborate

Momentum depends on memory across time — the preserved thread that lets progress accumulate rather than reset (MOM-113 Progress Continuity), the history that lets Spark read momentum patterns (MOM-136). But Momentum does not store anything itself. Knowledge Management owns preservation and organization of information. They collaborate so that momentum continuity and history persist reliably, while Momentum decides only *what about momentum* is worth preserving.

---

# What Information Is Exchanged

- **Momentum → Knowledge Management:** what is worth preserving for continuity and pattern reading — the next step across a gap, the minimal context to resume, momentum signals over time. Momentum specifies *what*; Knowledge Management handles *how it is stored*.
- **Knowledge Management → Momentum:** reliable retrieval of preserved continuity and history when Momentum needs to resume movement or read patterns.

---

# What Each Library Owns

- **Momentum Intelligence owns:** what momentum information matters — the thread to preserve, the context to carry, the signals to remember — and how movement uses it.
- **Knowledge Management owns:** the preservation, organization, connection, and lifecycle of information, including where and how momentum data is stored.

---

# What Each Library Never Owns

- **Momentum never owns:** storage mechanisms, data architecture, or information lifecycle. It references Knowledge Management for all preservation.
- **Knowledge Management never owns:** the definition or reasoning of momentum, or the judgment of what about momentum is meaningful.

---

# How the Collaboration Improves Reasoning

Momentum decides which slivers of information make progress continuous and patterns legible; Knowledge Management preserves them reliably and returns them when needed. This division lets Momentum stay focused on movement (not databases) and lets Knowledge Management stay focused on information (not momentum judgment). Together they turn isolated efforts into accumulating progress and make momentum patterns possible.

---

# Guiding Principles

## Momentum Chooses What to Keep; Knowledge Management Keeps It

Momentum specifies the minimal information worth preserving; storage belongs entirely to Knowledge Management.

## Preserve the Thread, Not the Clutter

Momentum keeps its continuity requests minimal (MOM-113), so preserved context aids resumption rather than becoming friction.

## History Enables Patterns

Reliable preservation by Knowledge Management is what makes momentum pattern reading (MOM-136) possible over time.

---

# Common Mistakes

Avoid:

- Momentum describing storage mechanisms or data architecture (reference Knowledge Management)
- Knowledge Management judging what about momentum is meaningful
- preserving excessive momentum context that becomes clutter
- treating continuity data as owned by Momentum rather than stored by Knowledge Management
