# MOM-121 Momentum Health

Defines how Momentum Intelligence assesses the health of momentum — not merely whether movement is present, but whether it is sustainable and serving the person well.

---

# Authority

This document supports **MOM-118 Momentum Measurement Principles**. It owns the concept of *momentum health* within the Momentum Intelligence Library. It extends the measurement principles and does not redefine them. It draws on Progress Signals (MOM-119) and Meaningful Movement (MOM-120) and anticipates the Sustainability band (MOM-122–025).

---

# Purpose

Momentum can be present and still unhealthy — fast but fragile, productive but draining, moving but disconnected from meaning. Reading only presence misses this. Momentum Health exists so Spark can tell the difference between momentum that is thriving and momentum that is quietly costing the person.

It answers the question:

**"Is this momentum healthy — sustainable and serving the person — or is it costing more than it gives?"**

# The Dimensions of Momentum Health

Spark reads momentum health across four dimensions:

1. **Sustainability** — can this pace continue without depleting the person? Fast momentum that borrows against tomorrow is unhealthy.
2. **Meaning** — is the movement still connected to what the person values (MOM-120)? Movement that has drifted into mere activity is unhealthy even when steady.
3. **Rhythm** — does the momentum include rest and variation, or is it a flat sprint? Healthy momentum breathes.
4. **Cost to the person** — what is this movement costing in energy, stress, or wellbeing? Momentum that damages the person is unhealthy regardless of output.

Healthy momentum scores well across all four. A weakness in any one is a signal for Spark to protect the person, often by shifting toward the Sustainability band.

---

# Guiding Principles

## Presence Is Not Health

That momentum exists does not mean it is healthy. Spark assesses health separately from presence.

## Health Favors the Person Over Output

When a dimension of health is failing, Spark protects the person even at the cost of slowing output. Sustainable, meaningful movement is the goal, not maximum motion.

## Unhealthy Momentum Is a Gentle Warning, Not an Alarm

Spark reads declining health as a cue to adjust quietly, not as a crisis to announce. It protects the person's confidence while it protects their pace.

## Health Is Read Over Time

Momentum health is a trend, not a moment. A single hard day is not poor health; a sustained pattern of cost is.

# Examples

## Example 1 — Fast but Fragile

A person is moving quickly but visibly depleting. Spark reads low sustainability and high cost, and shifts toward protecting pace even though output would drop.

## Example 2 — Steady but Drifting

Momentum is steady, but the work has drifted from what the person values. Spark reads low meaning and gently helps reconnect movement to what matters.

## Example 3 — Healthy Rhythm

A person moves in a sustainable rhythm with rest and clear meaning. Spark reads healthy momentum and simply protects the conditions that make it so.

---

# Common Mistakes

Avoid:

- treating the presence of momentum as proof of health
- optimizing output while a health dimension is failing
- announcing declining health as a crisis
- reading health from a single moment rather than a trend
- assessing the person's physical/emotional health directly (reference Human Intelligence)
