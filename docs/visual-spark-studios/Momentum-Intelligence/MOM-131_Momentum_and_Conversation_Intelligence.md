# MOM-131 Momentum and Conversation Intelligence

Defines the collaboration between Momentum Intelligence and Conversation Intelligence. This is a relationship document. It defines how the two libraries work together and never redefines either.

---

# Authority

This document describes a collaboration. It owns neither library's concepts. Momentum reasoning remains owned by the Momentum Intelligence Library; how anything is said remains owned by Conversation Intelligence.

---

# Why These Libraries Collaborate

Momentum decides *what* support a person needs; Conversation Intelligence decides *how* that support is said. A perfectly reasoned momentum decision delivered in the wrong words can break the very movement it meant to protect. These libraries collaborate so that momentum support is not only correct but lands well.

---

# What Information Is Exchanged

- **Momentum → Conversation:** the substance of the support — the next step to offer, the fact that this is a gentle restart, the need to protect rest, the recognition of meaningful movement. Momentum provides intent, not wording.
- **Conversation → Momentum:** signals from how the person communicates (hesitation, overwhelm, readiness) that inform the momentum reading.

---

# What Each Library Owns

- **Momentum Intelligence owns:** what momentum support is needed and why (the intent behind a nudge, offer, or recovery).
- **Conversation Intelligence owns:** how anything is said — tone, phrasing, timing, and delivery of every message.

---

# What Each Library Never Owns

- **Momentum never owns:** the wording of support. It hands Conversation Intelligence the intent and never dictates the exact phrasing.
- **Conversation Intelligence never owns:** the momentum decision itself — whether to protect, restart, or pace movement.

---

# How the Collaboration Improves Reasoning

Separating intent from delivery keeps both sharp. Momentum can reason about movement without contorting itself into phrasing rules; Conversation can craft humane delivery without having to re-derive momentum logic. The result is support that is both correctly chosen and gently delivered — the difference between a nudge that helps and one that pressures.

---

# Guiding Principles

## Momentum Provides Intent, Conversation Provides Words

Momentum never writes the message; it supplies what the message should accomplish.

## Delivery Can Make or Break Movement

Because wording can protect or break fragile momentum, Momentum trusts Conversation Intelligence to deliver its intent in a way that sustains movement.

## One Support, One Voice

Momentum supplies a single clear intent so Conversation can speak with one coherent voice, never a menu of competing nudges.

---

# Common Mistakes

Avoid:

- Momentum dictating exact phrasing (that belongs to Conversation Intelligence)
- Conversation Intelligence overriding the momentum decision
- delivering momentum intent in a tone that pressures fragile movement
- merging intent and delivery into one undifferentiated step
