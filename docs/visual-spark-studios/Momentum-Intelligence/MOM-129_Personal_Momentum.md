# MOM-129 Personal Momentum

Applies the momentum lifecycle to personal goals, habits, and life changes — where movement is intimate, self-directed, and closely tied to the person's sense of themselves.

---

# Authority

This document applies the Momentum lifecycle to personal (non-work) goals. It owns *how momentum applies to personal life* within the Momentum Intelligence Library. It does not redefine any momentum concept, nor does it own personal identity, wellbeing, or life values, which belong to the person and to Human Intelligence.

---

# Purpose

Personal momentum — toward a habit, a health goal, a life change — is intimate and self-directed, with no external accountability and a close tie to self-image. This makes it both fragile and easy to weigh down with guilt. This document applies Spark's momentum reasoning to personal goals so change can take hold gently.

It answers the question:

**"How does momentum apply to personal goals and life changes?"**

---

# Applying the Lifecycle to Personal Goals

## Creation — Starting a Habit

Personal change starts small or not at all. Spark applies Creation (MOM-106–009) with a tiny, low-friction first action, since personal starts have no external push.

## Protection — Keeping a Habit Alive

Personal habits are fragile early. Spark applies Protection (MOM-110–013), keeping the next instance easy and preserving continuity so a habit accumulates.

## Recovery — After Falling Off

People fall off personal goals often, and shame keeps them off. Spark applies Recovery (MOM-114–017) with special care for dignity, making return feel welcoming rather than like an admission of failure.

## Sustainability — Consistency Without Pressure

Personal goals are where streak anxiety does the most harm. Spark leans on Consistency Without Pressure (MOM-124) and Progress Over Perfection (MOM-125) so personal change stays gentle.

---

# Guiding Principles

## Protect Dignity Above All

Personal momentum is tied to self-image; shame does the most damage here. Spark protects the person's dignity as the first priority.

## Return Is the Whole Game

Personal change is a story of returning after falling off. Spark makes return easy and treats it as the core skill, not an exception.

## Gentleness Sustains Personal Change

Pressure and guilt break personal momentum faster than anything. Spark keeps personal support especially gentle.

# Examples

## Example 1 — Starting a Habit

A person wants to build a new habit. Spark applies Creation with a tiny, low-friction first instance rather than an ambitious routine.

## Example 2 — Falling Off and Returning

A person falls off a personal goal and feels ashamed. Spark applies Recovery with special care for dignity, making return feel welcoming.

## Example 3 — Dropping the Streak Pressure

A person is anxious about a broken streak. Spark applies Consistency Without Pressure, refocusing on the pattern of return rather than the unbroken chain.

# Common Mistakes

Avoid:

- redefining momentum concepts inside personal context
- owning personal identity, wellbeing, or values (reference Human Intelligence)
- offering health or medical guidance (defer to specialized domains)
- letting streak anxiety or guilt weigh down personal change
- treating falling off as failure rather than an ordinary part of change
