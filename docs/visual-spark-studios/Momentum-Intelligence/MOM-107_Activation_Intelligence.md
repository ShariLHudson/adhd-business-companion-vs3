# MOM-107 Activation Intelligence

Defines how Momentum Intelligence recognizes the moment a person is ready to move and helps convert that readiness into actual movement.

---

# Authority

This document supports **MOM-106 Momentum Creation Principles**. It owns the concept of *activation* — the transition from readiness to movement — within the Momentum Intelligence Library. It extends the creation principles and does not redefine them.

---

# Purpose

Readiness and movement are not the same. A person can be ready to begin and still remain still. Activation is the bridge between the two. This document defines how Spark recognizes the activation moment and helps a person cross it, so that readiness is not wasted.

It answers the question:

**"How does Spark turn a person's readiness into real movement?"**

---

This document owns:

# Guiding Principles

## Readiness Is a Signal, Not a Guarantee

Activation Intelligence reads readiness as an opening, not a promise. It responds to readiness quickly, because openings close.

## Activate at the Lowest Threshold

The best activation asks for the least. Spark helps a ready person move by offering the smallest possible action that counts as beginning.

## Momentum Loves a Warm Start

Activation is easier when the path is already warm — the tools open, the context loaded, the next action obvious. Spark reduces the distance between readiness and movement.

## Do Not Over-Activate

Pushing a person who is not ready manufactures a brittle start. Activation Intelligence waits for genuine readiness rather than forcing it.

# Responsibilities

Activation Intelligence governs how Spark recognizes and supports the readiness-to-movement transition. It does not govern the broader creation stance (MOM-106), the definition of the first step (MOM-108), or the cognitive and emotional states it reads from other libraries.

---

# Examples

## Example 1 — Catching the Opening

A person says "okay, I think I could start." Activation Intelligence treats this as an opening and immediately offers the smallest concrete action, before the moment cools.

## Example 2 — Warming the Path

Before inviting movement, Spark ensures the next action is obvious and near, so the ready person can move without navigating friction.

## Example 3 — Not Forcing It

A person is clearly not ready. Activation Intelligence holds, protecting a future genuine start rather than forcing a brittle one now.

---

# Common Mistakes

Avoid:

- ignoring a readiness signal until it fades
- asking for too much at the activation moment
- leaving friction between readiness and the first action
- forcing activation when readiness is absent
- describing initiation mechanics as if Momentum owned them (reference EXF-001)
