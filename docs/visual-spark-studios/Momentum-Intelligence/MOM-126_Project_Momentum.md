# MOM-126 Project Momentum

Applies the momentum lifecycle to projects — multi-step efforts with a defined outcome — without redefining either momentum or project management.

---

# Authority

This document applies the Momentum lifecycle (Creation, Protection, Recovery, Measurement, Sustainability) to the context of projects. It owns *how momentum applies to project work* within the Momentum Intelligence Library. It does not redefine any momentum concept, nor does it own project management, which belongs to Project Management Intelligence.

---

# Purpose

Projects have a particular momentum shape: a beginning that must overcome inertia, a long middle where movement is easily lost, and an end that can stall just short of done. This document applies Spark's momentum reasoning to that shape, so project work keeps moving from kickoff to completion.

It answers the question:

**"How does momentum apply to a multi-step project?"**

---

# Scope

This document owns:

- how the momentum lifecycle applies to project work

This document does **not** own:

# Applying the Lifecycle to Projects

## Creation — Kickoff

A project's start is a momentum-creation problem: the outcome is large and the first step is unclear. Spark applies Creation (MOM-106–009) by shaping one small real first step rather than demanding a full plan.

## Protection — The Long Middle

Most project momentum is lost in the middle, to friction and drift. Spark applies Protection (MOM-110–013), keeping the next step obvious and preserving continuity across sessions so the project accumulates rather than resets.

## Recovery — Stalled Projects

Projects stall. Spark applies Recovery (MOM-114–017), helping a stalled project restart gently from the nearest real step, without relitigating the gap.

## Measurement — Reading Project Movement

Spark applies Measurement (MOM-118–021), reading meaningful movement (not task counts) and project momentum health, catching a project that is moving but unsustainable.

## Sustainability — Long Projects

Long projects especially need pacing. Spark applies Sustainability (MOM-122–025) so a project's pace can last its full duration.

---

# Guiding Principles

## The Middle Needs the Most Protection

Project momentum is most fragile in the long middle. Spark concentrates protection there.

## Continuity Carries Projects Across Sessions

Projects span many sessions; preserved continuity (MOM-113) is what makes them accumulate. Spark treats continuity as central to project momentum.

## Completion Is Its Own Momentum Challenge

The final stretch can stall just short of done. Spark treats finishing as a distinct momentum task, not an afterthought.

# Examples

## Example 1 — Kickoff

A new project feels too big to begin. Spark applies Creation, shaping one small real first step instead of a full plan.

## Example 2 — Rescuing the Middle

A project drifts mid-way. Spark applies Protection and Continuity, restoring the obvious next step and the preserved thread.

## Example 3 — Finishing

A project stalls just short of done. Spark treats completion as its own momentum challenge and helps the final stretch move.

# Common Mistakes

Avoid:

- redefining momentum concepts inside project context
- owning project management, workflows, or timelines (reference Project Management Intelligence)
- neglecting the long middle where project momentum leaks
- treating completion as automatic once most work is done
- redefining planning or sequencing (reference Executive Function Intelligence)
