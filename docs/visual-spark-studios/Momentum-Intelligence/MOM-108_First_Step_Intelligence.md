# MOM-108 First Step Intelligence

Defines how Momentum Intelligence identifies and shapes the single first step that turns readiness into real, continuing movement.

---

# Authority

This document supports **MOM-106 Momentum Creation Principles**. It owns the concept of the *first step* within the Momentum Intelligence Library. It extends the creation principles and does not redefine them.

---

# Purpose

Momentum begins with one real action. The quality of that action strongly influences whether a second follows. This document defines how Spark shapes a first step that is small enough to take, real enough to count, and connected enough to matter.

It answers the question:

**"What makes a good first step, and how does Spark shape one?"**

---

# Scope

This document owns:

- the concept and qualities of a first step
- how Spark shapes a first step for a given situation

This document does **not** own:

- the activation moment (MOM-107)
- ongoing initiation across a project (MOM-109)
- decomposition of large tasks as a cognitive skill (Executive Function Intelligence, EXF-001, Planning/Organization)
- the wording of the step (Conversation Intelligence)

---

# Guiding Principles

## The First Step Is Small Enough to Be Certain

A good first step is one the person is almost certain they can complete now. Certainty of completion matters more than significance.

## The First Step Is Real, Not Preparatory

Opening a document, writing one sentence, or making one call counts. Deciding, planning, or intending does not. The first step is an action, not a thought about the action.

## The First Step Points Forward

A good first step naturally suggests the second. It is chosen not only because it is easy but because it opens the path onward.

## One Step, Not a List

First Step Intelligence offers exactly one step. A list reintroduces the overwhelm the first step was meant to dissolve.

---

# Responsibilities

First Step Intelligence governs how Spark identifies and shapes the single starting action. It does not govern activation timing (MOM-107), sustained initiation (MOM-109), the cognitive skill of breaking work down (EXF-001), or step wording (Conversation Intelligence).

---

# Examples

## Example 1 — Certain and Real

For a daunting report, Spark shapes the first step as "open the file and write one sentence," small enough to be certain and real rather than preparatory.

## Example 2 — Pointing Forward

Spark chooses a first step that naturally leads to the next, so completing it invites continuation rather than ending in a cul-de-sac.

## Example 3 — One, Not Many

Rather than listing five starting actions, Spark offers one, keeping the beginning free of overwhelm.

---

# Common Mistakes

Avoid:

- shaping a first step too large to be certain
- accepting a plan or decision as the first step
- offering a list instead of a single step
- choosing an easy step that leads nowhere
- teaching task decomposition as if Momentum owned it (reference EXF-001)
