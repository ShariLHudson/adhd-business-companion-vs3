# MOM-105 Knowledge Map

Defines the internal structure of the Momentum Intelligence Library — how its concepts are organized, how its documents relate, and where any given momentum question is answered.

---

# Authority

This document is the authoritative map of the Momentum Intelligence Library's internal structure. It defines how the library's concepts are arranged into bands and how documents depend on and support one another.

It organizes the concepts defined by **MOM-001 Momentum Model** and does not redefine them. Where the model defines momentum, this document shows where each part of momentum is developed across the library.

---

# Purpose

A library grows useful only when its knowledge is navigable. As Momentum Intelligence expands to dozens of documents, people and Chamber Members need a reliable way to find which document owns a concept and how concepts connect.

The purpose of this document is to provide that map: a single place that shows the shape of the library, so questions route to the right document and no concept is defined in two places.

It answers the question:

**"Where in the Momentum Intelligence Library is each concept, and how do the pieces fit together?"**

# The Shape of the Library

Momentum Intelligence is organized into a Core and seven functional bands. The Core establishes identity; each band develops one aspect of momentum, opening with a principles document that its supporting documents reference.

## Core (001–005) — identity and structure

Defines what momentum is (MOM-001), what Spark believes about it (MOM-102), how it reasons (MOM-103), the order of that reasoning (MOM-104), and this map (MOM-105). Everything else inherits from the Core.

## Creation (006–009) — starting movement

How momentum begins: creation principles (MOM-106), activation (MOM-107), the first step (MOM-108), and progress initiation (MOM-109). This band answers *how does movement start?*

## Protection (010–013) — keeping movement

How momentum is kept once it exists: protection principles (MOM-110), friction reduction (MOM-111), distraction protection (MOM-112), and progress continuity (MOM-113). This band answers *how does movement continue?*

## Recovery (014–017) — restoring movement

How momentum returns after loss: recovery principles (MOM-114), restart (MOM-115), interrupted momentum (MOM-116), and low-capacity recovery (MOM-117). This band answers *how does movement come back?*

## Measurement (018–021) — understanding movement

How momentum is read: measurement principles (MOM-118), progress signals (MOM-119), meaningful movement as a measurable idea (MOM-120), and momentum health (MOM-121). This band answers *how do we know movement is real and healthy?*

## Sustainability (022–025) — movement over time

How momentum lasts without harm: sustainable momentum (MOM-122), rhythm and pacing (MOM-123), consistency without pressure (MOM-124), and progress over perfection (MOM-125). This band answers *how does movement stay healthy long term?*

## Application (026–029) — movement in context

How momentum works in specific settings: project (MOM-126), creative (MOM-127), business (MOM-128), and personal (MOM-129). This band answers *how does momentum apply to this kind of work?*

## Collaboration (030–035) — movement with other libraries

Relationship documents that describe how Momentum works with Human, Conversation, Founder, Executive Function, Decision, and Knowledge Management intelligences. These never redefine either library.

## Adaptive (036–037) — movement that learns

How momentum reasoning adapts: patterns (MOM-136) and recommendations (MOM-137). This band answers *what recurring shapes does momentum take, and what should Spark recommend?*

---

# How the Bands Relate

- The **Core** is inherited by every band.
- **Creation → Protection → Recovery** form a lifecycle: movement starts, is kept, and is restored when lost.
- **Measurement** observes all three lifecycle bands and feeds **Adaptive**.
- **Sustainability** constrains every other band: no technique may create momentum that is unsustainable.
- **Application** applies the lifecycle to specific domains without redefining it.
- **Collaboration** connects the library outward; it consumes the Core and lifecycle but owns no momentum concept of its own.

# Common Mistakes

Avoid:

- defining a concept outside its owning document
- treating Application documents as if they redefine the lifecycle
- letting Collaboration documents own a momentum concept
- routing a cross-library question as if Momentum owned the other concept
- duplicating the file registry that INDEX.md and MANIFEST.md own
