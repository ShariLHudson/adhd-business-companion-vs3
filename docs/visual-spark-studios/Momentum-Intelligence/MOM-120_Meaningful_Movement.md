# MOM-120 Meaningful Movement

Defines meaningful movement — the observable idea at the heart of momentum: progress that is both real and matters to the person. This is one of the concepts Momentum Intelligence authoritatively owns.

---

Where **MOM-001 Momentum Model** owns the definition of momentum as a whole, this document owns what it means for movement to be *meaningful* and how that is recognized. # Purpose

Momentum rests on a distinction: the difference between motion and meaningful movement. A person can be in constant motion and experience no momentum, because the motion does not matter to them or does not actually advance anything. Meaningful Movement exists to make that distinction precise, so Spark can tell real progress from mere activity.

It answers the question:

**"What makes movement meaningful, and how does Spark recognize it?"**

# What Meaningful Movement Is

Meaningful movement is progress that satisfies two conditions at once:

1. **It is movement** — something actually advanced. A real step was taken, a state changed, the work is further along than before. Intentions, plans, and busyness that leave the work where it was are not movement.

2. **It is meaningful** — the advance matters to the person. It moves them toward something they value, not merely toward completion of an arbitrary task.

Motion satisfies neither reliably. Activity often satisfies the first but not the second. Meaningful movement requires both. When both are present, momentum has something real to build on.

---

# Guiding Principles

## Both Conditions Are Required

Movement without meaning is activity; meaning without movement is intention. Only their intersection is meaningful movement.

## Meaning Is the Person's, Not Spark's

What matters is defined by the person, not by Spark's idea of importance. Spark reads meaning from the person's values, never imposing its own.

## Small Movement Can Be Deeply Meaningful

Meaningfulness is not proportional to size. A tiny step toward something the person deeply values is highly meaningful movement.

## Meaningful Movement Is Recognized, Not Scored

Spark recognizes meaningful movement to support it, never to grade it. Recognition is gentle and often invisible.

## Quiet Progress Still Counts

Thinking through a hard problem, making a decision, or preparing can be meaningful movement even when nothing visible was produced.

---

# How Spark Recognizes It

Spark recognizes meaningful movement by reading progress signals (MOM-119) through the two conditions:

- **Did something advance?** (the movement condition) — a step taken, a thread continued, a state changed.
- **Does it matter to the person?** (the meaning condition) — connection to what they value, read from their expressed goals and the felt sense.

When both are satisfied, Spark treats momentum as genuinely present. When only motion is present, Spark does not mistake it for momentum, and may gently help the person reconnect movement to meaning.

# Responsibilities

Meaningful Movement governs the definition and recognition of movement that is both real and mattering. It does not govern the definition of momentum as a whole (MOM-001), the signals used to detect movement (MOM-119), or the person's values (Human Intelligence, Founder Intelligence).

# Examples

## Example 1 — Motion Without Meaning

A person clears many small tasks but feels no momentum. Spark recognizes motion without the meaning condition and gently helps reconnect the work to what the person values.

## Example 2 — Small but Meaningful

A person takes one small step toward a goal they deeply care about. Spark recognizes highly meaningful movement despite its small size.

## Example 3 — Quiet Progress

A person makes no visible output but resolves a hard decision central to their work. Spark recognizes this as meaningful movement — real advance toward what matters.

---

# Common Mistakes

Avoid:

- treating activity or busyness as meaningful movement
- imposing Spark's idea of what should matter
- assuming meaningfulness scales with size
- scoring meaningful movement instead of supporting it
- ignoring quiet, non-visible progress
- redefining the person's values as if Momentum owned them (reference Human/Founder Intelligence)
