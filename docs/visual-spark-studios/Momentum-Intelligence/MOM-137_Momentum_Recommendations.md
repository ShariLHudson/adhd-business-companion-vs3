# MOM-137 Momentum Recommendations

Defines momentum recommendations — what Spark should recommend to support a person's meaningful movement, given their current condition and patterns. This is one of the concepts Momentum Intelligence authoritatively owns.

---

# Authority

This document is the authoritative owner of *momentum recommendations* within the Visual Spark Studios™ ecosystem, and it closes the Adaptive band (MOM-136–037). It owns **what** momentum support to recommend. It does **not** own how recommendations are generated system-wide or how they are worded and delivered — those belong to Recommendation Intelligence and Conversation Intelligence.

It draws on the whole library — the lifecycle bands, Measurement (MOM-118–021), and Momentum Patterns (MOM-136).

---

# Purpose

Reading momentum is only useful if it leads to the right support. Momentum Recommendations exists to define how Spark chooses *what* to recommend — which single momentum action best serves the person's current condition — so that reading momentum turns into helping it.

It answers the question:

**"Given this person's momentum condition and patterns, what is the single best momentum action to recommend?"**

# How Momentum Recommendations Are Chosen

A momentum recommendation follows the Thinking Engine (MOM-104): observe, understand the condition, then determine the single most valuable support. The recommendation is the *what* — the substance of that support — expressed as intent, not wording:

- If movement is **forming**, recommend protecting the fragile start (MOM-109, MOM-110).
- If movement is **leaking**, recommend removing the specific friction or guarding the thread (MOM-111, MOM-112).
- If movement is **stalled**, recommend a gentle restart from the nearest real step (MOM-114, MOM-115).
- If momentum is **unhealthy**, recommend a sustainability adjustment — pace, rest, or reconnecting to meaning (MOM-121, MOM-122–025).
- Patterns (MOM-136) personalize the choice to what tends to work for this person.

Spark recommends exactly one momentum action — the highest-value one — and hands its intent to Conversation Intelligence for delivery.

---

# Guiding Principles

## Recommend One Thing

Spark recommends a single best momentum action, never a menu. One clear recommendation protects the person from the overwhelm that erodes momentum.

## Recommend the Smallest High-Value Action

The best recommendation is usually the smallest action that meaningfully helps — a small real step over an ambitious program.

## Recommend to Support, Never to Pressure

Momentum recommendations are offered as help the person can accept or decline, never as pressure, quota, or obligation.

## Recommend the What, Not the Words

Spark owns the substance of the recommendation and hands wording to Conversation Intelligence and Recommendation Intelligence.

## Personalize With Patterns, Lightly

Patterns (MOM-136) shape the recommendation toward what tends to work for this person, without hardening into a rule.

# Examples

## Example 1 — Stalled Movement

A person's momentum has stalled. Spark recommends one thing — a gentle restart from the nearest real step (MOM-115) — and hands the intent to Conversation Intelligence to word warmly.

## Example 2 — Unhealthy Momentum

Momentum is present but unsustainable. Spark recommends a single sustainability adjustment (pace or rest), not a productivity push.

## Example 3 — Personalized by Pattern

A person's patterns show they restart best with a tiny concrete action. Spark shapes the recommendation toward that, lightly, without treating the pattern as a rule.

---

# Common Mistakes

Avoid:

- recommending a menu instead of one best action
- recommending large actions when a small one would help more
- letting a recommendation become pressure or obligation
- writing the wording of the recommendation (reference Conversation Intelligence)
- owning system-wide recommendation generation (reference Recommendation Intelligence)
- treating a personalizing pattern as a fixed rule (reference MOM-136)
