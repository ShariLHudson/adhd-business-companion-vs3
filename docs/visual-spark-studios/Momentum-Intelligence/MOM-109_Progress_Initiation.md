# MOM-109 Progress Initiation

Defines how Momentum Intelligence turns a first step into early, self-sustaining progress — the transition from having started to being underway.

---

# Authority

This document supports **MOM-106 Momentum Creation Principles**. It owns the concept of *progress initiation* — the early stretch just after starting — within the Momentum Intelligence Library. It extends the creation principles and does not redefine them.

---

# Purpose

A first step is not yet momentum; it is the seed of it. The stretch just after starting decides whether a single action becomes a rhythm. This document defines how Spark nurtures that early stretch so that starting becomes being underway.

It answers the question:

**"How does Spark help a first step grow into early, self-sustaining progress?"**

---

# Scope

This document owns:

- the concept of the early post-start stretch
- how Spark helps a first step become continuing movement

This document does **not** own:

- the first step itself (MOM-108)
- protecting established movement (MOM-110 Protection band)
- the person's energy or emotional state (Human Intelligence)

---

# Guiding Principles

## The Second Step Matters Most

Momentum is confirmed by the second step, not the first. Progress Initiation focuses on making the second step as natural and near as the first.

## Let Early Progress Be Visible

Seeing that movement has actually begun strengthens the will to continue. Spark helps early progress be noticed without turning it into a scoreboard.

## Do Not Rush the Handoff to Protection

Early progress is still fragile. Progress Initiation holds a gentle stance until movement is steady enough to hand off to the Protection band.

## Keep the Next Action Obvious

The surest way to continue is to always know the next small action. Progress Initiation keeps that next action clear so momentum does not stall on ambiguity.

---

# Responsibilities

Progress Initiation governs the early stretch between starting and being underway. It does not govern the first step (MOM-108), the protection of established movement (MOM-110–013), or the person's state (Human Intelligence).

---

# Examples

## Example 1 — Securing the Second Step

After a person completes their first step, Spark makes the second step obvious and near, converting a single action into the beginning of a rhythm.

## Example 2 — Quietly Visible Progress

Spark reflects that movement has genuinely begun, strengthening continuation without turning early progress into a performance metric.

## Example 3 — Gentle Handoff

Once movement steadies, Progress Initiation hands off to the Protection band rather than continuing to treat the work as a fragile start.

---

# Common Mistakes

Avoid:

- celebrating the first step but leaving the second undefined
- turning early progress into a scoreboard
- handing off to protection before movement is steady
- letting the next action become ambiguous
- reading emotional state as if Momentum owned it (reference Human Intelligence)
