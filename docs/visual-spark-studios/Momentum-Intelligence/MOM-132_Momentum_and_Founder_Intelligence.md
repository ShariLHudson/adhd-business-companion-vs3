# MOM-132 Momentum and Founder Intelligence

Defines the collaboration between Momentum Intelligence and Founder Intelligence. This is a relationship document. It defines how the two libraries work together and never redefines either.

---

# Authority

This document describes a collaboration. It owns neither library's concepts. Momentum reasoning remains owned by the Momentum Intelligence Library; founder strategy, business meaning, and founder development remain owned by Founder Intelligence.

---

# Why These Libraries Collaborate

A founder's work is a long, high-stakes effort where momentum matters enormously and burnout is a constant risk. Founder Intelligence understands the founder's strategy, priorities, and the meaning of the business. Momentum Intelligence understands how meaningful movement continues. They collaborate so that a founder keeps moving on what matters without exhausting themselves — strategy from one library, sustainable movement from the other.

---

# What Information Is Exchanged

- **Founder Intelligence → Momentum:** what matters most in the business, the founder's priorities and goals, and the meaning behind the work. Momentum uses this to know which movement to protect.
- **Momentum → Founder Intelligence:** the state of the founder's momentum — where movement is strong, stalled, scattered, or unsustainable — informing founder guidance.

---

# What Each Library Owns

- **Momentum Intelligence owns:** how the founder's meaningful movement is created, protected, recovered, and sustained.
- **Founder Intelligence owns:** business strategy, founder priorities, business meaning, and founder development.

---

# What Each Library Never Owns

- **Momentum never owns:** business strategy, what the business should prioritize, or the meaning of the work. It reads these from Founder Intelligence.
- **Founder Intelligence never owns:** the definition or reasoning of momentum. It references Momentum for how movement continues.

---

# How the Collaboration Improves Reasoning

Founder Intelligence tells Momentum *which* movement matters; Momentum tells Founder Intelligence *how* to keep that movement alive and sustainable. Together they help a founder concentrate momentum on what matters and protect their capacity across a long horizon — neither scattering effort (a strategy failure) nor burning out (a momentum failure).

---

# Guiding Principles

## Strategy Names the Movement; Momentum Sustains It

Founder Intelligence decides what matters; Momentum protects movement toward it without redefining the strategy.

## Founder Capacity Is a Shared Concern

Both libraries care about the founder's endurance — Founder Intelligence for its strategic value, Momentum for its role in sustainable movement.

## Protect Meaning, Don't Set It

Momentum keeps the founder's work connected to the meaning Founder Intelligence owns; it does not define that meaning.

---

# Common Mistakes

Avoid:

- Momentum setting business strategy or priorities (reference Founder Intelligence)
- Founder Intelligence redefining momentum
- protecting scattered motion instead of strategically meaningful movement
- ignoring founder capacity as a shared concern
