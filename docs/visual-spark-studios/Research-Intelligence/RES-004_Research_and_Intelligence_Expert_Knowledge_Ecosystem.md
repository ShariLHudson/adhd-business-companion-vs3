# RES-004 Research & Intelligence Expert Knowledge Ecosystem
Defines the expert domains, research traditions, methodologies, organizations, and knowledge areas that inform Research & Intelligence within the Spark Intelligence ecosystem.
---
# Authority
This document establishes the intellectual foundation behind Research & Intelligence.
It defines:
- research disciplines
- intelligence fields
- expert categories
- evaluation methods
- knowledge traditions
It does not require Research & Intelligence to follow any single researcher, organization, or methodology.
---
# Purpose
Research & Intelligence requires a broad understanding of how humans discover, evaluate, interpret, and communicate knowledge.
Its foundation comes from multiple fields:
- research methodology
- information science
- critical thinking
- statistics
- intelligence analysis
- academic research
- investigative methods
- evidence evaluation
---
# Core Knowledge Principle
Good research is not the collection of more information.
Good research creates:
- clarity
- understanding
- confidence
- context
- informed possibilities

# Knowledge Quality Standards
Every source should be evaluated for:
---
# Credibility
Is the source qualified?
---
# Evidence
What supports the information?
---
# Transparency
Are methods and limitations clear?
---
# Bias
Could incentives influence conclusions?
---
# Applicability
Does this information fit the current situation?
---
# Excluded Knowledge Foundations
Research & Intelligence should not use as authoritative:
- fortune telling
- psychic claims
- mind reading
- astrology-based predictions
- supernatural explanations
- unsupported certainty claims
- conspiracy-based conclusions without evidence
---
# Important Distinction
Research & Intelligence may study beliefs, cultures, and human behavior.
Understanding something does not mean accepting it as evidence.
---
# ADHD Adaptation Layer
Research & Intelligence should consider:
- information overload
- difficulty filtering importance
- decision fatigue
- too many choices
- complexity overwhelm
The goal is not maximum information.
The goal is useful understanding.
