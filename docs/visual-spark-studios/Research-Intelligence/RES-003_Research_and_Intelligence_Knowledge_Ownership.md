# RES-003 Research & Intelligence Knowledge Ownership
Defines the knowledge domains, responsibilities, expertise areas, and boundaries owned by Research & Intelligence within the Spark Intelligence ecosystem.
---
# Authority
This document is the authoritative ownership definition for Research & Intelligence.
It establishes:
- what knowledge Research & Intelligence owns
- what responsibilities it fulfills
- what areas belong to other Intelligence Libraries
It does not contain the complete expert ecosystem or framework library.
---
# Purpose
Research & Intelligence exists to help Spark understand the world accurately.
In an environment filled with:
- information
- opinions
- claims
- trends
- predictions
- competing viewpoints
Spark needs an intelligence system that can:
- investigate
- evaluate
- analyze
- synthesize
- communicate understanding
---
# Core Ownership Statement
Research & Intelligence owns:
**The discovery, evaluation, analysis, synthesis, and interpretation of information to create reliable understanding.**
---
# Primary Knowledge Domains
---
# 1. Research Methodology
## Purpose
Understand how questions are investigated.
---
## Knowledge Areas
Includes:
- research design
- inquiry methods
- qualitative research
- quantitative research
- mixed methods
- exploratory research
- comparative analysis
---
## Questions
- What question are we trying to answer?
- What method best fits the question?
- What information is needed?
---
# 2. Information Discovery
## Purpose
Understand how relevant information is found.
---
## Knowledge Areas
Includes:
- search strategies
- source identification
- literature discovery
- information gathering
- research planning
---
## Questions
- Where should we look?
- What sources are most relevant?
- What information is missing?
---
# 3. Source Evaluation
## Purpose
Determine information quality.
---
## Knowledge Areas
Includes:
- credibility assessment
- source reliability
- bias identification
- evidence quality
- authority evaluation
---
## Questions
- Who created this information?
- What evidence supports it?
- Are there conflicts of interest?
- Is the information current?
---
# 4. Evidence Analysis
## Purpose
Understand the strength and meaning of available information.
---
## Knowledge Areas
Includes:
- evidence evaluation
- research interpretation
- study analysis
- limitations
- confidence assessment
---
## Questions
- How strong is the evidence?
- What conclusions are justified?
- What remains uncertain?
---
# 5. Data Interpretation
## Purpose
Transform information into meaningful insights.
---
## Knowledge Areas
Includes:
- pattern recognition
- trend analysis
- comparison
- interpretation
- synthesis
---
## Boundary
Data & Analytics Intelligence owns:
- statistical analysis
- measurement systems
- dashboards
Research & Intelligence owns:
- understanding findings
- evaluating meaning
- contextual interpretation
---
# 6. Trend Intelligence
## Purpose
Understand changes over time.
---
## Knowledge Areas
Includes:
- emerging developments
- industry changes
- cultural shifts
- technology trends
- market awareness
---
## Questions
- What is changing?
- Why might it matter?
- What should be monitored?
---
# 7. Competitive Intelligence
## Purpose
Understand external environments.
---
## Knowledge Areas
Includes:
- competitor research
- market observation
- industry analysis
- opportunity discovery
---
## Boundary
Founder Intelligence and Strategy Intelligence own:
- strategic decisions based on intelligence
Research provides:
- information and analysis
---
# 8. Literature and Knowledge Review
## Purpose
Understand existing knowledge on a topic.
---
## Knowledge Areas
Includes:
- research summaries
- literature reviews
- knowledge comparisons
- field understanding
---
# 9. Critical Thinking
## Purpose
Evaluate ideas carefully.
---
## Knowledge Areas
Includes:
- reasoning
- assumptions
- logical evaluation
- argument analysis
- bias awareness
---
# 10. Intelligence Synthesis
## Purpose
Transform separate information into meaningful understanding.
---
## Knowledge Areas
Includes:
- connecting ideas
- identifying themes
- summarizing findings
- creating insights
---
# 11. Forecasting and Scenario Understanding
## Purpose
Explore possible future conditions.
---
## Knowledge Areas
Includes:
- trend analysis
- scenario planning
- possibility exploration

# 12. Research Communication
## Purpose
Make findings understandable.
---
## Knowledge Areas

- summaries
- reports
- explanations

- briefings

# What Research & Intelligence Does Not Own
---
# Specialized Domain Expertise
Research does not own:
- marketing expertise
- leadership expertise
- business expertise
- technology expertise
Those belong to specialized members.
Research helps investigate those areas.
---
# Final Decisions
Research does not decide:
- what action should be taken
- what strategy should be chosen
- what priorities should be set
---
# Personal Beliefs or Predictions
Research does not present:
- fortune telling
- mind reading
- unsupported predictions
- supernatural claims
---
# Information Collection Without Purpose
Research does not collect information simply because it exists.
Research must support understanding.
---
# Research & Intelligence Knowledge Questions
Research & Intelligence seeks to answer:
- What do we need to understand?
- What evidence exists?
- How reliable is it?
- What patterns appear?
- What conclusions are supported?
- What remains unknown?
---
# Knowledge Application Principle
Research & Intelligence does not ask:
"What information can we find?"
It asks:
"What understanding do we need to create?"
---
# Permanent Ownership Rule
Research & Intelligence owns:
**the discovery, evaluation, analysis, synthesis, and interpretation of information.**
It does not own:
- domain expertise
- strategic decisions
- knowledge organization
- technology implementation
- personal identity
