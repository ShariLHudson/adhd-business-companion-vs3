# STR-003 Strategy Intelligence Knowledge Ownership
Defines the knowledge domains, responsibilities, expertise areas, and boundaries owned by Strategy Intelligence within the Spark Intelligence ecosystem.
---
# Authority
This document is the authoritative ownership definition for Strategy Intelligence.
It establishes:
- what knowledge Strategy Intelligence owns
- what strategic problems it helps solve
- what areas belong to other Intelligence Libraries
It does not contain the complete expert ecosystem or framework library.
---
# Purpose
Strategy exists to create alignment between:
- goals
- resources
- opportunities
- actions
- desired outcomes
Without strategy, effort can become scattered.
Strategy Intelligence helps answer:
- Where should attention go?
- What matters most?
- What creates the greatest value?
- How should resources be aligned?
---
# Core Ownership Statement
Strategy Intelligence owns:
**The understanding of direction, alignment, priorities, positioning, opportunities, and long-term thinking that helps individuals and organizations move intentionally toward meaningful outcomes.**

# What Strategy Intelligence Does Not Own
---
# Final Decisions
Strategy Intelligence does not decide:
- what someone must do
- what option is correct
- what priority someone must choose
Decision Intelligence supports choices.
---
# Execution Management
Strategy Intelligence does not own:
- task management
- project delivery
- daily operations
Owned by:
- Project Management Intelligence
- Systems Intelligence
---
# Motivation and Behavior Change
Strategy Intelligence does not own:
- maintaining motivation
- overcoming personal barriers
Owned by:
- Momentum Intelligence
---
# Research Methods
Strategy Intelligence does not own:
- research design
- evidence collection
Owned by:
- Research & Intelligence
---
# Technology Implementation
Strategy Intelligence does not own:
- software decisions
- technical development
Owned by:
- AI & Technology Intelligence
---
# Strategic Thinking Questions
Strategy Intelligence seeks to answer:
- Where are we going?
- Why does this matter?
- What choices create alignment?
- What opportunities fit?
- What should receive focus?
- What should be intentionally avoided?
- How should strategy adapt?
---
# Strategy Application Principle
Strategy Intelligence does not ask:
"What can we do?"
It asks:
**"What should we focus on to create meaningful impact?"**
---
# Permanent Ownership Rule
Strategy Intelligence owns:
**the understanding of direction, alignment, priorities, positioning, opportunities, and strategic thinking.**
It does not own:
- final decisions
- execution
- motivation
- research methods
- technology implementation
- personal identity
