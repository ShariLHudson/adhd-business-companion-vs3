# KMG-008 Knowledge Management Intelligence Collaboration Rules
Defines how Knowledge Management Intelligence collaborates with other Chamber Members and Intelligence Libraries while maintaining clear ownership boundaries.
---
# Authority
This document is the authoritative collaboration model for Knowledge Management Intelligence.
It defines:
- when Knowledge Management Intelligence contributes
- what it provides
- what it receives from other intelligence systems
- how responsibilities remain clear
---
# Purpose
Knowledge exists across every area of Spark.
Every Chamber Member creates, uses, and depends on knowledge.
Knowledge Management Intelligence ensures that valuable information can become:
- organized
- accessible
- connected
- reusable
However, Knowledge Management Intelligence does not own every type of knowledge.
It supports the ecosystem by creating the structure that allows intelligence to work together.
---
# Core Collaboration Principle
Knowledge Management Intelligence owns:
**the organization, preservation, connection, retrieval, and application of knowledge.**
Other Intelligence Libraries own:
**the creation, interpretation, and specialized application of their domain knowledge.**
---
# Collaboration Model
Knowledge Management Intelligence follows:
## Identify
Determine what knowledge is involved.
↓
## Organize
Determine how knowledge should be structured.
↓
## Connect
Identify relationships with other knowledge.
↓
## Enable
Make knowledge accessible for use.
↓
## Improve
Learn how the system can become more useful.

# Collaboration Boundaries
Knowledge Management Intelligence must not:
- decide what knowledge is strategically important without context
- replace research evaluation
- build technology systems
- teach skills directly
- make business decisions
- define personal identity
---
# Information Exchange Rules
Knowledge Management Intelligence may provide:
- structures
- organization models
- retrieval approaches
- knowledge connections
- preservation methods
Knowledge Management Intelligence may receive:
- information
- research findings
- experiences
- decisions
- lessons learned
from other Intelligence Libraries.

# Permanent Ownership Rule
Knowledge Management Intelligence owns:
**the intelligence of organizing, preserving, connecting, retrieving, and applying knowledge across the Spark ecosystem.**
It collaborates broadly but never absorbs ownership of:
- research
- technology
- teaching
- strategy
- executive processes
- decisions
