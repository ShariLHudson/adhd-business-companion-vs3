# KMG-003 Knowledge Management Intelligence Knowledge Ownership
Defines the knowledge domains, responsibilities, expertise areas, and boundaries owned by Knowledge Management Intelligence within the Spark Intelligence ecosystem.
---
# Authority
This document is the authoritative ownership definition for Knowledge Management Intelligence.
It establishes:
- what knowledge Knowledge Management Intelligence owns
- what problems it helps solve
- what areas belong to other Intelligence Libraries
It does not contain the complete expert ecosystem or framework library.
---
# Purpose
Modern individuals and organizations create enormous amounts of information.
The challenge is not simply creating knowledge.
The challenge is:
- capturing it
- organizing it
- finding it
- connecting it
- applying it
Knowledge Management Intelligence exists to transform information into a usable knowledge asset.
---
# Core Ownership Statement
Knowledge Management Intelligence owns:
**The systems, principles, and practices involved in capturing, organizing, connecting, preserving, retrieving, and applying knowledge.**

- educational techniques

Does not own:
- strategic direction
- business choices

---
# Data Analytics
Does not own:
- statistical analysis
- measurement systems

Data & Analytics Intelligence
---
# Executive Function Processes
Does not own:
- memory

Executive Function Intelligence

# Knowledge Management Intelligence Knowledge Questions
Knowledge Management Intelligence seeks to answer:
- What knowledge exists?
- Where does it belong?
- How is it connected?
- How can it be found?
- How can it be reused?
- How does knowledge become valuable?
---
# Knowledge Application Principle
Knowledge Management Intelligence does not ask:
"Where can we store this?"
It asks:
"How can this knowledge continue creating value?"
---
# Permanent Ownership Rule
Knowledge Management Intelligence owns:
**the organization, preservation, retrieval, connection, and application of knowledge.**
It does not own:
- research creation
- technology development
- teaching methods
- business decisions
- executive processes
