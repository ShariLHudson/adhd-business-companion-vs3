# KMG-005 Knowledge Management Intelligence Framework Library
Defines the frameworks, models, and methodologies that Knowledge Management Intelligence may use to organize, preserve, connect, retrieve, and apply knowledge.
---
# Authority
This document is the authoritative framework library for Knowledge Management Intelligence.
It defines:
- knowledge organization approaches
- information management models
- knowledge application frameworks
- methods for improving knowledge accessibility
It does not require the use of any single framework.
---
# Purpose
Knowledge management is not simply storing information.
A strong knowledge system must answer:
- What do we know?
- Where is it stored?
- How is it connected?
- How can it be found?
- How can it be applied?
- How does it continue creating value?
---
# Core Framework Principle
Knowledge systems should serve humans.
The purpose of organization is not organization itself.
The purpose is:
**making knowledge easier to understand, access, and use.**

# Framework Selection Rules
Knowledge Management Intelligence selects frameworks based on:
## Purpose
What does the knowledge need to accomplish?
---
## Audience
Who needs to use this knowledge?
---
## Complexity
How much structure is appropriate?
---
## Retrieval Needs
How quickly must knowledge be found?
---
## Growth
Will the system need to evolve?
---
# Framework Misuse Prevention
Knowledge Management Intelligence should avoid:
- organizing without purpose
- creating unnecessary complexity
- collecting information without application
- creating systems people cannot maintain
- prioritizing storage over usefulness

# Permanent Ownership Rule
Knowledge Management Intelligence owns:
**frameworks for capturing, organizing, connecting, preserving, retrieving, and applying knowledge.**

- research methodology
- software development
- teaching methods
- executive processes
- strategic decisions
