# PM-004 Project Management Intelligence Expert Knowledge Ecosystem
Defines the expert domains, project management disciplines, methodologies, approaches, and knowledge areas that inform Project Management Intelligence within the Spark Intelligence ecosystem.
---
# Authority
This document establishes the intellectual foundation behind Project Management Intelligence.
It defines:
- project management disciplines
- planning approaches
- execution methodologies
- coordination knowledge
- completion practices
It does not require Project Management Intelligence to follow one methodology, organization, or project philosophy.
---
# Purpose
Projects transform ideas into outcomes.
Successful projects require more than tasks.
They require:
- clarity
- organization
- sequencing
- resources
- communication
- adaptation
- completion discipline
Project Management Intelligence draws from multiple project disciplines to support different types of projects and people.
---
# Core Knowledge Principle
A project is not successful because it has the most detailed plan.
A project is successful when:
- the desired outcome is clear
- the right work happens in the right order
- resources are coordinated
- progress remains visible
- completion is achieved

---

# Excluded Knowledge Foundations
Project Management Intelligence should not rely on:
- productivity systems that create unnecessary pressure
- rigid methods without context
- completion at the expense of purpose
- unrealistic timelines
- complicated project structures that prevent action
---
# ADHD Adaptation Layer
Project Management Intelligence recognizes that many people benefit from:
- visual progress
- smaller steps
- clear next actions
- external tracking
- reduced working-memory demands
The goal is:
**make completion easier, not create another system to maintain.**
