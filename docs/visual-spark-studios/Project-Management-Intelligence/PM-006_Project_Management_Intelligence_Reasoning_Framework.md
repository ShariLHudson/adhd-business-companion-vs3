# PM-006 Project Management Intelligence Reasoning Framework
Defines how Project Management Intelligence analyzes goals, organizes work, creates execution pathways, manages progress, and supports completion within the Spark Intelligence ecosystem.
---
# Authority
This document is the authoritative reasoning framework for Project Management Intelligence.
It defines:
- how projects are understood
- how work is organized
- how progress is evaluated
- how completion is supported
It does not define:
- what projects should be chosen
- strategic priorities
- personal motivation
- technical implementation
---
# Purpose
Projects often fail not because people lack ability.
They fail because:
- outcomes are unclear
- work is not broken down
- priorities compete
- progress is invisible
- obstacles are discovered too late
Project Management Intelligence creates the pathway between:
**desired outcome → organized action → completed result**
---
# Core Reasoning Principle
Project Management Intelligence does not ask:
"How many tasks can we create?"
It asks:
**"What is the clearest path from the current reality to the desired completed outcome?"**
---
# Project Reasoning Model
Project Management Intelligence evaluates projects through ten reasoning stages.
---
# Stage 1 — Define the Outcome
## Question
What are we trying to accomplish?
---
Identify:
- desired result
- purpose
- success criteria
- completion definition
---
## Questions
- What does finished look like?
- Why does this matter?
- How will we know it is complete?
---
# Stage 2 — Understand the Current Reality
## Question
Where are we starting?
---
Evaluate:
- current progress
- available resources
- existing materials
- limitations
---
## Principle
A realistic plan begins with reality, not assumptions.
---
# Stage 3 — Define Project Scope
## Question
What belongs in this project?
---
Identify:
## Included
Work required for completion.
---
## Excluded
Work that may be valuable but belongs elsewhere.
---
## Purpose
Prevent unnecessary expansion.
---
# Stage 4 — Identify Deliverables
## Question
What tangible outcomes must be created?
---
Examples:
- documents
- products
- decisions
- completed tasks
- experiences
---
## Reasoning
Deliverables create visibility.
---
# Stage 5 — Break Down the Work
## Question
What actions create the outcome?
---
Transform:
Large Goal
↓
Major Components
↓
Milestones
↓
Tasks
↓
Next Action
---
## Principle
A project becomes manageable when the path becomes visible.
---
# Stage 6 — Sequence the Work
## Question
What order makes sense?
---
Consider:
- dependencies
- required preparation
- logical flow
- timing
---
## Questions
- What must happen first?
- What can happen simultaneously?
- What is blocking progress?
---
# Stage 7 — Assess Resources
## Question
What is required for completion?
---
Evaluate:
- time
- people
- money
- tools
- information
- energy
---
## Principle
Plans must match available capacity.
---
# Stage 8 — Identify Risks and Obstacles
## Question
What could prevent completion?
---
Consider:
- missing resources
- competing priorities
- uncertainty
- dependencies
- changing conditions
---
## Response
Prepare adjustments before problems become crises.
---
# Stage 9 — Manage Progress
## Question
How do we know movement is happening?
---
Track:
- completed work
- current focus
- blockers
- upcoming actions
---
## Principle
Visible progress creates awareness and momentum.
---
# Stage 10 — Complete and Learn
## Question
How do we close the project?
---
Confirm:
- outcome achieved
- remaining work resolved
- lessons captured
- next steps identified
---
# Project Health Assessment Model
Project Management Intelligence evaluates projects through seven dimensions.
---
# 1. Clarity
Is the desired outcome understood?
---
# 2. Organization
Are the steps visible?
---
# 3. Priority
Is the most important work receiving attention?
---
# 4. Progress
Can movement be seen?
---
# 5. Resources
Are requirements realistic?
---
# 6. Adaptability
Can the project adjust when needed?
---
# 7. Completion
Is the outcome actually finished?
---
# Project Failure Pattern Detection
Project Management Intelligence watches for:
---
# Starting Without Clarity
Pattern:
Beginning work before defining the outcome.
---
# Too Many Active Projects
Pattern:
Progress becomes scattered.
---
# Hidden Complexity
Pattern:
A project appears simple but contains many unknown steps.
---
# No Visible Next Action
Pattern:
People know the goal but not what to do next.
---
# Planning Instead of Doing
Pattern:
The plan becomes the work instead of supporting the work.
---
# No Completion Definition
Pattern:
The project continues indefinitely.
---
# ADHD-Aware Project Reasoning
Project Management Intelligence recognizes that some people experience:
- difficulty estimating project size
- excitement at starting
- challenges with follow-through
- losing track of steps
- overwhelm from complexity
- difficulty seeing progress
Support should create:
- smaller steps
- visible movement
- clear next actions
- external tracking
- realistic pacing
The goal:
**make completion easier without creating unnecessary pressure.**

# Collaboration Reasoning
---
# Strategy Intelligence
Strategy asks:
"Why are we doing this?"
Project Management asks:
"How do we organize completion?"
---
# Decision Intelligence
Decision asks:
"Should we do this?"
Project Management asks:
"How do we execute this choice?"
---
# Systems Intelligence
Systems asks:
"How should things work?"
Project Management asks:
"How do we complete this specific outcome?"
---
# Momentum Intelligence
Momentum asks:
"How do we keep moving?"
Project Management asks:
"What movement needs to happen next?"
---
# Executive Function Intelligence
Executive Function asks:
"What support does the brain need?"
Project Management asks:
"How can the work be structured externally?"
---
# Example
User:
"I want to create an online course but I feel overwhelmed."
Project Management Intelligence reasons:
## Define Outcome
What does the finished course include?
## Identify Deliverables
- lessons
- materials
- platform setup
- launch plan
## Break Down Work
Create manageable phases.
## Sequence
Determine what must happen first.
## Manage Progress
Track milestones.
Collaboration:
- Founder → business purpose
- Strategy → priorities
- Systems → course delivery structure
- Momentum → continued progress

- systems design
- technology implementation

- personal identity
