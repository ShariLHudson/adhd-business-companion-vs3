# DEC-003 Decision Intelligence Knowledge Ownership
Defines the knowledge domains, responsibilities, expertise areas, and boundaries owned by Decision Intelligence within the Spark Intelligence ecosystem.
---
# Authority
This document is the authoritative ownership definition for Decision Intelligence.
It establishes:
- what knowledge Decision Intelligence owns
- what decision challenges it helps solve
- what areas belong to other Intelligence Libraries
It does not contain the complete expert ecosystem or framework library.
---
# Purpose
Every person and organization makes decisions constantly.
Some decisions are simple.
Others involve:
- uncertainty
- competing priorities
- limited resources
- emotional factors
- incomplete information
- significant consequences
Decision Intelligence exists to improve the quality of thinking that happens before choices are made.
---
# Core Ownership Statement
Decision Intelligence owns:
**The knowledge, methods, and principles involved in clarifying choices, evaluating options, understanding tradeoffs, managing uncertainty, and improving decision quality.**
---
# Primary Knowledge Domains
---
# 1. Decision-Making Fundamentals
## Purpose
Understand the foundations of how choices are made.
---
## Knowledge Areas
Includes:
- decision processes
- choice structures
- reasoning approaches
- decision quality
- judgment improvement
---
## Questions
- What decision is actually being made?
- What factors matter?
- How should the choice be approached?
---

## Purpose
Identify the real decision beneath confusion.
---

Includes:

- What outcome matters most?
- What problem are we solving?

# What Decision Intelligence Does Not Own
---
# Final Choice Authority
Decision Intelligence does not decide for:
- individuals
- leaders
- organizations
---
# Prediction
Decision Intelligence does not:
- guarantee outcomes
- predict the future
- claim certainty
---
# Personal Values
Decision Intelligence does not define:
- beliefs
- priorities
- meaning
---
# Specialized Expertise
Decision Intelligence does not replace:
- legal expertise
- medical expertise
- financial expertise
- technical expertise
- business expertise
---
# Strategy Creation
Decision Intelligence does not own:
- organizational direction
- vision creation
Owned by:
Strategy Intelligence.
---
# Decision Intelligence Questions
Decision Intelligence seeks to answer:
- What choice are we making?
- What outcomes matter?
- What options exist?
- What information do we need?
- What are the tradeoffs?
- What assumptions are influencing us?
- How can we decide intentionally?
---
# Decision Application Principle
Decision Intelligence does not ask:
"What is the perfect choice?"
It asks:
**"What choice best fits the available information, desired outcome, and current reality?"**
---
# Permanent Ownership Rule
Decision Intelligence owns:
**the understanding and improvement of decision-making processes, option evaluation, tradeoffs, uncertainty management, and choice clarity.**
It does not own:
- final decisions
- strategy
- research methods
- personal identity
- execution systems
- specialized expertise
