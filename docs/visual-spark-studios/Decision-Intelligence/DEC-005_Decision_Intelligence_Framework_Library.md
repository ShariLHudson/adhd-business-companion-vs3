# DEC-005 Decision Intelligence Framework Library
Defines the decision frameworks, models, and methodologies that Decision Intelligence may use to clarify choices, evaluate options, understand tradeoffs, and support intentional decisions.
---
# Authority
This document is the authoritative framework library for Decision Intelligence.
It defines:
- decision-making models
- evaluation approaches
- comparison methods
- uncertainty frameworks
- reflection methods
It does not determine the final choice.
The purpose of frameworks is to improve thinking quality, not replace human judgment.
---
# Purpose
Decision frameworks exist to create clarity when choices become difficult.
The goal is not to find a perfect answer.
The goal is to create a thoughtful decision process.
---
# Core Framework Principle
Decision Intelligence follows:
**Clarify → Explore → Evaluate → Choose → Learn**
Not:
**Rush → Guess → Regret**
---
# Framework Category 1 — Decision Clarity Frameworks
## Purpose
Define what decision is actually being made.
---
## Core Questions
- What choice needs to be made?
- Why does this decision matter?
- What outcome is desired?
- What constraints exist?
---
## Common Problems Addressed
- unclear decisions
- solving the wrong problem
- reacting instead of choosing
---
# Framework Category 2 — Decision Statement Framework
## Purpose
Create a clear decision question.
---
## Structure
"I need to decide whether/how/which ______ because ______."
---
## Example
Instead of:
"Should I do this?"
Clarify:
"Should I invest my limited time and money into this opportunity because it supports my larger goal?"
---
# Framework Category 3 — Options Generation Framework
## Purpose
Avoid limiting decisions to the first obvious choice.
---
## Consider:
- obvious options
- alternatives
- smaller experiments
- delaying the decision
- combining approaches
---
## Questions
- What else could we do?
- Are we missing a possibility?
- Are we assuming only two choices exist?
---
# Framework Category 4 — Pros and Cons Analysis
## Purpose
Compare advantages and disadvantages.
---
## Evaluate:
## Benefits
What could improve?
---
## Costs
What resources are required?
---
## Risks
What could go wrong?
---
## Limitations
What challenges exist?
---
# Framework Category 5 — Decision Matrix
## Purpose
Compare options using selected criteria.
---
## Possible Criteria
- importance
- cost
- effort
- alignment
- risk
- timing
- potential value
---
## Process
1. Identify options.
2. Identify criteria.
3. Compare options.
4. Review results.
---
# Framework Category 6 — Weighted Decision Matrix
## Purpose
Evaluate choices when some factors matter more than others.
---
## Process
1. Identify criteria.
2. Assign importance.
3. Score options.
4. Compare results.
---
## Important Rule
Numbers support thinking.
They do not create certainty.
---
# Framework Category 7 — Cost-Benefit Analysis
## Purpose
Understand whether potential value justifies resources required.
---
## Consider:
Benefits:
- financial
- personal
- strategic
- practical
Costs:
- money
- time
- energy
- opportunity cost
---
# Framework Category 8 — Opportunity Cost Framework
## Purpose
Understand what is sacrificed by choosing one option.
---
## Questions
- If I choose this, what am I not choosing?
- What important opportunity may be delayed?
- Is this the best use of limited resources?
---
# Framework Category 9 — Risk Assessment Framework
## Purpose
Understand possible negative outcomes.
---
## Evaluate:
## Probability
How likely is this?
---
## Impact
How significant would it be?
---
## Response
What could reduce the risk?
---
# Framework Category 10 — Reversible vs. Irreversible Decisions
## Purpose
Determine how much evaluation is needed.
---
# Reversible Decisions
Can easily be changed.
Examples:
- testing a tool
- trying a small experiment
---
# Irreversible Decisions
Difficult or costly to change.
Examples:
- major investments
- permanent commitments
---
## Application
Spend more time evaluating high-impact irreversible choices.
---
# Framework Category 11 — Values Alignment Framework
## Purpose
Ensure decisions reflect what matters.
---
## Consider:
- priorities
- beliefs
- desired outcomes
- personal commitments
---
## Questions
- Does this fit who I want to become?
- Does this support what matters most?
---
# Framework Category 12 — Future Self Framework
## Purpose
Consider long-term consequences.
---
## Questions
- How will I feel about this later?
- Will this support future goals?
- What would future me appreciate?
---
# Framework Category 13 — Scenario Analysis Framework
## Purpose
Explore possible outcomes.
---
## Consider:
## Best Case
What could go well?
---
## Expected Case
What is realistic?
---
## Challenging Case
What could create difficulty?
---
# Framework Category 14 — Regret Minimization Framework
## Purpose
Consider future satisfaction with a decision.
---
## Questions
- Which choice would I regret avoiding?
- Which risk matters more?
- What lesson would I want to gain?
---
# Framework Category 15 — Second-Order Thinking Framework
## Purpose
Consider consequences beyond the immediate result.
---
## Questions
- What happens next?
- What unintended effects could occur?
- How might this affect other areas?
---
# Framework Category 16 — Decision Journal Framework
## Purpose
Improve future decisions through reflection.
---
## Capture:
- decision made
- reasoning
- assumptions
- expected outcome
- actual outcome
- lessons learned
---
# Framework Category 17 — "Good Enough Decision" Framework
## Purpose
Avoid perfectionism and endless analysis.
---
## Consider:
- Is enough information available?
- Is additional research likely to change the decision?
- What level of certainty is realistic?
---
# Framework Category 18 — Intuition Integration Framework
## Purpose
Consider experience and instinct appropriately.
---
## Important Principle
Intuition can provide useful information.
It should be considered alongside:
- evidence
- goals
- reasoning
- context
---
# Framework Category 19 — Group Decision Frameworks
## Purpose
Support decisions involving multiple people.
---
## Consider:
- perspectives
- stakeholder needs
- communication
- shared understanding
---
# Framework Category 20 — Decision Review Framework
## Purpose
Learn after decisions are made.
---
## Questions
- What happened?
- What did we learn?
- What would we repeat?
- What would we change?
---
# Framework Selection Rules
Decision Intelligence selects frameworks based on:
---
# Decision Importance
Small decisions require less structure.
Major decisions require deeper evaluation.
---
# Time Available
Urgent decisions require different approaches than long-term decisions.
---
# Uncertainty Level
More uncertainty requires:
- assumptions
- scenarios
- risk evaluation
---
# Number of Options
More choices may require:
- comparison frameworks
- prioritization methods
---
# Emotional Impact
High emotional decisions may require:
- reflection
- values clarification
- additional perspective
---
# Framework Misuse Prevention
Decision Intelligence should avoid:
- creating analysis paralysis
- pretending frameworks guarantee outcomes
- removing human judgment
- ignoring emotions
- ignoring evidence
- making choices for users
---
