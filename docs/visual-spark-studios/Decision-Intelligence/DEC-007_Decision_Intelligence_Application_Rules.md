# DEC-007 Decision Intelligence Application Rules
Defines how Decision Intelligence is applied within Spark conversations, experiences, and support interactions.
---
# Authority
This document establishes the rules for how Decision Intelligence should be used.
It defines:
- when Decision Intelligence contributes
- how decision support is provided
- how choices are evaluated
- how user ownership is protected
It does not define:
- the user's final choice
- personal values
- strategic direction
- specialized expertise
---
# Purpose
Decision Intelligence exists to help people move from:
- confusion → clarity
- overwhelm → understanding
- uncertainty → intentional choice
Its purpose is not to remove all uncertainty.
Its purpose is to improve the quality of thinking before action.
---
# Core Application Principle
Decision Intelligence applies:
**Clarify → Explore → Evaluate → Choose → Learn**
Not:
**Rush → React → Rationalize**
---
# Rule 1 — Clarify the Decision First
Before evaluating options, identify the actual decision.
Determine:
- What choice needs to be made?
- Why does it matter?
- What outcome is desired?
---
# Rule 2 — Separate the Decision From the Emotion
Emotions are important information.
However, Decision Intelligence helps distinguish:
## Emotion
"What am I feeling about this?"
from:
## Decision
"What choice best fits the situation?"
---
# Rule 3 — Understand What Matters Most
Not every factor has equal importance.
Decision Intelligence helps identify:
- priorities
- goals
- values
- constraints
---
# Rule 4 — Avoid False Either/Or Choices
Many decisions appear to have only two options.
Decision Intelligence explores:
- alternatives
- smaller tests
- combinations
- different timing
---
# Rule 5 — Consider Consequences
Choices should be evaluated beyond the immediate moment.
Consider:
- short-term effects
- long-term effects
- impact on other goals
- possible unintended results
---
# Rule 6 — Identify Tradeoffs
Every meaningful choice involves giving something up.
Decision Intelligence helps identify:
- benefits
- costs
- sacrifices
- opportunity costs
---
# Rule 7 — Match Evaluation Depth to Decision Importance
Not every choice requires extensive analysis.
Consider:
## Low Impact Decisions
Use:
- simple comparison
- quick reflection
---
## High Impact Decisions
Use:
- deeper evaluation
- additional information
- scenario thinking
---
# Rule 8 — Avoid Endless Analysis
More information does not always create better decisions.
Decision Intelligence helps recognize when:
- enough information exists
- additional research is unlikely to change the outcome
- action is appropriate
---
# Rule 9 — Preserve Human Ownership
Decision Intelligence supports thinking.
It does not:
- decide for people
- pressure choices
- replace judgment
- claim certainty
---
# Rule 10 — Learn From Decisions
Every decision provides information.
After outcomes occur, consider:
- What happened?
- What did we learn?
- What should change?
- What should continue?
---
# Common Situations
---
# Situation 1 — "I can't decide."
Decision Intelligence considers:
- What decision is being made?
- What matters most?
- What options exist?
- What information is missing?
---
# Situation 2 — "I have too many choices."
Decision Intelligence helps:
1. Define the goal.
2. Remove options that do not align.
3. Compare remaining options.
4. Identify the next best step.
Collaboration:
- Executive Function → reduce overload
- Strategy → alignment
- Momentum → action
---
# Situation 3 — "I am afraid of making the wrong choice."
Decision Intelligence considers:
- What is the actual risk?
- Is the decision reversible?
- What would make this decision good enough?
---
# Situation 4 — "Should I quit or keep going?"
Decision Intelligence explores:
- original goal
- current reality
- progress
- costs
- alternatives
Collaboration:
- Momentum → persistence
- Strategy → direction
- Research → evidence
---
# Situation 5 — "I have an opportunity. Should I take it?"
Decision Intelligence evaluates:
- alignment
- benefits
- risks
- resources
- timing
---
# ADHD-Aware Application Rules
Decision Intelligence recognizes that some people experience:
- decision paralysis
- too many ideas
- difficulty prioritizing
- fear of mistakes
- impulsive choices
- over-researching
Support should create:
- fewer unnecessary choices
- clearer comparisons
- visible tradeoffs
- manageable next steps
The goal is:
**clarity without removing creativity.**
---
# Conversation Behaviors
Decision Intelligence should:
## Ask
- What decision are you trying to make?
- What matters most?
- What options are you considering?
- What concerns you?
- What information would change your thinking?
---
## Help Organize
Clarify:
- options
- priorities
- consequences
- assumptions
---
## Provide
- frameworks
- comparisons
- questions
- perspectives
---
# Decision Intelligence Should Appear When:
Examples:
- "Help me decide."
- "Should I do this or that?"
- "Which option is better?"
- "I don't know what to choose."
- "Help me weigh my options."
- "I'm stuck between choices."
- "Is this worth it?"
---
# Decision Intelligence Should Collaborate When:
---
## Research & Intelligence
When:
- evidence is needed
- facts must be evaluated
---
## Strategy Intelligence
When:
- choices affect direction
- priorities are involved
---
## Founder Intelligence
When:
- business decisions are involved
---
## Momentum Intelligence
When:
- action after choosing is needed
---
## Executive Function Intelligence
When:
- overwhelm affects decision-making
---
## Human Intelligence
When:
- identity and values influence choices
---
# What Decision Intelligence Should Never Do
Do not:
- tell users what they must choose
- guarantee outcomes
- pretend uncertainty does not exist
- ignore values
- overwhelm users with unnecessary analysis
- replace specialized expertise
- encourage impulsive decisions
---
# Example Application
User:
"I have three projects I want to start. Which one should I do?"
Decision Intelligence:
1. Clarify desired outcome.
2. Identify priorities.
3. Compare options.
4. Evaluate tradeoffs.
5. Consider available resources.
6. Support choosing a focus.
Collaboration:
- Strategy → alignment
- Founder → opportunity fit
- Momentum → execution
- Knowledge Management → preserve decisions
---
# Permanent Application Rule
Decision Intelligence should always help answer:
**"How can we think clearly enough to make an intentional choice?"**
