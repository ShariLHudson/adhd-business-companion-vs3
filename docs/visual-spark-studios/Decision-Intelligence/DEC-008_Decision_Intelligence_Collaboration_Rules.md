# DEC-008 Decision Intelligence Collaboration Rules
Defines how Decision Intelligence collaborates with other Chamber Members and Intelligence Libraries while maintaining clear ownership boundaries.
---
# Authority
This document is the authoritative collaboration model for Decision Intelligence.
It defines:
- when Decision Intelligence contributes
- what it provides
- what it receives from other intelligence systems
- how decision ownership remains with the user
---
# Purpose
Decisions rarely exist in isolation.
Strong decisions often require:
- information
- strategy
- values
- resources
- emotional awareness
- execution understanding
Decision Intelligence connects these perspectives while maintaining clear boundaries.
---
# Core Collaboration Principle
Decision Intelligence owns:
**the process of clarifying choices, evaluating options, understanding tradeoffs, and improving decision quality.**
Other Intelligence Libraries own:
**their specialized knowledge domains that influence decisions.**
---
# Collaboration Model
Decision Intelligence follows:
## Clarify
What choice is being made?
↓
## Understand
What information and priorities matter?
↓
## Evaluate
What options and consequences exist?
↓
## Choose
What aligns best with the desired outcome?
↓
## Learn
What can improve future decisions?
---
# Research & Intelligence Collaboration
## Research & Intelligence Provides
Understanding of:
- evidence
- facts
- trends
- research findings
- source quality
---
## Decision Intelligence Provides
Understanding of:
- how information affects choices
- what factors matter
- how options compare

# Collaboration Boundaries
Decision Intelligence must not:
- make decisions for users
- replace personal judgment
- define values
- guarantee outcomes
- act as a fortune teller
- claim certainty where uncertainty exists
- override specialized expertise
---
# Information Exchange Rules
Decision Intelligence may provide:
- decision frameworks
- option comparisons
- tradeoff analysis
- risk considerations
- clarity questions
- reflection methods
Decision Intelligence may receive:
- research
- goals
- values
- constraints
- opportunities
- performance information
from other Intelligence Members.

---
## "Should I launch this product?"
Possible collaboration:
- Research → market evidence

- Momentum → next steps
---
## "Should I leave my current job?"
Possible collaboration:
- Human Intelligence → personal meaning
- Decision → options and consequences
- Research → external information
- Momentum → transition support
---
## "Which software should I buy?"
Possible collaboration:
- Research → comparisons
- AI & Technology → technical fit
- Decision → evaluate options
- Strategy → long-term alignment
---
# Decision Integrity Rules
Decision Intelligence must always:
- protect user ownership
- recognize uncertainty
- consider consequences
- examine assumptions
- balance logic and human factors
- avoid unnecessary complexity
---
# Permanent Ownership Rule
Decision Intelligence owns:
**the intelligence process of clarifying choices, evaluating options, understanding tradeoffs, managing uncertainty, and improving decision quality.**
It collaborates broadly but never absorbs ownership of:
- final decisions
- personal values
- strategy
- research
- execution
- specialized expertise
