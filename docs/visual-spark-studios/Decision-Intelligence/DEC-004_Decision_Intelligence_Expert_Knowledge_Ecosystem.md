# DEC-004 Decision Intelligence Expert Knowledge Ecosystem
Defines the expert domains, decision sciences, methodologies, research traditions, and knowledge areas that inform Decision Intelligence within the Spark Intelligence ecosystem.
---
# Authority
This document establishes the intellectual foundation behind Decision Intelligence.
It defines:
- decision science disciplines
- expert categories
- decision-making traditions
- evaluation approaches
- knowledge domains
It does not require Decision Intelligence to follow any single researcher, framework, or decision model.
---
# Purpose
Decision-making is a complex human activity influenced by:
- information
- goals
- emotions
- values
- uncertainty
- consequences
- available resources
Decision Intelligence draws from multiple disciplines to help people make thoughtful choices.
---
# Core Knowledge Principle
A good decision is not defined only by the outcome.
A good decision is created through:
- clear understanding
- thoughtful evaluation
- awareness of uncertainty
- alignment with priorities

# Decision Quality Standards
Decision Intelligence evaluates decisions through:
---
# Clarity
Is the actual decision understood?
---
# Alignment
Does it fit goals and priorities?
---
# Evidence
Is available information considered?
---
# Alternatives
Were meaningful options explored?
---
# Consequences
Were outcomes considered?
---
# Ownership
Does the person making the decision understand and accept the choice?
---
# Excluded Knowledge Foundations
Decision Intelligence should not rely on:
- fortune telling
- predictions presented as certainty
- mind reading
- supernatural decision guidance
- manipulation techniques
- claims of guaranteed outcomes
---
# Important Distinction
Decision Intelligence may consider:
- emotions
- intuition
- experience
- personal preferences
However, these are evaluated as part of decision context, not as guaranteed sources of truth.
---
# ADHD Adaptation Layer
Decision Intelligence should recognize that some people experience:
- decision paralysis
- too many options
- difficulty comparing choices
- fear of making mistakes
- impulsive choices
Support should help transform:
overwhelm → clarity → intentional choice
