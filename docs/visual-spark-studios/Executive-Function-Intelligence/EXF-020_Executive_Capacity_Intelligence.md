# EXF-020 Executive Capacity Intelligence
Defines Executive Capacity as an executive function concept and establishes how Executive Function Intelligence understands the available ability to manage cognitive demands, purposeful action, and adaptive functioning at a given moment.
---

Executive Capacity is owned exclusively by Executive Function Intelligence.
Other Intelligence Libraries may reference Executive Capacity but must not redefine it.
Momentum Intelligence may consider available capacity when determining appropriate movement strategies, but Momentum does not own Executive Capacity.

# Purpose
People do not have identical executive resources available at all times.
Capacity changes.
A person may function differently depending upon:
- energy
- stress
- health
- emotional state
- cognitive demands
- environment
- recovery
Executive Capacity Intelligence exists to understand the amount of executive resource currently available for meaningful action.
It answers the question:
**"How much executive ability is available right now to manage current demands?"**

# Executive Capacity Defined
Executive Capacity refers to the currently available ability to use executive functions effectively within present conditions.
Capacity influences how easily a person can:
- organize information
- regulate attention
- initiate action
- manage emotions
- make adjustments
- sustain effort
Executive Capacity is dynamic.
It changes based on internal and external conditions.
---
# Guiding Principles
## Capacity Changes Throughout Life
Executive functioning is not a fixed resource.
Daily circumstances influence available capacity.
---
## Capacity Is Different From Capability
A person may have the ability to do something while having reduced capacity to do it at a particular moment.
Capability reflects potential.
Capacity reflects current availability.
---
## Capacity Should Influence Expectations
Effective support considers current resources.
Expectations should reflect reality rather than ideal conditions.
---
## Protecting Capacity Supports Sustainability
Using executive resources wisely helps preserve future ability.
---
## Reduced Capacity Is Not Reduced Potential
Temporary difficulty does not define a person's ability.
---
## Capacity Can Be Supported
Support can increase effective functioning by reducing unnecessary demand.
Examples:
- simplifying tasks
- reducing interruptions
- creating structure
- providing clarity
- allowing recovery
