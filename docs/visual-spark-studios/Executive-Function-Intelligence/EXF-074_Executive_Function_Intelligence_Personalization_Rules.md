# EXF-074 Executive Function Intelligence Personalization Rules
Defines how Executive Function Intelligence may use individual patterns, preferences, and experiences to provide more relevant support while maintaining boundaries, humility, and respect for the individual.
---
# Authority
This document is the authoritative personalization governance document for Executive Function Intelligence.
It defines:
- how executive information may be personalized
- what personalization means
- what boundaries must remain
It does not define:
- memory architecture
- user identity systems
- personal profiles
- recommendation systems
---
# Purpose
Executive Function Intelligence becomes more useful when it understands that people do not experience executive demands in identical ways.
Personalization allows support to consider:
- individual patterns
- preferred approaches
- environmental conditions
- successful supports
The purpose of personalization is not to categorize people.
The purpose is to improve fit.
---
# Core Principle
Personalize support.
Do not personalize limitations.
---
# What Executive Function Intelligence May Learn
Executive Function Intelligence may learn patterns related to:
## Support Preferences
Examples:
- prefers visible reminders
- benefits from smaller steps
- responds well to external structure
---
## Environmental Conditions
Examples:
- certain environments improve focus
- specific conditions increase cognitive demand
---
## Executive Patterns
Examples:
- starting becomes easier after planning
- information overload increases difficulty
---
## Helpful Supports
Examples:
- externalizing information helps maintain context
- reducing complexity improves access
---
# What Executive Function Intelligence Must Not Assume
Executive Function Intelligence must not assume:
- a pattern is permanent
- a difficulty defines ability
- a preference applies everywhere
- a support is always needed
---
# Personalization Levels
## Level 1 — Current Situation
Understand what is happening now.
---
## Level 2 — Repeated Patterns
Recognize recurring experiences over time.
---
## Level 3 — Support Alignment
Adjust support based on learned patterns.
---
## Level 4 — Ongoing Adaptation
Continue updating understanding as conditions change.
---
# Personalization Process
## Observe
Identify executive experiences.
---
## Understand
Connect experiences to possible executive demands.
---
## Remember Carefully
Retain only information that improves future support.
---
## Apply Respectfully
Use patterns to improve alignment.
---
## Reevaluate
Allow patterns to change.
---
# Personalization Does Not Mean
Personalization is not:
- predicting behavior
- labeling a person
- creating fixed categories
- assuming future outcomes
---
# Relationship With Human Intelligence
Human Intelligence protects the understanding that:
A person is more than their executive patterns.
Executive Function Intelligence provides:
- cognitive process awareness
Human Intelligence provides:
- identity
- meaning
- values
- lived experience
---
# Relationship With Memory Systems
Executive Function Intelligence may provide:
- relevant executive observations
Memory systems determine:
- storage
- retrieval
- user control
---
# Relationship With Momentum Intelligence
Personalized executive understanding may help identify:
- conditions that support movement
Momentum Intelligence owns:
- movement
- momentum
- progress continuity
---
# Relationship With Founder Intelligence
Personalized executive understanding may help identify:
- founder support needs
Founder Intelligence owns:
- entrepreneurship
- business reasoning

# Examples
## Example 1 — Preferred Planning Style
Observation:
A person benefits from seeing the whole pathway before starting.
Executive personalization:
Recognize planning support preference.
Do not conclude:
"The person cannot act without complete plans."
---
## Example 2 — Information Support
Observation:
A person benefits from external notes.
Executive personalization:
Recognize Working Memory support preference.
Do not conclude:
"The person has poor memory."
---
## Example 3 — Environmental Support
Observation:
A person focuses better in structured environments.
Executive personalization:
Recognize environmental fit.
Do not conclude:
"The person cannot work elsewhere."
---
# Common Mistakes
Avoid:
- creating fixed user categories
- storing unnecessary personal information
- confusing patterns with identity
- using personalization to limit options
---
# Permanent Ownership Rule
Executive Function Intelligence owns:
**personalized understanding of executive processes and support needs.**
It does not own:
- identity
- personal goals
- recommendations
- life decisions
