Defines Cognitive Flexibility as an authoritative Executive Function concept and establishes how Executive Function Intelligence understands the process of adapting thinking, approaches, and responses when conditions change.

# Purpose
Life rarely follows a perfectly predictable path.
People regularly encounter:
- changing information
- unexpected events
- new requirements
- different perspectives
- obstacles
Cognitive Flexibility Intelligence exists to understand the executive process involved in adjusting thoughts, strategies, and actions when circumstances change.
It answers the question:
**"How does a person adapt when reality does not match the original expectation?"**

# Cognitive Flexibility Defined
Cognitive Flexibility is the executive process of adapting thinking, perspective, or approach in response to changing information, circumstances, or demands.
It involves:
- recognizing change
- considering alternatives
- adjusting strategies
- updating understanding
---
# Cognitive Flexibility Components
## Recognizing Change
A person must notice when:
- conditions have shifted
- information has changed
- the current approach is not working
---
## Considering Alternatives
Cognitive Flexibility allows exploration of:
- different approaches
- new possibilities
- alternative explanations
---
## Updating Thinking
New information can modify previous assumptions.
---
## Adjusting Behavior
Cognitive Flexibility supports changing actions when needed.
---
## Integrating Feedback
Feedback provides information for adaptation.
---
# Cognitive Flexibility and Executive Function
Cognitive Flexibility interacts with multiple executive processes.
## Planning
Plans require adjustment when conditions change.
---
## Self-Monitoring
Self-Monitoring identifies when adaptation may be needed.
---
## Emotional Self-Regulation
Emotional responses can influence the ability to adjust.
---
## Decision Making
Decisions may require reconsidering information.
---
## Transitions
Changing situations often require cognitive shifts.
---
# Cognitive Flexibility Does Not Mean
Cognitive Flexibility is not:
- changing opinions constantly
- lacking commitment
- avoiding decisions
- accepting every alternative
Flexibility involves purposeful adaptation.
---
# Common Cognitive Flexibility Challenges
## Unexpected Changes
Sudden changes may require additional adjustment.
---
## Strong Existing Plans
Changing direction may require releasing previous expectations.
---
## Emotional Attachment
Emotions may make alternative thinking more difficult.
---
## Too Many Possibilities
Excess options may create confusion.
---
## New Information
Updating understanding requires cognitive adjustment.

# Cognitive Flexibility Support Principles
Support may include:
- identifying what changed
- separating facts from assumptions
- considering alternatives
- allowing adjustment time
- creating safe opportunities to revise approaches
The goal is adaptive thinking.
