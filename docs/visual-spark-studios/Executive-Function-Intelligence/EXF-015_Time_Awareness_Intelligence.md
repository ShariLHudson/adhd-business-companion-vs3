Time influences nearly every aspect of meaningful action.

- deadlines

# Time Awareness Defined
Time Awareness is the executive function process of recognizing the passage of time, estimating duration, understanding timing relationships, and using time information to guide action.
It involves:
- estimating how long activities require
- recognizing available time
- understanding deadlines
- sequencing activities across time
- adjusting expectations based on time realities
Time Awareness connects present action with future outcomes.
---
# Guiding Principles
## Time Is Experienced Differently
People may perceive and interact with time differently.
Executive Function Intelligence recognizes variation rather than assuming one universal experience.
---
## Time Awareness Influences Planning
Understanding time helps people prepare realistic future action.
---
## Time Awareness Requires Context
Time perception and management can be influenced by:
- familiarity
- interest
- attention
- stress
- interruptions
- complexity
- environment
---
## Time Awareness Is Not a Character Trait
Difficulty estimating or managing time does not indicate:
- lack of responsibility
- lack of commitment
- lack of intelligence
It represents an executive process that can require support.
---
## External Time Supports Are Valuable
People often use external systems to strengthen Time Awareness.
Examples include:
- calendars
- reminders
- visual timelines
- timers
- schedules
External supports reduce reliance on internal estimation alone.
---
## Time Awareness Requires Flexibility
Real life changes.
Effective time awareness includes adjusting when:
- priorities change
- unexpected events occur
- capacity changes
- assumptions prove inaccurate
