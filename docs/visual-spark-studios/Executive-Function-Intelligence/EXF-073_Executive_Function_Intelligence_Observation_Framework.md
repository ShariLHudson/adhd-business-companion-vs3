# EXF-073 Executive Function Intelligence Observation Framework
Defines how Executive Function Intelligence observes executive experiences, patterns, demands, and support effectiveness without diagnosing, labeling, or reducing the individual.
---
# Authority
This document is the authoritative observation framework for Executive Function Intelligence.
It defines:
- what Executive Function Intelligence observes
- how observations should be interpreted
- what conclusions should not be made
It does not define:
- diagnosis
- personality
- identity
- personal evaluation
---
# Purpose
Executive Function Intelligence becomes more useful when it can understand patterns over time.
However, observation must remain separate from judgment.
The purpose of observation is to understand:
- executive demands
- environmental conditions
- support effectiveness
- changing patterns
The purpose is not to categorize people.
---
# Core Principle
Observe processes.
Do not label people.
---
# What Executive Function Intelligence Observes
## Executive Demands
Executive Function Intelligence may observe:
- complexity
- information requirements
- attention requirements
- planning requirements
- transition requirements
---
## Conditions
Executive Function Intelligence may observe:
- environment
- available support
- cognitive demand
- current capacity
---
## Patterns
Executive Function Intelligence may observe:
- repeated experiences
- successful conditions
- recurring friction points
---
## Support Effectiveness
Executive Function Intelligence may observe:
- what reduces demand
- what improves access
- what creates unnecessary burden
---
# What Executive Function Intelligence Does Not Observe as Identity
Executive Function Intelligence does not define:
- intelligence
- worth
- character
- effort
- potential
---
# Observation Categories
## Initiation Observations
Questions:
- What influences starting?
- Is the first step clear?
- What reduces beginning friction?
---
## Planning Observations
Questions:
- Is the pathway visible?
- Are future requirements understood?
---
## Working Memory Observations
Questions:
- What information must be actively maintained?
- What external supports reduce burden?
---
## Attention Observations
Questions:
- What affects focus direction?
- What creates competing demands?
---
## Transition Observations
Questions:
- What supports movement between states?
- Where does shifting require additional effort?
---
## Regulation Observations
Questions:
- What conditions influence response management?
- What supports recovery?
---
# Observation Process
## Step 1 — Notice
Identify the experience.
---
## Step 2 — Describe
Describe the executive demand without judgment.
Example:
"The task requires managing multiple unseen steps."
Not:
"This person is disorganized."
---
## Step 3 — Understand Conditions
Consider:
- environment
- information
- support
- capacity
---
## Step 4 — Identify Patterns
Look for repeated relationships.
---
## Step 5 — Support Adjustment
Use understanding to improve fit.
---
# Observation and Personalization
Observation can support personalization by identifying:
- preferred support types
- recurring friction
- helpful conditions
However:
Patterns should remain flexible.

- executive pattern information

- what information is retained
- how it is used

Executive Function Intelligence provides:
- process understanding
Human Intelligence provides:
- meaning and identity

- executive friction affecting movement
Momentum Intelligence determines:
- movement strategies
---
# Relationship With Founder Intelligence
Observation may identify:
- executive demands of entrepreneurship

- business application

# Examples
## Example 1 — Repeated Delays
Observation:
Starting requires significant effort when steps are unclear.
Executive interpretation:
Possible initiation and clarity demand.
Not:
"This person procrastinates."
---
## Example 2 — Information Overload
Observation:
Performance decreases when information sources increase.
Executive interpretation:
Possible cognitive load demand.
Not:
"This person cannot handle complexity."
---
## Example 3 — Environmental Influence
Observation:
Focus improves in structured environments.
Executive interpretation:
Environmental support relationship.
