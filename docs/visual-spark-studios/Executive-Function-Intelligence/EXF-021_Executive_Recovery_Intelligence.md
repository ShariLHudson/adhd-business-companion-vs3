# EXF-021 Executive Recovery Intelligence
Defines Executive Recovery as an executive function concept and establishes how Executive Function Intelligence understands the process of restoring executive capacity after periods of demand, disruption, or depletion.
---
# Authority
This document is the authoritative definition of Executive Recovery Intelligence within the Executive Function Intelligence Library.
Executive Recovery is owned exclusively by Executive Function Intelligence.
Other Intelligence Libraries may reference Executive Recovery but must not redefine it.
Momentum Intelligence may reference recovery when supporting continued movement, but Momentum does not own Executive Recovery.

# Purpose
Executive functioning is not unlimited.
Periods of:
- sustained attention
- complex thinking
- emotional demand
- decision making
- adaptation
- interruption
consume executive resources.
Executive Recovery Intelligence exists to understand how executive resources are restored after demand.
It answers the question:
**"How does a person regain executive capacity after it has been used?"**

# Executive Recovery Defined
Executive Recovery is the executive process of restoring available resources after cognitive, emotional, or behavioral demands.
Recovery supports the ability to return to meaningful functioning after:
- intense focus
- prolonged effort
- stress
- complex decision making
- emotional activation
- repeated transitions
Recovery is not the absence of productivity.
Recovery protects future capability.
---
# Guiding Principles
## Recovery Is Part of Functioning
Sustainable functioning requires periods of restoration.
Recovery is not a failure to perform.
---
## Capacity Must Be Restored
Continued demand without recovery may reduce available executive resources.
---
## Recovery Needs Vary
Different situations require different forms and amounts of recovery.
---
## Recovery Should Be Intentional
Effective recovery considers what restores executive resources.
---
## Recovery Prevents Accumulated Friction
Insufficient recovery may increase difficulty with:
- attention
- initiation
- planning
- emotional regulation
- decision making
---
## Recovery Supports Long-Term Sustainability
The goal is not simply returning to action quickly.
The goal is maintaining the ability to continue meaningful action over time.
