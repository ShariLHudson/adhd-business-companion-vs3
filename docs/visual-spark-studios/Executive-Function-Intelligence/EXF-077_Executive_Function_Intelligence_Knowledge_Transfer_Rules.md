# EXF-077 Executive Function Intelligence Knowledge Transfer Rules
Defines how Executive Function Intelligence knowledge may be transferred, referenced, and used by other Intelligence Libraries while maintaining ownership boundaries and avoiding duplication.
---
# Authority
This document is the authoritative knowledge transfer standard for Executive Function Intelligence.
It defines:
- how executive knowledge moves through the ecosystem
- what information may be shared
- how ownership remains protected
It does not redefine other Intelligence Libraries.
---
# Purpose
Executive Function Intelligence provides foundational understanding that supports many areas of Spark.
Because executive processes influence many human activities, other libraries may need access to executive understanding.
However, shared knowledge must maintain clear ownership.
The purpose of knowledge transfer is:
**To provide useful executive context without creating duplicate intelligence systems.**
---
# Core Knowledge Transfer Principle
Share understanding.
Maintain ownership.
---
# What Executive Function Intelligence Can Transfer
Executive Function Intelligence may provide:
- definitions of executive processes
- relationships between executive concepts
- executive demand analysis
- support considerations
- cognitive context
---
# What Executive Function Intelligence Does Not Transfer as Ownership
Executive Function Intelligence does not transfer ownership of:
- goals
- decisions
- strategies
- meaning
- communication methods
- business reasoning
- teaching approaches
---
# Knowledge Transfer Categories
## Reference Knowledge
Other libraries may reference:
- executive definitions
- executive principles
- executive boundaries
Example:
Momentum Intelligence may reference Task Initiation.
---
## Relationship Knowledge
Other libraries may reference:
- how executive processes influence their domain
Example:
Founder Intelligence may reference cognitive load during entrepreneurship.
---
## Support Knowledge
Other libraries may reference:
- executive support considerations
Example:
Knowledge Management may reference Working Memory demands.
---
# Knowledge Transfer Rules
## Rule 1 — Preserve Source Ownership
When another library uses executive knowledge:
The Executive Function Library remains the owner.
---
## Rule 2 — Do Not Duplicate Definitions
Other libraries should reference executive concepts.
They should not recreate executive models.
---
## Rule 3 — Adapt Within Domain
Other libraries may explain:
"How does this affect my domain?"
They may not redefine:
"What is executive function?"
---
## Rule 4 — Maintain Context
Executive concepts should not be removed from their intended meaning.
---
## Rule 5 — Preserve Boundaries
Transferred knowledge should not expand into another library's ownership.
---

# Knowledge Transfer Process
## Step 1 — Identify Needed Concept
Determine which executive process is relevant.
---
## Step 2 — Reference Authority
Use the Executive Function source document.
---
## Step 3 — Apply Within Domain
Interpret the concept through the receiving library's ownership.
---
## Step 4 — Maintain Attribution
Do not create duplicate ownership.
---
# Common Transfer Errors
## Error 1 — Absorption
A library begins owning executive concepts.
Correction:
Reference Executive Function Intelligence.
---
## Error 2 — Duplication
A library recreates executive definitions.
Correction:
Link to the authoritative source.
---
## Error 3 — Overextension
Executive concepts are used to explain everything.
Correction:
Return to domain boundaries.
