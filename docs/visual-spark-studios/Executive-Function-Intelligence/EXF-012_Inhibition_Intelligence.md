# Inhibition Defined
Inhibition is the executive function process of pausing, filtering, and regulating responses before acting.
It supports the ability to:
- pause before responding
- resist unnecessary distractions
- consider consequences
- filter competing information
- delay immediate reactions when appropriate
- maintain alignment with intentions
Inhibition is not the absence of spontaneity.
It is the ability to intentionally choose when and how to respond.
---
# Guiding Principles
## Inhibition Supports Choice
Inhibition creates space for intentional decisions.
It allows a person to consider options before responding.
---
## Inhibition Is Not Suppression
Effective inhibition does not mean eliminating thoughts, emotions, or impulses.
It means creating awareness and choice.
---
## Context Influences Inhibition
The ability to pause and regulate responses changes depending on:
- stress
- fatigue
- emotional state
- environment
- urgency
- cognitive demand
---
## Inhibition Requires Available Capacity
When executive resources are reduced, response regulation may become more difficult.
Capacity influences access to executive skills.
---
## Inhibition Protects Attention
Filtering unnecessary interruptions and distractions helps preserve attention for meaningful activity.
---
## Inhibition Supports Relationships
Pausing before responding can improve communication, understanding, and collaboration.
