# EXF-061 Self-Monitoring Intelligence
Defines Self-Monitoring as an authoritative Executive Function concept and establishes how Executive Function Intelligence understands the process of observing, evaluating, and adjusting one's own thoughts, actions, and progress.
---
# Authority
This document is the authoritative definition of Self-Monitoring Intelligence within the Executive Function Intelligence Library.
Other Intelligence Libraries may reference Self-Monitoring.
They may not redefine Self-Monitoring.
---
# Purpose
Effective action requires awareness.
People must often recognize:
- what is happening
- whether something is working
- whether adjustments are needed
- whether progress is occurring
Self-Monitoring Intelligence exists to understand the executive process involved in observing and adjusting one's own functioning during purposeful activity.
It answers the question:
**"How does a person notice, evaluate, and adjust their own process?"**

# Self-Monitoring Defined
Self-Monitoring is the executive process of observing, evaluating, and adjusting one's thoughts, actions, behaviors, and progress in relation to an intended outcome.
It involves:
- awareness
- comparison
- reflection
- adjustment
---
# Self-Monitoring Components
## Observing Current State
A person recognizes:
- what they are doing
- what is happening
- what condition they are in
---
## Comparing Against Intention
Self-Monitoring considers:
- desired outcome
- current progress
- expected direction
---
## Identifying Gaps
A person notices:
- what is working
- what is not working
- what may need adjustment
---
## Adjusting Approach
Self-Monitoring supports:
- changing strategies
- modifying actions
- correcting direction
---
## Learning From Experience
Self-Monitoring allows experiences to inform future action.
---
# Self-Monitoring and Executive Function
Self-Monitoring interacts with multiple executive processes.
## Planning
Monitoring helps determine whether plans remain effective.
---
## Task Initiation
Monitoring helps recognize whether action has begun.
---
## Working Memory
Monitoring requires awareness of goals and current information.
---
## Cognitive Flexibility
Monitoring identifies when adaptation may be needed.
---
## Emotional Self-Regulation
Monitoring includes awareness of emotional conditions.
---
# Self-Monitoring Does Not Mean
Self-Monitoring is not:
- constant self-criticism
- perfectionism
- judging personal worth
- overanalyzing every action
Effective self-monitoring creates awareness for adjustment.
---
# Common Self-Monitoring Challenges
## Limited Awareness
A person may not notice patterns while they are happening.
---
## Delayed Feedback
Results may not be immediately visible.
---
## Emotional Influence
Strong emotions may affect interpretation.
---
## Unclear Goals
Monitoring is difficult without knowing what is being evaluated.
---
## Excessive Monitoring
Too much self-analysis can create additional burden.
---
# Self-Monitoring Support Principles
Support may include:
- defining desired outcomes
- creating visible progress markers
- reviewing patterns
- using reflection questions
- separating observation from judgment
The goal is awareness, not criticism.
