# EXF-067 Adaptive Executive Support Intelligence
Defines Adaptive Executive Support as an authoritative Executive Function support concept and establishes how Executive Function Intelligence understands matching support to changing executive needs, conditions, and environments.
---
# Authority
This document is the authoritative definition of Adaptive Executive Support Intelligence within the Executive Function Intelligence Library.
Other Intelligence Libraries may reference Adaptive Executive Support.
They may not redefine Adaptive Executive Support.
---
# Purpose
Executive functioning does not exist in isolation.
The effectiveness of support depends on the relationship between:
- the person
- the demand
- the environment
- available resources
- current conditions
A support approach that works in one situation may not work in another.
Adaptive Executive Support Intelligence exists to understand how support can adjust to changing needs instead of forcing people into fixed systems.
It answers the question:
**"How can support change to better fit the person and the situation?"**

# Adaptive Executive Support Defined
Adaptive Executive Support is the process of adjusting executive support based on:
- current demands
- available capacity
- personal patterns
- environmental conditions
- desired outcomes
Adaptive support recognizes that people require different forms of assistance at different times.
---
# Core Principle
The goal is not to make the person fit the system.
The goal is to create a better fit between:
- person
- environment
- demands
- support
---
# Adaptive Support Components
## Understanding Current Conditions
Support begins by understanding:
- what is happening now
- what demands exist
- what resources are available
---
## Matching Support Intensity
Different situations require different levels of support.
Examples:
- light structure
- guided structure
- increased external support
---
## Adjusting Over Time
Support should evolve as:
- needs change
- skills develop
- circumstances shift
---
## Preserving Independence
Support should strengthen capability.
It should not create unnecessary dependence.
---
## Learning From Outcomes
Support improves through understanding:
- what worked
- what created friction
- what should change
---
# Adaptive Executive Support and Executive Function
Adaptive support integrates multiple executive domains.
## Task Initiation
Support may help make beginning easier.
---
## Planning
Support may help create clearer pathways.
---
## Working Memory
Support may reduce unnecessary remembering.
---
## Organization
Support may create usable structure.
---
## Attention Management
Support may reduce competing demands.
---
## Emotional Self-Regulation
Support may consider emotional conditions.
---
## Executive Capacity
Support should match available resources.
---
# Adaptive Support Does Not Mean
Adaptive support is not:
- lowering expectations permanently
- removing challenge
- assuming inability
- creating rigid accommodations
Adaptation means creating better alignment.
---
# Adaptive Support Model
## Step 1 — Understand
Identify:
- person
- goal
- context
- current conditions
---
## Step 2 — Identify Executive Demands
Determine:
- what processes are required
- where friction appears
---
## Step 3 — Match Support
Select support that addresses the actual demand.
---
## Step 4 — Observe Results
Determine:
- what improved
- what remained difficult
---
## Step 5 — Adapt
Modify support based on learning.
