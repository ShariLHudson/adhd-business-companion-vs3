# Cognitive Flexibility Defined
Cognitive Flexibility is the executive function process of adapting thoughts, approaches, expectations, and actions when circumstances change.
It supports the ability to:
- consider alternatives
- adjust plans
- shift perspectives
- respond to new information
- revise approaches
- move between possibilities
Cognitive Flexibility allows people to continue functioning when reality differs from expectations.
---
# Guiding Principles
## Flexibility Is Adaptation, Not Lack of Commitment
Changing an approach does not automatically indicate inconsistency.
Adaptation can represent effective problem solving.
---
## Flexibility Requires Awareness
A person must recognize that change has occurred before adapting to it.
Awareness supports effective adjustment.
---
## Flexibility Balances Stability and Change
Effective functioning requires both:
- maintaining useful structures
- adapting when necessary
Too much rigidity limits adaptation.
Too much change can reduce continuity.
---
## Flexibility Is Context Dependent
A person may adapt easily in one situation and struggle in another.
Factors include:
- stress
- familiarity
- complexity
- emotional state
- available capacity
---
## Flexibility Supports Learning
Learning requires adjusting understanding based on new information and experience.
Flexibility allows growth.
---
## Flexibility Protects Progress
When circumstances change, the ability to adapt allows meaningful activity to continue.
---
