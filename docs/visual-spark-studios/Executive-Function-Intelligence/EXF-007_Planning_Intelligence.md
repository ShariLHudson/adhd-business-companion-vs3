# EXF-007 Planning Intelligence
Defines Planning as an executive function domain and establishes how Executive Function Intelligence understands the process of preparing purposeful action.
---

Planning is owned exclusively by Executive Function Intelligence.
Other Intelligence Libraries may reference Planning but must not redefine it.
Momentum Intelligence may reference planning readiness when understanding movement conditions, but Momentum does not own planning.

# Purpose
Planning transforms intention into a structured path toward action.
People often know what they want to accomplish but require additional executive processes to determine:
- what needs to happen
- when it should happen
- what resources are needed
- what sequence makes sense
- how progress will be recognized
Planning Intelligence exists to understand the executive processes involved in preparing for meaningful action.
It answers the question:
**"How does a person organize future action before it occurs?"**

# Planning Defined
Planning is the executive function process of creating a mental or external structure for future action.
Planning involves:
- identifying desired outcomes
- understanding requirements
- determining steps
- considering resources
- anticipating challenges
- preparing for execution
Planning connects intention with future action.
---
# Guiding Principles
## Planning Creates Direction
Planning helps transform broad intentions into meaningful paths forward.
Without direction, action may become scattered or inefficient.
---
## Planning Is Different From Doing
Planning prepares action.
Execution carries out action.
A person may be strong in one area and challenged in another.
Planning Intelligence recognizes this distinction.
---
## Good Planning Balances Structure and Flexibility
Effective plans provide guidance without becoming restrictive.
Too little structure may create uncertainty.
Too much structure may create unnecessary pressure.
---
## Planning Requires Available Capacity
Planning requires executive resources.
When capacity is limited, planning demands may become more difficult.
Planning Intelligence recognizes current conditions.
---
## Planning Benefits From External Support
Externalizing plans can reduce cognitive demand.
Examples include:
- written plans
- visual structures
- reminders
- organized information
Planning Intelligence recognizes external support as an effective adaptation.
---
## Planning Improves Through Experience
Plans become stronger through:
- reflection
- feedback
- adjustment
- learning
Planning is an evolving capability.
---
