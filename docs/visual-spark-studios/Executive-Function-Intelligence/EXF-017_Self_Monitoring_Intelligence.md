# Purpose
Meaningful action requires awareness.
People continually need to recognize:
- what is happening
- what is working
- what is not working
- what needs adjustment
- whether progress is occurring
Self-Monitoring Intelligence exists to understand the executive processes involved in observing and adjusting one's own behavior, thinking, and actions.
It answers the question:
**"How does a person notice, evaluate, and adjust their own process while moving toward meaningful outcomes?"**

# Self-Monitoring Defined
Self-Monitoring is the executive function process of observing, evaluating, and adjusting one's own thoughts, actions, behaviors, and progress.
It supports the ability to:
- notice current state
- compare action with intention
- recognize effectiveness
- identify needed adjustments
- learn from experience
Self-Monitoring creates awareness that supports adaptation.
---
# Guiding Principles
## Awareness Comes Before Adjustment
A person must first recognize what is occurring before meaningful change can happen.
---
## Self-Monitoring Is Not Self-Judgment
Effective self-monitoring observes information.
It does not create unnecessary criticism.
---
## Feedback Improves Function
Self-monitoring allows people to learn from:
- experience
- outcomes
- patterns
- feedback
---
## Self-Monitoring Requires Perspective
People need enough distance from an experience to observe it.
Strong emotion, overload, or pressure may influence this ability.
---
## Self-Monitoring Supports Independence
Recognizing patterns allows people to make increasingly informed adjustments.
---
## Self-Monitoring Develops Over Time
Experience, reflection, and learning strengthen awareness.
---
