Defines Prioritization as an executive function domain and establishes how Executive Function Intelligence understands the process of determining relative importance when multiple demands compete for attention and action.

# Purpose
People regularly face multiple:
- responsibilities
- opportunities
- requests
- ideas
- obligations
- goals
Executive functioning requires determining what deserves attention now, what can wait, and what may no longer matter.
Prioritization Intelligence exists to understand the executive processes involved in evaluating relative importance and directing resources toward what matters most.
It answers the question:
**"How does a person determine what deserves attention and action first?"**

# Prioritization Defined
Prioritization is the executive function process of evaluating competing demands and determining relative importance.
It involves:
- recognizing competing needs
- evaluating significance
- considering timing
- allocating attention
- choosing focus
Prioritization does not mean deciding that something is unimportant.
It means determining what requires attention first, now, or differently.
---
# Guiding Principles
## Prioritization Creates Direction
Without prioritization, everything can appear equally urgent.
Prioritization creates meaningful focus.
---
## Prioritization Requires Context
Importance depends upon circumstances.
The same activity may have different priority levels depending on:
- timing
- goals
- consequences
- resources
- current needs
---
## Prioritization Is Not Simply Urgency
Urgent tasks are not always the most important.
Effective prioritization considers broader context.
---
## Prioritization Requires Trade-Off Awareness
Choosing one priority often means temporarily reducing attention elsewhere.
Recognizing trade-offs is part of effective prioritization.
---
## Prioritization Changes Over Time
Priorities evolve as circumstances change.
A previous priority may become less relevant.
Adaptation is part of effective prioritization.
---
## Too Many Priorities Reduce Priority
When everything receives equal importance, meaningful focus becomes difficult.
Prioritization requires distinction.
