# EXF-056 Prioritization Intelligence
Defines Prioritization as an authoritative Executive Function concept and establishes how Executive Function Intelligence understands the process of determining relative importance, sequence, and focus.
---

Other Intelligence Libraries may reference Prioritization.
They may not redefine Prioritization.
---
# Purpose
People constantly face competing demands.
They must determine:
- what matters most
- what should happen first
- what can wait
- where resources should be directed
Prioritization Intelligence exists to understand the executive process involved in evaluating importance and organizing attention and effort accordingly.
It answers the question:
**"How does a person determine what deserves focus right now?"**

# Prioritization Defined
Prioritization is the executive process of determining relative importance among competing demands and directing resources accordingly.
Prioritization involves:
- comparing demands
- identifying significance
- determining sequence
- allocating attention and effort
Prioritization helps answer:
"What matters most at this moment?"

# Prioritization Does Not Mean
Prioritization is not:
- doing everything quickly
- completing only urgent tasks
- choosing based only on pressure
- deciding what matters according to someone else's values
Prioritization requires context.
---
# Common Prioritization Challenges
## Too Many Important Tasks
Multiple meaningful demands can create difficulty selecting focus.
---
## Limited Capacity
Available resources may not match all demands.
---
## Emotional Weight
Emotionally significant tasks may compete strongly for attention.
---
## Unclear Criteria
Prioritization becomes harder without clear evaluation factors.
---
## Constant Changes
Changing conditions may require repeated reassessment.

# Prioritization Support Principles
Support may include:
- clarifying desired outcomes
- identifying meaningful criteria
- reducing unnecessary options
- separating urgent from important
- creating visible priorities
The goal is not simply doing more.
The goal is directing resources intentionally.
