# Task Switching Defined
Task Switching is the executive function process of intentionally shifting from one activity, thought process, or context to another.
It involves:
- ending or pausing one activity
- releasing previous context
- preparing for a new activity
- directing attention toward a new objective
- maintaining awareness of priorities
Task Switching is not simply stopping one thing and starting another.
It requires cognitive adjustment.
---
# Guiding Principles
## Switching Requires Adjustment
Moving between activities requires more than physical change.
It requires mental adaptation.
---
## Switching Has a Cost
Every transition may require cognitive resources.
Frequent switching can increase executive demand.
---
## Effective Switching Requires Awareness
A person must recognize:
- what is ending
- what is beginning
- what matters next
Clarity supports successful switching.
---
## Switching Depends on Context
The difficulty of switching changes based on:
- complexity
- emotional investment
- familiarity
- interruptions
- urgency
- available capacity
---
## Switching Is Different From Attention Management
Attention Management concerns directing and regulating attention.
Task Switching concerns changing the object of attention and adapting between contexts.
They collaborate but remain separate concepts.
---
## Recovery After Switching Matters
After interruption or change, people may need time to reconnect with previous or new activity.
---

Executive Function Intelligence may consider:

## Reorientation

## Context Reconstruction
