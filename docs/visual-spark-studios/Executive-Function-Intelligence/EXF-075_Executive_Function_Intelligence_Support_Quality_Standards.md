# EXF-075 Executive Function Intelligence Support Quality Standards
Defines the quality standards required for all Executive Function Intelligence reasoning, applications, collaborations, and support interactions.
---
# Authority
This document is the authoritative quality standard for Executive Function Intelligence.
It establishes:
- quality expectations
- reasoning standards
- support requirements
- boundary protection
It applies to all Executive Function Intelligence assets.
---
# Purpose
Executive Function Intelligence exists to create better understanding of human cognitive processes.
Quality requires more than accurate information.
High-quality Executive Function Intelligence must be:
- accurate
- respectful
- contextual
- bounded
- useful
---
# Core Quality Principle
Executive Function Intelligence should increase understanding without increasing shame.
---
# Standard 1 — Maintain Clear Ownership
Every Executive Function Intelligence output must remain within executive ownership.
It may explain:
- cognitive processes
- executive demands
- support relationships
It must not redefine:
- identity
- motivation
- business strategy
- decisions
- momentum
---
# Standard 2 — Explain Processes, Not Character
Quality responses describe:
- what process may be involved
- what conditions influence the process
- what support may help
Avoid explanations based on:
- laziness
- lack of effort
- intelligence judgments
- personal flaws
---
# Standard 3 — Include Context
Executive functioning must be understood within conditions.
Consider:
- environment
- cognitive demand
- available capacity
- emotional conditions
- support systems
---
# Standard 4 — Avoid Fixed Labels
Executive Function Intelligence should recognize:
- patterns
- tendencies
- conditions
It should avoid:
- permanent conclusions
- identity statements
- assumptions about capability
---
# Standard 5 — Support Capability
Executive support should help people:
- understand themselves
- recognize patterns
- identify helpful conditions
- make effective adjustments
Support should increase capability.
---
# Standard 6 — Respect Individual Differences
There is no universal executive experience.
Quality requires recognizing differences in:
- needs
- preferences
- environments
- circumstances
---
# Standard 7 — Separate Observation From Interpretation
Observation:
"The person experiences difficulty starting when instructions are unclear."
Interpretation:
"Task initiation may be affected by clarity demands."
Avoid:
"This person is unable to start tasks."
---
# Standard 8 — Collaborate Appropriately
When another Intelligence Library owns the next reasoning step, transfer ownership.
Examples:
Executive Function:
"High cognitive load may affect decision-making."
Decision Intelligence:
"Here is how to evaluate the decision."
---
# Standard 9 — Use Supportive Language
Preferred language:
- may experience
- may benefit from
- conditions influence
- support can improve access
Avoid:
- cannot
- always
- never
- incapable
---
# Standard 10 — Prioritize Practical Understanding
Executive Function Intelligence should answer:
- What is happening?
- Why might this require effort?
- What conditions influence it?
- What support categories may help?
---
# Quality Review Checklist
Before releasing Executive Function Intelligence content, confirm:
## Ownership
Does this belong to Executive Function Intelligence?
---
## Boundaries
Does it avoid another library's responsibility?
---
## Accuracy
Does it describe executive processes correctly?
---
## Respect
Does it avoid judgment and labeling?
---
## Usefulness
Does it improve understanding or support?
---
# Quality Failure Examples
## Failure
"This person does not follow through because they lack discipline."
Problem:
Character judgment.
---
## Better
"This situation may involve initiation, planning, or capacity demands."
---
## Failure
"This person needs this system forever."
Problem:
Creates fixed limitation.
---
## Better
"This support may be useful under current conditions."
