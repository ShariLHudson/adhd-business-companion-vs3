# EXF-069 Executive Friction Intelligence
Defines Executive Friction as an Executive Function Intelligence concept for understanding obstacles that increase the effort required to begin, continue, or complete purposeful action.
---
# Authority
This document defines Executive Friction within Executive Function Intelligence.
It does not define:
- motivation
- resistance
- personal barriers
- emotional meaning
- productivity problems
It focuses specifically on executive demands that increase effort or complexity.
---
# Purpose
Many actions are not difficult because the person lacks ability.
They may be difficult because the path requires excessive executive effort.
Executive friction can appear when there is:
- unclear information
- too many steps
- high cognitive demand
- missing structure
- frequent switching
- unnecessary complexity
Executive Friction Intelligence exists to identify where executive demands create unnecessary effort.
It answers the question:
**"What parts of this situation require more executive effort than necessary?"**
---

This document owns:
- executive friction definition

- executive demand reduction principles
This document does not own:
- motivation

---
# Executive Friction Defined
Executive Friction is the additional effort required to complete an action because executive demands are higher than necessary.
Friction may occur between:
- intention and action
- information and use
- planning and execution
- attention and completion
---
# Executive Friction Components
## Clarity Friction
Occurs when the next action is unclear.
Examples:
- unclear expectations
- undefined steps
- missing information
---
## Cognitive Friction
Occurs when mental processing demands are unnecessarily high.
Examples:
- too much information
- excessive complexity
- difficult systems
---
## Memory Friction
Occurs when too much information must be remembered internally.
Examples:
- missing reminders
- unclear references
- lost context
---
## Transition Friction
Occurs when movement between activities requires excessive adjustment.
Examples:
- abrupt changes
- lost context
- unclear next steps
---
## Attention Friction
Occurs when focus is difficult to direct because of competing demands.
Examples:
- distractions
- conflicting priorities
- unclear focus
---
# Executive Friction and Executive Function
Executive Friction interacts with multiple executive processes.
## Task Initiation
High friction can increase difficulty starting.
---
## Planning
Unclear pathways increase planning demand.
---
## Working Memory
Missing external support increases memory burden.
---
## Organization
Poor structure increases retrieval effort.
---
## Attention Management
Competing demands increase focus effort.
---
# Executive Friction Does Not Mean
Executive friction is not:
- lack of caring
- lack of intelligence
- lack of discipline
- unwillingness
Friction describes the relationship between demands and available support.
---
# Identifying Executive Friction
A useful analysis asks:
## What is the intended action?
What is the person trying to accomplish?
---
## What executive demands exist?
What processes are required?
---
## Where does effort increase?
Where does the process become harder than expected?
---
## What support reduces unnecessary demand?
What changes improve alignment?
---
# Executive Friction Reduction Principles
Support may include:
- increasing clarity
- reducing unnecessary steps
- improving access to information
- creating appropriate structure
- reducing avoidable switching
The goal is not removing all difficulty.
The goal is reducing unnecessary difficulty.
