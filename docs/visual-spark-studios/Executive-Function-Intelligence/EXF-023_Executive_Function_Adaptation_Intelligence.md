# EXF-023 Executive Function Adaptation Intelligence
Defines how Executive Function Intelligence adapts understanding and support based on changing executive conditions, individual needs, and accumulated knowledge.
---
# Authority
This document is the authoritative definition of Executive Function Adaptation Intelligence within the Executive Function Intelligence Library.
Executive Function Adaptation Intelligence owns the process of adapting executive support based on executive understanding.
Other Intelligence Libraries may reference adaptation but must not redefine Executive Function adaptation.
Momentum Intelligence may adapt movement strategies.
Conversation Intelligence may adapt communication.
Decision Intelligence may adapt decision support.
Executive Function Intelligence owns adaptation related specifically to executive functioning.

# Purpose
Executive functioning changes.
People change.
Circumstances change.
Demands change.
Effective support must recognize that what works in one moment may not work in another.
Executive Function Adaptation Intelligence exists to ensure that executive support remains responsive rather than rigid.
It answers the question:
**"How should executive support change as the person's executive reality changes?"**

# Executive Function Adaptation Defined
Executive Function Adaptation is the process of adjusting support based on current executive conditions, recurring patterns, and changing circumstances.
It recognizes that effective support requires alignment between:
- person
- task
- environment
- capacity
- available resources
Adaptation is not lowering expectations.
It is improving fit.
---
# Guiding Principles
## Support Should Match Current Reality
Effective support begins with understanding current conditions.
---
## Adaptation Is Not Failure
Changing an approach does not mean the original approach failed.
It means new information created an opportunity for improvement.
---
## Individual Fit Matters
Different people require different forms of executive support.
Universal solutions rarely fit every situation.
---
## Small Adjustments Can Create Large Improvements
Reducing one source of friction can significantly improve functioning.
---
## Adaptation Protects Confidence
Support should help people experience capability rather than repeated failure.
---
## Adaptation Should Build Independence
The goal is not permanent external support.
The goal is increasing understanding and capability over time.
