# EXF-058 Transition Intelligence
Defines Transitions as an authoritative Executive Function concept and establishes how Executive Function Intelligence understands the process of moving attention, activity, or mental state from one situation to another.
---
# Authority
This document is the authoritative definition of Transition Intelligence within the Executive Function Intelligence Library.
Other Intelligence Libraries may reference Transitions.
They may not redefine Transitions.
---
# Purpose
Human life requires constant movement between:
- activities
- environments
- priorities
- mental states
- responsibilities
Transitions create moments where a person must disengage from one state and enter another.
Transition Intelligence exists to understand the executive process involved in shifting effectively between different activities, contexts, and demands.
It answers the question:
**"How does a person move from one state of action or attention into another?"**

# Transition Defined
Transition is the executive process of moving from one activity, focus state, environment, or mental context into another.
Transitions involve:
- ending one state
- shifting resources
- preparing for the next state
- beginning a new activity
---
# Transition Components
## Disengaging
Transitions often require releasing attention from the current activity.
---
## Shifting
Transitions require moving focus toward a new activity or demand.
---
## Reorienting
Transitions require understanding:
- what is next
- what changed
- what is expected
---
## Preparing
Transitions may require gathering:
- information
- resources
- emotional readiness
---
## Re-engaging
Transitions involve entering the new activity effectively.
---
# Transition and Executive Function
Transitions interact with multiple executive processes.
## Attention Management
Transitions require shifting attention intentionally.
---
## Working Memory
Transitions often require remembering what comes next.
---
## Planning
Transitions depend on understanding future activity.
---
## Task Initiation
Transitions often require beginning a new action.
---
## Emotional Self-Regulation
Transitions may involve adjusting emotional state.
---
## Cognitive Flexibility
Transitions require adapting to changing demands.
---
# Transition Does Not Mean
Transition difficulty is not:
- unwillingness to change
- lack of effort
- lack of flexibility as a person
Transitions simply create executive demands.
---
# Common Transition Challenges
## Leaving Engaging Activities
A person may experience difficulty shifting away from something highly engaging.
---
## Starting New Activities
A new activity may require significant preparation.
---
## Unexpected Changes
Sudden changes can increase executive demand.
---
## Multiple Transitions
Frequent switching may increase cognitive load.
---
## Emotional Transitions
Moving between emotional states may require additional resources.
---
# Transition Support Principles
Support may include:
- advance awareness of changes
- clear next steps
- transition cues
- preparation time
- reducing unnecessary complexity
The goal is smoother movement between states.
---
# Relationship With Momentum Intelligence
Transition Intelligence and Momentum Intelligence collaborate.
Transition Intelligence owns:
**the executive process of shifting between states.**
Momentum Intelligence owns:
**the process of maintaining meaningful movement over time.**
Transitions can protect momentum.
They do not define momentum.
