# EXF-083 Executive Function Intelligence Completion Standard
Defines the criteria for determining when the Executive Function Intelligence Library is complete, stable, and ready to support the broader Spark Intelligence ecosystem.
---
# Authority
This document is the authoritative completion standard for Executive Function Intelligence.
It defines:
- when the library is considered complete
- how future additions should be evaluated
- how scope expansion should be controlled
It does not prevent future improvement.
---
# Purpose
A strong intelligence library requires boundaries.
Without completion standards, libraries can continue expanding indefinitely, creating:
- duplication
- unclear ownership
- unnecessary complexity
The purpose of this standard is to ensure Executive Function Intelligence remains:
- focused
- useful
- connected
- maintainable
---
# Completion Definition
The Executive Function Intelligence Library is complete when it provides sufficient understanding of:
- executive processes
- executive demands
- executive support relationships
- executive collaboration boundaries
while avoiding ownership of other intelligence domains.
---
# Completion Criteria
## Criterion 1 — Core Executive Domains Are Defined
The library must contain authoritative definitions for foundational executive processes.
Completed domains include:
- Task Initiation
- Planning
- Working Memory
- Organization
- Attention Management
- Prioritization
- Time Awareness
- Transitions
- Task Switching
- Cognitive Flexibility
- Self-Monitoring
- Emotional Self-Regulation
- Cognitive Load
- Executive Capacity
- Executive Recovery
---
# Criterion 2 — Support Understanding Exists
The library must explain how executive demands may be supported.
Completed support concepts include:
- Externalization Intelligence
- Adaptive Executive Support Intelligence
- Executive Pattern Recognition Intelligence
- Executive Friction Intelligence
- Executive Environment Design Intelligence
- Executive Support Matching Intelligence
---
# Criterion 3 — Boundaries Are Established
The library must clearly define what Executive Function Intelligence does and does not own.
Completed boundary areas include:
- collaboration
- knowledge transfer
- assessment
- application
- quality standards
---
# Criterion 4 — Integration Rules Exist
The library must define how it collaborates with:
- Human Intelligence
- Momentum Intelligence
- Founder Intelligence
- Decision Intelligence
- Knowledge Management
- Teaching Intelligence
- Conversation Intelligence
---
# Criterion 5 — Personalization Is Governed
The library must explain how executive patterns may be used without:
- labeling people
- creating fixed categories
- limiting capability
---
# Future Addition Requirements
A new Executive Function Intelligence document should only be created if it satisfies all questions below.
---
## Question 1
Is this a universal executive process?
If no:
It belongs elsewhere.
---
## Question 2
Does it explain cognitive demand rather than application?
If no:
It belongs elsewhere.
---
## Question 3
Will multiple intelligence libraries benefit from this concept?
If no:
It may not belong in the global library.
---
## Question 4
Does it avoid duplicating another library?
If no:
The other library remains the owner.
---
# Examples of Appropriate Future Additions
Potential future additions may include:
- clarification of existing executive concepts
- improved architecture documentation
- expanded collaboration rules
- implementation refinements
---
# Examples of Inappropriate Expansion
Do not add:
## Executive Motivation Intelligence
Reason:
Overlaps with Momentum and Human Intelligence.
---
## Executive Productivity Intelligence
Reason:
Overlaps with workflows and application systems.
---
## Executive Success Intelligence
Reason:
Not an executive process.
---
## Executive Business Intelligence
Reason:
Belongs to Founder Intelligence.
---
## Executive Creativity Intelligence
Reason:
Belongs to Creative or Innovation Intelligence.
---
# Maintenance Rules
Future updates should:
- improve clarity
- preserve ownership
- reduce confusion
- strengthen collaboration
Future updates should not:
- expand unnecessarily
- duplicate other libraries
- create overlapping concepts
---
# Final Completion Statement
Executive Function Intelligence is complete when it successfully provides the cognitive foundation that allows other Intelligence Libraries to understand executive demands without attempting to become the entire human reasoning system.
---
# Permanent Ownership Rule
Executive Function Intelligence owns:
**the understanding of executive processes and their relationship to cognitive demands and support.**
It does not own:
- the person's identity
- their goals
- their choices
- their purpose
- their success
