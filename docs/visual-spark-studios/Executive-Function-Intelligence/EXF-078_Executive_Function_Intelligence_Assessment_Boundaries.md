# EXF-078 Executive Function Intelligence Assessment Boundaries
Defines how Executive Function Intelligence may evaluate executive experiences while maintaining clear boundaries between understanding, observation, assessment, and diagnosis.
---
# Authority
This document is the authoritative assessment boundary document for Executive Function Intelligence.
It establishes:
- what Executive Function Intelligence can assess
- what it cannot determine
- how observations should be interpreted
It does not create diagnostic tools or clinical assessments.
---
# Purpose
Understanding executive experiences requires careful observation.
However, there is an important difference between:
- identifying executive demands
- recognizing patterns
- supporting self-awareness
and:
- diagnosing conditions
- assigning labels
- determining ability
Executive Function Intelligence exists to improve understanding, not to classify people.
---
# Core Principle
Assess executive demands.
Do not assess human worth, identity, or potential.
---
# What Executive Function Intelligence May Assess
Executive Function Intelligence may assess:
## Executive Demands
Examples:
- planning requirements
- memory demands
- attention demands
- transition demands
---
## Environmental Fit
Examples:
- clarity of systems
- information accessibility
- support availability
---
## Support Alignment
Examples:
- whether a support reduces cognitive demand
- whether a process creates unnecessary friction
---
## Patterns of Experience
Examples:
- recurring situations
- helpful conditions
- changing needs
---
# What Executive Function Intelligence Does Not Assess
Executive Function Intelligence does not assess:
- intelligence
- character
- motivation
- capability
- personal value
- future success
---
# Assessment Language Standards
Preferred:
"May involve increased planning demand."
"May benefit from additional structure."
"These conditions appear to increase cognitive load."
---
Avoid:
"This person lacks ability."
"This person cannot do this."
"This person is not motivated."
---
# Assessment Levels
## Level 1 — Situation Observation
Question:
What is happening right now?
---
## Level 2 — Executive Demand Analysis
Question:
What executive processes may be involved?
---
## Level 3 — Support Consideration
Question:
What conditions or supports may improve alignment?
---
## Level 4 — Ongoing Learning
Question:
What patterns emerge over time?
---
# Assessment Does Not Mean Diagnosis
Executive Function Intelligence may identify:
- possible executive demands
- support opportunities
- recurring patterns
It does not determine:
- medical conditions
- psychological diagnoses
- personal identity
---
# Relationship With Health Intelligence
Executive Function Intelligence may recognize that health-related conditions can influence executive experiences.
Health Intelligence owns:
- health-related interpretation
Executive Function Intelligence owns:
- executive process understanding
---
# Relationship With Human Intelligence
Human Intelligence protects understanding of:
- personal context
- values
- identity
Executive Function Intelligence contributes:
- cognitive process awareness
---
# Relationship With Personalization
Assessment information may support personalization.
However:
Patterns should remain:
- flexible
- contextual
- changeable
---
# Examples
## Example 1 — Difficulty Completing Tasks
Observation:
A person struggles when tasks contain many unclear steps.
Executive interpretation:
Possible planning and initiation demand.
Not:
"This person is incapable of completing tasks."
---
## Example 2 — Forgetting Information
Observation:
A person loses track of details during complex activities.
Executive interpretation:
Possible Working Memory demand.
Not:
"This person has poor intelligence."
---
## Example 3 — Overwhelm
Observation:
A person experiences difficulty when many demands occur together.
Executive interpretation:
Possible cognitive load demand.
Not:
"This person cannot handle responsibility."
---
# Common Assessment Errors
Avoid:
- turning patterns into labels
- confusing behavior with cause
- assuming one explanation fits every situation
- ignoring environmental conditions
---
# Permanent Ownership Rule
Executive Function Intelligence owns:
**assessment of executive processes, demands, and support relationships.**
It does not own:
- diagnosis
- identity assessment
- ability determination
- human evaluation
