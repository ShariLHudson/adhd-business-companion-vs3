# Attention Management Defined
Attention Management is the executive function process of intentionally directing, sustaining, shifting, and regulating attention.
It involves:
- selecting what deserves focus
- maintaining attention toward an objective
- shifting attention when appropriate
- managing competing information
- recognizing attention conditions
Attention is not simply a matter of willpower.
It is influenced by:
- environment
- cognitive demand
- interest
- emotional state
- energy
- familiarity
- available structure
---
# Guiding Principles
## Attention Is a Limited Resource
Attention cannot be directed toward everything equally.
Choosing what receives attention requires prioritization and awareness.
---
## Attention Changes With Context
A person may demonstrate strong attention in one situation and difficulty in another.
Context influences attention.
---
## Attention Is Not the Same as Effort
Difficulty maintaining attention does not automatically indicate lack of commitment.
Executive Function Intelligence considers the conditions influencing attention.
---
## Attention Requires Direction
Meaningful action often requires clarity about where attention should be placed.
Unclear priorities increase attention demands.
---
## Attention Can Be Supported
Attention is influenced by systems and environments.
Support may include:
- reducing distractions
- increasing clarity
- creating structure
- limiting unnecessary choices
- organizing information
---
## Attention Shifting Is Part of Functioning
Effective action often requires moving attention intentionally between activities.
The ability to shift is distinct from the ability to sustain attention.
