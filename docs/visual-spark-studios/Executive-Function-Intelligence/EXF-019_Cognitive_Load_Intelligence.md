# EXF-019 Cognitive Load Intelligence
Defines Cognitive Load as an executive function concept and establishes how Executive Function Intelligence understands the amount of mental effort required to process information, manage demands, and complete meaningful activity.
---

# Purpose
Every activity requires mental resources.
The amount of effort required can change depending upon:
- complexity
- uncertainty
- information volume
- interruptions
- emotional demands
- competing responsibilities
Cognitive Load Intelligence exists to understand the mental demands placed upon executive systems during meaningful activity.
It answers the question:
**"How much cognitive effort is this situation requiring, and how does that influence available executive capacity?"**

# Cognitive Load Defined
Cognitive Load refers to the amount of mental effort required to understand information, manage demands, and complete activity.
Cognitive Load is influenced by:
- number of elements being managed
- complexity of relationships
- amount of uncertainty
- need for active problem solving
- emotional involvement
- environmental demands
Cognitive Load is not simply the amount of work.
A simple task can have high cognitive load.
A complex task can have lower cognitive load when it is familiar and well-supported.
---
# Guiding Principles
## Cognitive Capacity Is Limited
Executive resources are finite.
When demands exceed available capacity, functioning may become more difficult.
---
## Cognitive Load Changes
Cognitive demands are not fixed.
They change with:
- experience
- familiarity
- environment
- stress
- energy
- support systems
---
## Reduce Unnecessary Load
Effective support does not always require increasing capacity.
Often, reducing unnecessary demand creates better outcomes.
---
## Complexity Requires Structure
Complex situations become easier when information is organized, prioritized, and sequenced.
---
## External Supports Reduce Internal Demand
Tools, systems, and structures can reduce the amount of information that must be managed mentally.
---
## Cognitive Load Influences Performance
Performance reflects both ability and current demands.
Difficulty may indicate excessive load rather than insufficient capability.
---
# Cognitive Load Components
Executive Function Intelligence may consider:
## Information Load
The amount of information being processed.
---
## Decision Load
The number of choices requiring evaluation.
---
## Emotional Load
The emotional effort required by a situation.
---
## Transition Load
The effort required to shift between contexts.
---
## Complexity Load
The effort required to understand relationships and systems.
---
# Cognitive Load and Other Executive Domains
Cognitive Load interacts with multiple executive processes.
## Working Memory
High cognitive load increases demands on active information management.
---
## Attention Management
High load can make directing attention more difficult.
---
## Planning
Complex plans increase cognitive demands.
---
## Organization
Effective organization can reduce unnecessary cognitive effort.
---
## Emotional Self-Regulation
Emotional demands can increase overall cognitive load.
---
Cognitive Load Intelligence recognizes these relationships without redefining other executive domains.

Momentum Intelligence and Cognitive Load Intelligence collaborate closely.

**"How much mental effort does this situation require?"**

Momentum does not define Cognitive Load.

# Common Mistakes
Avoid:
- assuming difficulty means inability
- confusing cognitive load with intelligence
- ignoring environmental demands
- increasing complexity when simplification would help
- treating overload as personal failure
- redefining cognitive load outside Executive Function Intelligence
Cognitive Load Intelligence seeks to understand demand before judging performance.
