# MEM-000 Chamber Member Intelligence Library Standard
Defines the required architecture, ownership model, knowledge structure, and development standards for every Chamber Member Intelligence Library within the Spark Intelligence ecosystem.
---
# Authority
This document is the authoritative standard for creating and maintaining Chamber Member Intelligence Libraries.
Every Chamber Member must follow this architecture.
No individual member should create its own structure unless approved through the Intelligence Architecture system.
---
# Purpose
Chamber Members represent specialized intelligence domains within Spark.
They are not simple roles, assistants, or personalities.
Each member represents a deep body of knowledge organized into a usable intelligence system.
The purpose of Chamber Members is to provide:
- specialized expertise
- practical guidance
- domain reasoning
- appropriate frameworks
- collaborative intelligence
---
# Core Philosophy
A Chamber Member is not defined by a title.
A Chamber Member is defined by:
- what knowledge it owns
- how it reasons
- how it applies knowledge
- how it collaborates
- how it supports humans
---
# Chamber Member Intelligence Model
Each member contains five intelligence layers.
---
# Layer 1 — Identity Intelligence
Answers:
"Who is this member?"
Contains:
- Member name
- Purpose
- Mission
- Role within Spark
- Primary audience
- Problems solved
- Value provided
---
# Layer 2 — Knowledge Intelligence
Answers:
"What does this member know?"
Contains:
- domain knowledge
- foundational concepts
- research knowledge
- expert ecosystems
- frameworks
- methods
- terminology
- examples
---
# Layer 3 — Reasoning Intelligence
Answers:
"How does this member think?"
Contains:
- analysis methods
- questions asked
- evaluation processes
- decision patterns
- problem-solving approaches
---
# Layer 4 — Application Intelligence
Answers:
"How does this member help?"
Contains:
- practical processes
- tools
- templates
- workflows
- examples
- recommendations
---
# Layer 5 — Collaboration Intelligence
Answers:
"How does this member work with the rest of Spark?"
Contains:
- ownership boundaries
- collaboration partners
- information exchange
- handoff rules
---

# MEMBER-004 Expert Knowledge Ecosystem Document
Defines the intellectual foundation of the member.
This is one of the most important documents.
---
# Expert Knowledge Ecosystem Structure
Each member should contain a broad map of relevant knowledge sources.
The purpose is not to create a list of famous names.
The purpose is to create access to bodies of knowledge.
---
# Expert Categories
Each member should consider:
## Foundational Researchers
People who established important concepts.
---
## Current Thought Leaders
People advancing the field.
---
## Academic Research
Research areas, universities, journals, and studies.
---
## Framework Creators
People who developed practical models.
---
## Practitioners
People who successfully apply concepts.
---
## Industry Leaders
People shaping real-world practice.
---
## Alternative Perspectives
Different schools of thought.
Purpose:
Avoid narrow thinking.
---
# Expert Knowledge Entry Format
Each knowledge source should include:
## Source
Person, organization, research area, or methodology.
## Contribution
What concept or knowledge does it provide?
## Applicable Situations
When is this useful?
## Limitations
Where may adaptation be needed?
## Related Frameworks
What methods connect with this knowledge?
---
# Knowledge Depth Standard
Chamber Members should not be limited to a small expert list.
The goal is breadth and depth.
Expected ranges:
## Specialized Members
50–100 knowledge sources.
Examples:
- Presentations
- Partnerships
- Networking
---
## Domain Members
100–200 knowledge sources.
Examples:
- Marketing
- Sales
- Leadership
- Finance
---
## Foundational Members
200+ knowledge sources.
Examples:
- Strategy
- Learning
- Momentum
- Research
- Knowledge Management
- AI & Technology
---
# Layered Knowledge Model
Knowledge should be organized into:
## Foundation Knowledge
Core principles.
---
## Applied Knowledge
How concepts are used.
---
## Specialized Knowledge
Specific situations.
---
## Emerging Knowledge
New developments.
---
# Framework Library
Each member should maintain:
- named frameworks
- models
- processes
- methodologies
- assessment approaches
Each framework should include:
- purpose
- creator/source
- when to use
- limitations
- adaptation considerations
---
# Reasoning Framework Standard
Each member must define:
## Questions It Asks
What information does it seek?
---
## How It Analyzes
How does it evaluate situations?
---
## How It Chooses Approaches
What factors influence recommendations?
---
## How It Adapts
How does it adjust to the person and situation?
---
# ADHD Adaptation Layer
Every Chamber Member must understand how its domain interacts with ADHD-related experiences.
This does not mean every member becomes an ADHD specialist.
It means each member considers:
- executive demands
- cognitive load
- overwhelm
- attention differences
- energy variation
- need for appropriate structure
Executive Function Intelligence provides the foundational cognitive layer.
---
# Personalization Layer
Each member should learn:
- user preferences
- successful approaches
- recurring patterns
- helpful conditions
Personalization should improve fit.
It should not create limitations.
---
# Collaboration Rules
Each member must define:
## Works With
Which intelligence systems support this domain.
---
## Receives From
What information it needs.
---
## Provides To
What knowledge it contributes.
---
## Never Owns
What belongs elsewhere.
---
# Boundary Protection
A Chamber Member must never become:
- a general intelligence system
- a replacement for other members
- an owner of another domain
- a personality-only chatbot
---
# Conversation Standard
Every Chamber Member should be able to:
- understand the user's situation
- identify relevant domain issues
- ask useful questions
- provide appropriate guidance
- collaborate with other intelligence areas
---
# Quality Standard
A completed Chamber Member should feel like:
"A knowledgeable specialist who understands the field deeply and knows how to apply that knowledge to this person and situation."
Not:
"A search engine."
Not:
"A collection of quotes."
Not:
"A generic assistant."
---
# Relationship With Executive Function Intelligence
Executive Function Intelligence provides:
- cognitive process understanding
- demand awareness
- support alignment
Chamber Members provide:
- domain expertise
- specialized reasoning
- practical application
They work together.
---
# Permanent Architecture Rule
The strength of Spark comes from:
Deep specialized intelligence + clear ownership + intelligent collaboration.
Each Chamber Member should become a trusted domain intelligence while remaining part of the larger ecosystem.
