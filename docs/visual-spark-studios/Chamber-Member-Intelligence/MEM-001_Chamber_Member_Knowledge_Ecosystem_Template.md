# MEM-001 Chamber Member Knowledge Ecosystem Template
Defines the structure, standards, and quality requirements for building the deep knowledge ecosystems behind every Chamber Member Intelligence Library.
---
# Authority
This document is the authoritative template for creating Chamber Member knowledge ecosystems.
Every Chamber Member must use this structure when developing its knowledge foundation.
---
# Purpose
A Chamber Member is not a simple expert role.
A Chamber Member represents a specialized intelligence domain supported by:
- accumulated knowledge
- proven frameworks
- research
- expert perspectives
- practical methods
- real-world application
The purpose of the Knowledge Ecosystem is to create deep, reliable, and adaptable intelligence.
---
# Core Principle
The goal is not to collect information.
The goal is to organize wisdom into usable intelligence.
---
# Knowledge Ecosystem Structure
Every Chamber Member Knowledge Ecosystem contains the following layers:
---
# Layer 1 — Domain Foundation
Defines the field itself.
Includes:
- history of the domain
- foundational concepts
- terminology
- major schools of thought
- accepted principles
- core challenges
Purpose:
Understand the landscape before applying knowledge.
---
# Layer 2 — Expert Knowledge Network
Defines the people and organizations that contribute to the field.
Experts are organized by contribution, not popularity.
---
# Expert Categories
## Foundational Thinkers
Purpose:
Identify individuals who shaped the field.
Include:
- major theories
- foundational concepts
- lasting contributions
---
## Research Experts
Purpose:
Understand evidence and findings.
Include:
- researchers
- academic contributors
- studies
- institutions
---
## Framework Creators
Purpose:
Identify practical models and methodologies.
Include:
- systems
- processes
- models
- approaches
---
## Current Thought Leaders
Purpose:
Understand modern developments.
Include:
- current practitioners
- evolving approaches
- contemporary applications
---
## Industry Practitioners
Purpose:
Understand real-world application.
Include:
- successful operators
- experienced professionals
- proven practitioners
---
## Alternative Perspectives
Purpose:
Avoid narrow thinking.
Include:
- different approaches
- competing viewpoints
- contrasting methods
Alternative perspectives must still meet quality standards.
---
# Expert Knowledge Entry Format
Every expert or knowledge source should include:
## Source
Name of:
- person
- organization
- research body
- methodology
- framework
---
## Contribution
What knowledge does this source provide?
Examples:
- theory
- framework
- method
- research
- practical application
---
## Applicable Situations
When is this knowledge useful?
---
## Strengths
What does this approach contribute?
---
## Limitations
Where should this approach be adapted or questioned?
---
## Related Knowledge
What other ideas connect with this source?
---
# Layer 3 — Framework Library
Every Chamber Member should maintain a collection of relevant frameworks.
Each framework includes:
## Framework Name
---
## Creator or Source
---
## Purpose
What problem does it solve?
---
## Core Components
What are the major parts?
---
## Application Situations
When should it be used?
---
## Limitations
Where may it not fit?
---
## Adaptation Considerations
How might Spark personalize it?
---
# Layer 4 — Method Library
Defines practical methods used within the domain.
Includes:
- processes
- approaches
- techniques
- workflows
- best practices
Each method includes:
- purpose
- steps
- appropriate use
- limitations
---
# Layer 5 — Research Foundation
Includes:
- academic research
- evidence-based findings
- established studies
- recognized organizations
Research should be used to improve understanding and application.
---
# Layer 6 — Practical Application Library
Defines how knowledge becomes useful.
Includes:
- examples
- scenarios
- case applications
- templates
- checklists
- exercises
---
# Layer 7 — Emerging Knowledge
Tracks:
- new developments
- changing practices
- evolving technology
- new research
Emerging knowledge must be clearly separated from established knowledge.
---
# Knowledge Quality Standards
All knowledge sources should be evaluated.
---
# Approved Sources
Priority should be given to:
## Research-Based Knowledge
Including:
- peer-reviewed research
- academic institutions
- established studies
---
## Recognized Experts
Experts should demonstrate:
- expertise
- meaningful contribution
- credibility
- practical application
---
## Proven Frameworks
Frameworks should have:
- clear methodology
- transparent principles
- practical usefulness
---
## Established Professional Practices
Including:
- industry standards
- recognized organizations
- accepted methods
---
# Excluded Knowledge Standards
Chamber Members should not use unsupported or unreliable systems as authoritative foundations.
Examples include:
## Fortune-Telling Systems
Do not use:
- predictions
- psychic claims
- guaranteed future outcomes
- mind-reading claims
---
## Astrology as a Decision System
Do not use astrology for:
- personality determination
- business decisions
- predictions
- recommendations
---
## Supernatural Claims
Do not use claims involving:
- magical outcomes
- hidden powers
- unverifiable abilities
---
## Manipulative Systems
Avoid approaches based on:
- deception
- coercion
- exploitation
- fear-based influence
---
## Unsupported Claims
Avoid treating as authoritative:
- guaranteed formulas
- secret methods
- viral claims without evidence
---
# Important Distinction
Spark may understand cultural, historical, or personal beliefs.
However:
Understanding a belief system does not mean using it as an authoritative reasoning framework.
---
# ADHD Adaptation Requirement
Every Chamber Member should consider:
How does this domain interact with:
- executive demands
- cognitive load
- attention differences
- overwhelm
- energy variation
- need for appropriate structure
Executive Function Intelligence provides the cognitive foundation.
---
# Personalization Requirement
Knowledge must be adaptable.
Spark should consider:
- user's goals
- experience level
- preferences
- circumstances
- previous successful approaches
Knowledge should improve fit.
It should not create labels.
---
# Knowledge Integration Standard
A Chamber Member should be able to answer:
## What does the field know?
Knowledge foundation.
## How does the field think?
Frameworks and reasoning.
## How is it applied?
Methods and examples.
## When does it fit?
Situational judgment.
## How should Spark adapt it?
Personalization.
---
# Permanent Architecture Rule
A Chamber Member Knowledge Ecosystem is not a collection of experts.
It is an organized intelligence system built from credible knowledge, practical frameworks, and adaptable application.
