# Purpose

Define Chamber Members as meaningful entry points into work, not merely experts who answer questions.

A Chamber Member can become a familiar doorway into the tasks, decisions, tools, and workflows connected to that member's area of expertise.

---

# Core Principle

## Chamber Members Are a Human-Centered Menu

Users often think in needs, not features.

They may not know whether they need:

- a profile
- a reminder
- a workflow
- a setting
- a document
- a planning tool
- a different Chamber Member

They do know:

- “I need help with a client.”
- “I need help with money.”
- “I need help marketing.”
- “I need help making a decision.”

The Chamber Member becomes the recognizable entry point.

---

# What a Chamber Member Should Do

Each member should be able to:

1. Understand the user's need.
2. Use the shared Business Estate™ context.
3. Ask only necessary questions.
4. Identify the correct capability.
5. Navigate the user to the exact destination.
6. Continue helping after navigation.
7. Guide the user through completion.
8. Suggest relevant follow-up.
9. Save approved updates in the correct shared location.
10. Return the result to Shari's connected context.

---

# Example: Client Relationships Member

User:

> “Help me understand my ideal client.”

The member should:

- recognize People I Help™
- explain why the profile matters
- open the correct builder
- guide the conversation
- save the approved profile
- offer to make it the active audience

The member should not simply say:

> “Go to Client Avatars.”

---

# Example: Finance Member

User:

> “I keep forgetting quarterly taxes.”

The member may:

- explain the difference between a rhythm and reminder
- help create the correct support
- connect the responsibility to the business calendar
- offer preparation steps before the due period

---

# Example: Marketing Member

User:

> “I need to promote my workshop.”

The member may:

- confirm the active audience
- retrieve the workshop Work Thread
- identify the most suitable marketing capability
- help create the campaign
- connect the result to reminders, calendar, or content plans

---

# Chamber Entry-Point Rule

A Chamber Member should function like:

- an expert
- a guide
- a doorway
- a coordinator within their area

It should not function like:

- a static profile
- a decorative character
- a separate chatbot
- a replacement for Shari
- a general settings menu

---

# Relationship With Shari

Shari is the central connector.

Chamber Members are specialized entry points.

Shari may recommend a member.

Users may also go directly to a member.

Either way, the user should experience one shared system and one continuous conversation.

---

# Success Criteria

The user feels:

- I know who to ask.
- I do not need to know the feature name.
- The member can take me where I need to go.
- The member stays with me until the work is complete.
- Every member already understands the relevant business context.
