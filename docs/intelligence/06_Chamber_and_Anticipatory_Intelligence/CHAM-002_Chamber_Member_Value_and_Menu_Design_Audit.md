# Purpose

Audit every Chamber Member to determine whether the member provides meaningful expertise and serves as a useful entry point into getting work done.

---

# Audit Questions

For each Chamber Member, document:

## Identity

- Member name
- Primary area of expertise
- User problems the member solves
- Why the user would choose this member

## Entry-Point Value

- What natural-language needs should route here?
- What common outcomes can begin here?
- What capabilities can the member open?
- What actions can the member perform?
- What workflows can the member guide?

## Shared Context

- Which parts of My Business Estate™ should the member read?
- Which People I Help™ profiles matter?
- Which values, goals, projects, or decisions matter?
- What context should the member not use?

## Collaboration

- Which other members may contribute?
- When should Shari coordinate?
- How is the user protected from being bounced between members?

## Completion

- What does a completed interaction look like?
- What information may be saved?
- What follow-up might matter next?
- Where can the user review the result?

## Risk

- Does this member duplicate another member?
- Is the expertise too broad?
- Is the member mostly decorative?
- Does the member create an extra decision?
- Could the capability be better accessed through Shari alone?

---

# Member Classification

Each member should be classified as:

## Core Entry Point

Frequently used and connected to several capabilities.

## Specialist Entry Point

Used for a narrower but important expertise area.

## Supporting Advisor

Adds a perspective but rarely owns a full workflow.

## Merge Candidate

Overlaps heavily with another member.

## Remove or Repurpose

Does not create clear user value.

---

# Menu Design Principle

The Chamber should not display every possible function.

Each member represents a meaningful category of need.

The user chooses the person who seems most relevant, and the member handles the internal routing.

---

# Founder Decision Output

The audit should produce:

- members to keep
- members to strengthen
- members to merge
- members to repurpose
- missing expertise
- capability connections to add
- routing tests to create
- menu simplifications

---

# Success Criteria

The Chamber becomes:

- easier to understand
- more useful
- less decorative
- less overwhelming
- a genuine pathway from question to completion
