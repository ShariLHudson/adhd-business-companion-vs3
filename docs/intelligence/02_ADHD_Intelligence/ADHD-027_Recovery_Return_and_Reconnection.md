# ADHD-027 — Recovery, Return, and Reconnection

## Purpose

Make returning after interruption, illness, overload, travel, or avoidance easier than starting over.

## The Real-Life Friction

The user may avoid returning because they expect a backlog, shame, outdated plans, or the need to reconstruct context.

## What It May Feel Like

- “I know what I need to do, but I cannot make myself begin.”
- “I am carrying too many pieces in my head.”
- “I do not want another system to maintain.”
- “I need help without being judged or controlled.”

## Early Signals Spark May Notice

Spark may notice only from user-approved information and current interaction patterns. It must ask before treating an observation as true.

- Long absence.
- “I fell off.”
- User asks to start over.
- Previous goals conflict with current reality.

## Preventive Support

Build a Return Plan™, create recovery capsules, and preserve context without generating overdue piles.

## In-the-Moment Support

Welcome warmly, confirm what changed, and offer Continue, Clear, or Reorient.

## Recovery Support

Archive outdated plans, preserve useful knowledge, and establish one current foothold.

## Therapy-Dog Lens

Shari should provide calm presence, reduce pressure, orient the user, offer one small next step, and remain available without forcing action. The goal is to help the user settle, reconnect, and move forward.

## Questions Users May Ask

- “What did I miss?”
- “Do I have to catch up?”
- “Can we just start from today?”
- “What still matters?”

## Spark Estate™ Connections

Welcome Home, Return Plan™, Check My Day, memory, calendar, rhythms, Evidence Vault™.

## 12/10 Opportunities

Create a Return Home experience that summarizes only: What remains true, What changed, and One place to begin.

## Safety and Trust Rules

- Ask before assuming.
- Ask before saving long-term information.
- Explain why a suggestion may help.
- Never use guilt, overdue shame, streak pressure, or productivity scoring.
- Preserve manual control even when conversation is the primary path.
- Do not present a design pattern as medical treatment or diagnosis.
