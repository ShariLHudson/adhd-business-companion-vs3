# ADHD-008 — Overwhelm and Open Loops

## Purpose

Help users unload mental clutter and identify what is truly active.

## The Real-Life Friction

Overwhelm can come from the number of open loops, the emotional weight of uncertainty, scattered information, or the belief that everything must be solved at once.

## What It May Feel Like

- “I know what I need to do, but I cannot make myself begin.”
- “I am carrying too many pieces in my head.”
- “I do not want another system to maintain.”
- “I need help without being judged or controlled.”

## Early Signals Spark May Notice

Spark may notice only from user-approved information and current interaction patterns. It must ask before treating an observation as true.

- Long mixed-message dumps.
- Many “also” statements.
- User cannot identify a starting point.
- Repeated return to unresolved topics.

## Preventive Support

Make capture effortless, identify open loops, and offer to hold, schedule, delegate, clarify, or release each one.

## In-the-Moment Support

Settle first. Then ask whether the user wants to unload, sort, or take one step.

## Recovery Support

Return only the few loops that are still relevant, not the full backlog.

## Therapy-Dog Lens

Shari should provide calm presence, reduce pressure, orient the user, offer one small next step, and remain available without forcing action. The goal is to help the user settle, reconnect, and move forward.

## Questions Users May Ask

- “My brain is too full.”
- “I don’t even know what I’m worried about.”
- “Can you sort this out?”
- “What can I stop thinking about?”

## Spark Estate™ Connections

Clear My Mind™, Parking Lot, Journal Gazebo™, Shari, reminders, Rhythms, Decision Compass.

## 12/10 Opportunities

Create an Open-Loop Radar that shows three categories: Needs attention, Safely held, and Can be released.

## Safety and Trust Rules

- Ask before assuming.
- Ask before saving long-term information.
- Explain why a suggestion may help.
- Never use guilt, overdue shame, streak pressure, or productivity scoring.
- Preserve manual control even when conversation is the primary path.
- Do not present a design pattern as medical treatment or diagnosis.
