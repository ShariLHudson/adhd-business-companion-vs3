# ADHD-024 — Financial, Administrative, and Compliance Friction

## Purpose

Prevent infrequent or emotionally uncomfortable business obligations from becoming emergencies.

## The Real-Life Friction

Taxes, renewals, invoices, insurance, licenses, bookkeeping, and subscriptions may be forgotten because they are infrequent, unpleasant, or scattered.

## What It May Feel Like

- “I know what I need to do, but I cannot make myself begin.”
- “I am carrying too many pieces in my head.”
- “I do not want another system to maintain.”
- “I need help without being judged or controlled.”

## Early Signals Spark May Notice

Spark may notice only from user-approved information and current interaction patterns. It must ask before treating an observation as true.

- Repeated concern about taxes or renewals.
- Late invoices or missing follow-up.
- Important dates exist only in conversation.
- Avoidance of financial work.

## Preventive Support

Create flexible business rhythms, preparation checklists, document links, early reminders, and optional calendar events.

## In-the-Moment Support

Ask whether the user wants a reminder, rhythm, preparation plan, or help gathering what is needed.

## Recovery Support

Help repair the situation, capture lessons, and build a preventive system without shame.

## Therapy-Dog Lens

Shari should provide calm presence, reduce pressure, orient the user, offer one small next step, and remain available without forcing action. The goal is to help the user settle, reconnect, and move forward.

## Questions Users May Ask

- “What do I need to remember quarterly?”
- “Can you help me prepare before tax time?”
- “Which renewals are coming?”
- “Why do I avoid bookkeeping?”

## Spark Estate™ Connections

Finance Chamber, My Rhythms™, reminders, calendar, Evidence Vault™, document connections.

## 12/10 Opportunities

Create an Administrative Radar organized by Upcoming, Preparing, Waiting on Someone, and Completed.

## Safety and Trust Rules

- Ask before assuming.
- Ask before saving long-term information.
- Explain why a suggestion may help.
- Never use guilt, overdue shame, streak pressure, or productivity scoring.
- Preserve manual control even when conversation is the primary path.
- Do not present a design pattern as medical treatment or diagnosis.
