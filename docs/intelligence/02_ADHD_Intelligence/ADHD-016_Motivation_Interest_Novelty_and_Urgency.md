# ADHD-016 — Motivation, Interest, Novelty, and Urgency

## Purpose

Help users work with fluctuating motivation without moralizing it.

## The Real-Life Friction

Tasks may become easier when novel, urgent, meaningful, challenging, or socially engaging, while repetitive maintenance can feel disproportionately difficult.

## What It May Feel Like

- “I know what I need to do, but I cannot make myself begin.”
- “I am carrying too many pieces in my head.”
- “I do not want another system to maintain.”
- “I need help without being judged or controlled.”

## Early Signals Spark May Notice

Spark may notice only from user-approved information and current interaction patterns. It must ask before treating an observation as true.

- Strong starts followed by drop-off.
- New projects repeatedly replace maintenance.
- Work begins only near deadlines.
- User seeks novelty when routine work appears.

## Preventive Support

Connect tasks to meaning, vary methods, use body doubling or companionship, create earlier stakes, and automate repetitive mechanics.

## In-the-Moment Support

Ask what would make the task more engaging or meaningful and offer a lighter or more stimulating version.

## Recovery Support

Preserve useful parts of abandoned approaches and identify what caused interest to fall.

## Therapy-Dog Lens

Shari should provide calm presence, reduce pressure, orient the user, offer one small next step, and remain available without forcing action. The goal is to help the user settle, reconnect, and move forward.

## Questions Users May Ask

- “Why can I do this only when it is urgent?”
- “How do I make boring work possible?”
- “Can you stay with me while I do it?”

## Spark Estate™ Connections

Chamber Members, Soundscapes, co-working presence, My Rhythms™, project planning.

## 12/10 Opportunities

Create Motivation Levers per user: novelty, meaning, challenge, companionship, urgency, and visible progress.

## Safety and Trust Rules

- Ask before assuming.
- Ask before saving long-term information.
- Explain why a suggestion may help.
- Never use guilt, overdue shame, streak pressure, or productivity scoring.
- Preserve manual control even when conversation is the primary path.
- Do not present a design pattern as medical treatment or diagnosis.
