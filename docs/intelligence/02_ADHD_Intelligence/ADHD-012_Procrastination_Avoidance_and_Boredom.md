# ADHD-012 — Procrastination, Avoidance, and Boredom

## Purpose

Distinguish between inability, uncertainty, emotional avoidance, low stimulation, and strategic delay.

## The Real-Life Friction

A task may be delayed because it is unclear, boring, emotionally uncomfortable, too large, low-value, or poorly timed—not because the user does not care.

## What It May Feel Like

- “I know what I need to do, but I cannot make myself begin.”
- “I am carrying too many pieces in my head.”
- “I do not want another system to maintain.”
- “I need help without being judged or controlled.”

## Early Signals Spark May Notice

Spark may notice only from user-approved information and current interaction patterns. It must ask before treating an observation as true.

- Repeated rescheduling.
- Excessive preparation.
- Sudden interest in unrelated tasks.
- Strong negative language about a routine activity.

## Preventive Support

Clarify purpose, reduce scope, pair boring work with stimulation, automate mechanics, or question whether the task belongs at all.

## In-the-Moment Support

Ask what feels hard: unclear, boring, uncomfortable, too big, or not worth doing. Then adapt.

## Recovery Support

Review the design of the task, not the character of the user.

## Therapy-Dog Lens

Shari should provide calm presence, reduce pressure, orient the user, offer one small next step, and remain available without forcing action. The goal is to help the user settle, reconnect, and move forward.

## Questions Users May Ask

- “Why do I avoid simple things?”
- “Can you make this less boring?”
- “Can you do the setup?”
- “Do I even need to do this?”

## Spark Estate™ Connections

Shari, Chamber Members, Soundscapes, templates, automation, reminders, Rhythms.

## 12/10 Opportunities

Add a Friction Diagnosis prompt with five plain-language choices and a tailored response for each.

## Safety and Trust Rules

- Ask before assuming.
- Ask before saving long-term information.
- Explain why a suggestion may help.
- Never use guilt, overdue shame, streak pressure, or productivity scoring.
- Preserve manual control even when conversation is the primary path.
- Do not present a design pattern as medical treatment or diagnosis.
