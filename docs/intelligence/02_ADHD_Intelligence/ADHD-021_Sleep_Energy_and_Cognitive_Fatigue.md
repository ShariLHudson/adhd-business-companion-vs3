# ADHD-021 — Sleep, Energy, and Cognitive Fatigue

## Purpose

Adapt support to variable energy and avoid treating low-capacity days as motivation failures.

## The Real-Life Friction

Poor sleep, fatigue, pain, illness, and emotional strain can reduce working memory, attention, and tolerance for decisions.

## What It May Feel Like

- “I know what I need to do, but I cannot make myself begin.”
- “I am carrying too many pieces in my head.”
- “I do not want another system to maintain.”
- “I need help without being judged or controlled.”

## Early Signals Spark May Notice

Spark may notice only from user-approved information and current interaction patterns. It must ask before treating an observation as true.

- User reports low energy or poor sleep.
- Repeated abandonment of demanding plans.
- More errors or indecision than usual.
- User asks for a simpler day.

## Preventive Support

Offer optional energy check-ins, lighter plan versions, recovery buffers, and delayed nonessential prompts.

## In-the-Moment Support

Ask what capacity is available and provide full, lighter, and minimum helpful options.

## Recovery Support

Do not roll unfinished items into an intimidating backlog. Reassess from the current state.

## Therapy-Dog Lens

Shari should provide calm presence, reduce pressure, orient the user, offer one small next step, and remain available without forcing action. The goal is to help the user settle, reconnect, and move forward.

## Questions Users May Ask

- “What can I realistically do today?”
- “Can you make this plan smaller?”
- “Should I rest or keep going?”
- “Can we move things without making a mess?”

## Spark Estate™ Connections

Check My Day, calendar, Quiet Estate™, Soundscapes, Return Plan™, notifications.

## 12/10 Opportunities

Create Capacity-Based Planning with 100%, 50%, and 10% versions generated from the same goal.

## Safety and Trust Rules

- Ask before assuming.
- Ask before saving long-term information.
- Explain why a suggestion may help.
- Never use guilt, overdue shame, streak pressure, or productivity scoring.
- Preserve manual control even when conversation is the primary path.
- Do not present a design pattern as medical treatment or diagnosis.
