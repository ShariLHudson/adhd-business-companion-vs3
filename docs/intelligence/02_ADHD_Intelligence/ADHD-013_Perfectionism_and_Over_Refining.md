# ADHD-013 — Perfectionism and Over-Refining

## Purpose

Help users move from endless refinement to a useful, values-aligned version.

## The Real-Life Friction

Fear of judgment, unclear standards, or difficulty defining “done” can keep work in revision indefinitely.

## What It May Feel Like

- “I know what I need to do, but I cannot make myself begin.”
- “I am carrying too many pieces in my head.”
- “I do not want another system to maintain.”
- “I need help without being judged or controlled.”

## Early Signals Spark May Notice

Spark may notice only from user-approved information and current interaction patterns. It must ask before treating an observation as true.

- Repeated small changes.
- Requesting many versions without selecting.
- Delaying release because something is “not ready.”
- Expanding scope near completion.

## Preventive Support

Define the purpose, audience, minimum quality, review point, and what would justify another revision.

## In-the-Moment Support

Offer Good Enough, Strong, and Polished versions. Ask what level the situation actually needs.

## Recovery Support

If a release is delayed, preserve learning and create a smaller test rather than framing it as failure.

## Therapy-Dog Lens

Shari should provide calm presence, reduce pressure, orient the user, offer one small next step, and remain available without forcing action. The goal is to help the user settle, reconnect, and move forward.

## Questions Users May Ask

- “How do I know when it is done?”
- “Is this good enough?”
- “What if people judge it?”
- “Can we make a smaller version first?”

## Spark Estate™ Connections

Creation tools, People I Help™, values, Business Lens, Chamber Members, Evidence Vault™.

## 12/10 Opportunities

Create a Definition of Done memory for recurring work types.

## Safety and Trust Rules

- Ask before assuming.
- Ask before saving long-term information.
- Explain why a suggestion may help.
- Never use guilt, overdue shame, streak pressure, or productivity scoring.
- Preserve manual control even when conversation is the primary path.
- Do not present a design pattern as medical treatment or diagnosis.
