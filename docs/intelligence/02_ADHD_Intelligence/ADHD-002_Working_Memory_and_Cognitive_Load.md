# ADHD-002 — Working Memory and Cognitive Load

## Purpose

Define how Spark reduces the need to hold multiple intentions, details, decisions, and relationships in mind.

## The Real-Life Friction

The user may forget intentions, lose the thread of a task, repeat explanations, or depend on scattered notes. Cognitive load grows when information lives across calendars, emails, project tools, documents, and conversations.

## What It May Feel Like

- “I know what I need to do, but I cannot make myself begin.”
- “I am carrying too many pieces in my head.”
- “I do not want another system to maintain.”
- “I need help without being judged or controlled.”

## Early Signals Spark May Notice

Spark may notice only from user-approved information and current interaction patterns. It must ask before treating an observation as true.

- Repeated re-explaining.
- “What was I doing?”
- Multiple unsorted captures.
- Forgotten commitments or reasons behind decisions.
- User asks where information lives.

## Preventive Support

Preserve context, create decision memory, connect related work, summarize before transitions, and let Shari offer to remember or organize information.

## In-the-Moment Support

Show “what I already know,” reconstruct the current thread, and offer one clear continuation point.

## Recovery Support

Provide a recovery capsule containing where the user stopped, why it mattered, the last decision, and the smallest next step.

## Therapy-Dog Lens

Shari should provide calm presence, reduce pressure, orient the user, offer one small next step, and remain available without forcing action. The goal is to help the user settle, reconnect, and move forward.

## Questions Users May Ask

- “What did we decide?”
- “Why did I choose this?”
- “Where did I put that?”
- “Can you remember this for me?”

## Spark Estate™ Connections

Memory, Clear My Mind™, Decision Compass, Business Estate™, People I Help™, project context, reminders, Estate Concierge.

## 12/10 Opportunities

Create a Connected Memory view that shows what Spark remembers, why it matters, and where it is used—without becoming a database the user must maintain.

## Safety and Trust Rules

- Ask before assuming.
- Ask before saving long-term information.
- Explain why a suggestion may help.
- Never use guilt, overdue shame, streak pressure, or productivity scoring.
- Preserve manual control even when conversation is the primary path.
- Do not present a design pattern as medical treatment or diagnosis.
