# ADHD-014 — Shame, Self-Criticism, and Negative History

## Purpose

Prevent the app from reinforcing years of criticism, missed expectations, or internalized failure.

## The Real-Life Friction

Many adults diagnosed later in life report histories of being seen as careless, lazy, inconsistent, or underperforming. App language can easily repeat that harm.

## What It May Feel Like

- “I know what I need to do, but I cannot make myself begin.”
- “I am carrying too many pieces in my head.”
- “I do not want another system to maintain.”
- “I need help without being judged or controlled.”

## Early Signals Spark May Notice

Spark may notice only from user-approved information and current interaction patterns. It must ask before treating an observation as true.

- “I always fail.”
- User avoids returning after a gap.
- Apologizing for not using the app.
- Minimizing accomplishments.
- Fear of being judged by the system.

## Preventive Support

Use no-shame language, celebrate evidence, normalize return, and avoid streaks or overdue scoring.

## In-the-Moment Support

Separate the current problem from identity. Offer relief and one reversible step.

## Recovery Support

Use the Evidence Vault™ and Return Plan™ to show continuity and past capability.

## Therapy-Dog Lens

Shari should provide calm presence, reduce pressure, orient the user, offer one small next step, and remain available without forcing action. The goal is to help the user settle, reconnect, and move forward.

## Questions Users May Ask

- “Are you going to show everything I missed?”
- “Why do I feel guilty opening the app?”
- “Can we start over without deleting everything?”

## Spark Estate™ Connections

Evidence Vault™, Welcome Home, Return Plan™, Shari personality, notifications.

## 12/10 Opportunities

Build a Shame-Safe UX review that scans copy for blame, urgency, and failure framing.

## Safety and Trust Rules

- Ask before assuming.
- Ask before saving long-term information.
- Explain why a suggestion may help.
- Never use guilt, overdue shame, streak pressure, or productivity scoring.
- Preserve manual control even when conversation is the primary path.
- Do not present a design pattern as medical treatment or diagnosis.
