# ADHD-004 — Transitions and Task Switching

## Purpose

Reduce the cognitive and emotional cost of moving between activities, roles, rooms, meetings, and states.

## The Real-Life Friction

ADHD friction often occurs not only during work but between activities. A person may lose context, resist stopping, forget follow-ups, or need recovery time after meetings or intense focus.

## What It May Feel Like

- “I know what I need to do, but I cannot make myself begin.”
- “I am carrying too many pieces in my head.”
- “I do not want another system to maintain.”
- “I need help without being judged or controlled.”

## Early Signals Spark May Notice

Spark may notice only from user-approved information and current interaction patterns. It must ask before treating an observation as true.

- User stays in one activity far longer than intended.
- Frequent “What was next?”
- Meetings end without captured decisions.
- Difficulty resuming after an interruption.
- Calendar items are scheduled back-to-back.

## Preventive Support

Add buffers, pre-transition prompts, meeting closeouts, context snapshots, and next-activity preparation—with permission.

## In-the-Moment Support

Offer: capture decisions, save the stopping point, prepare the next activity, take a short reset, or continue intentionally.

## Recovery Support

Restore the last stable context and ask what changed during the interruption.

## Therapy-Dog Lens

Shari should provide calm presence, reduce pressure, orient the user, offer one small next step, and remain available without forcing action. The goal is to help the user settle, reconnect, and move forward.

## Questions Users May Ask

- “How do I stop without losing my place?”
- “Why is switching so exhausting?”
- “Can you prepare me for the next thing?”
- “What did I miss after that interruption?”

## Spark Estate™ Connections

Calendar Concierge, time blocks, Soundscapes, breathing, project context, reminders, Return Plan™, Check My Day.

## 12/10 Opportunities

Create Transition Cards that appear only at meaningful boundaries: Before, Close, Reset, and Resume.

## Safety and Trust Rules

- Ask before assuming.
- Ask before saving long-term information.
- Explain why a suggestion may help.
- Never use guilt, overdue shame, streak pressure, or productivity scoring.
- Preserve manual control even when conversation is the primary path.
- Do not present a design pattern as medical treatment or diagnosis.
