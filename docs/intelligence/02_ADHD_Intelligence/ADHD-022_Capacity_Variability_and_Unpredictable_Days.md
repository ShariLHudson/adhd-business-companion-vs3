# ADHD-022 — Capacity Variability and Unpredictable Days

## Purpose

Design for changing mental, emotional, physical, and logistical capacity.

## The Real-Life Friction

A plan that fit yesterday may not fit today. Rigid plans can create shame and make the system feel unreliable.

## What It May Feel Like

- “I know what I need to do, but I cannot make myself begin.”
- “I am carrying too many pieces in my head.”
- “I do not want another system to maintain.”
- “I need help without being judged or controlled.”

## Early Signals Spark May Notice

Spark may notice only from user-approved information and current interaction patterns. It must ask before treating an observation as true.

- User frequently changes plans because of energy, appointments, caregiving, pain, or unexpected events.
- “Today is not going as planned.”
- Repeated need to reschedule.

## Preventive Support

Make plans modular, preserve the goal while changing the size, and identify what can be postponed without harm.

## In-the-Moment Support

Ask: “Do you want a lighter day, a different kind of work, or help protecting only one essential thing?”

## Recovery Support

Rebuild from current capacity without treating adaptation as failure.

## Therapy-Dog Lens

Shari should provide calm presence, reduce pressure, orient the user, offer one small next step, and remain available without forcing action. The goal is to help the user settle, reconnect, and move forward.

## Questions Users May Ask

- “Can the plan adapt without starting over?”
- “What is the minimum that still matters?”
- “Can you help me make room for this unexpected thing?”

## Spark Estate™ Connections

Check My Day, Calendar Concierge, Rhythms, Return Plan™, project planning.

## 12/10 Opportunities

Build Adaptive Day Versions that can be switched instantly without losing the original plan.

## Safety and Trust Rules

- Ask before assuming.
- Ask before saving long-term information.
- Explain why a suggestion may help.
- Never use guilt, overdue shame, streak pressure, or productivity scoring.
- Preserve manual control even when conversation is the primary path.
- Do not present a design pattern as medical treatment or diagnosis.
