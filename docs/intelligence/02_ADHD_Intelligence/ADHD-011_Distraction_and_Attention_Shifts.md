# ADHD-011 — Distraction and Attention Shifts

## Purpose

Support attention without treating every shift as failure.

## The Real-Life Friction

Attention may move toward novelty, urgency, emotion, sensory input, or a new idea. Distraction can also signal unclear work, unmet needs, or a necessary change.

## What It May Feel Like

- “I know what I need to do, but I cannot make myself begin.”
- “I am carrying too many pieces in my head.”
- “I do not want another system to maintain.”
- “I need help without being judged or controlled.”

## Early Signals Spark May Notice

Spark may notice only from user-approved information and current interaction patterns. It must ask before treating an observation as true.

- Frequent topic shifts.
- New ideas appear during a focused task.
- User opens multiple rooms or workflows rapidly.
- Repeated requests to restart.

## Preventive Support

Keep the next step visible, provide quick idea capture, reduce visual clutter, and let users choose a focus environment.

## In-the-Moment Support

Capture the distraction safely and ask whether to return, intentionally switch, or reconsider the plan.

## Recovery Support

Restore the previous thread and explain what was preserved.

## Therapy-Dog Lens

Shari should provide calm presence, reduce pressure, orient the user, offer one small next step, and remain available without forcing action. The goal is to help the user settle, reconnect, and move forward.

## Questions Users May Ask

- “How do I stop chasing every idea?”
- “Can you hold this thought without making me lose my place?”
- “Was that a distraction or a better direction?”

## Spark Estate™ Connections

Clear My Mind™, Parking Lot, focus audio, minimal mode, project context, time blocks.

## 12/10 Opportunities

Create a One-Tap Detour Capture that returns the user to the exact previous context.

## Safety and Trust Rules

- Ask before assuming.
- Ask before saving long-term information.
- Explain why a suggestion may help.
- Never use guilt, overdue shame, streak pressure, or productivity scoring.
- Preserve manual control even when conversation is the primary path.
- Do not present a design pattern as medical treatment or diagnosis.
