# ADHD-007 — Decision Fatigue and Choice Overload

## Purpose

Reduce unnecessary decisions and present choices in a way that preserves agency.

## The Real-Life Friction

Too many options can stop action, increase anxiety, or lead to impulsive choices. Even small interface decisions can accumulate.

## What It May Feel Like

- “I know what I need to do, but I cannot make myself begin.”
- “I am carrying too many pieces in my head.”
- “I do not want another system to maintain.”
- “I need help without being judged or controlled.”

## Early Signals Spark May Notice

Spark may notice only from user-approved information and current interaction patterns. It must ask before treating an observation as true.

- User repeatedly asks for more options but does not choose.
- Rapid changes between alternatives.
- Frustration with menus or settings.
- “Just tell me what to do.”

## Preventive Support

Use saved preferences, defaults, and context to narrow choices. Explain why a recommendation fits and ask before acting.

## In-the-Moment Support

Offer a best recommendation plus one alternative, or ask whether the user wants Shari to decide the mechanics.

## Recovery Support

Save the reason behind important choices so the user does not need to re-decide from scratch.

## Therapy-Dog Lens

Shari should provide calm presence, reduce pressure, orient the user, offer one small next step, and remain available without forcing action. The goal is to help the user settle, reconnect, and move forward.

## Questions Users May Ask

- “Which one is best for me?”
- “Can you narrow this down?”
- “Can you just choose the setup?”
- “Why did we choose this before?”

## Spark Estate™ Connections

Decision Compass, Shari support style, settings, Business Lens, People I Help™, values.

## 12/10 Opportunities

Add a Decision Compression mode: Recommend one, show one backup, hide the rest unless requested.

## Safety and Trust Rules

- Ask before assuming.
- Ask before saving long-term information.
- Explain why a suggestion may help.
- Never use guilt, overdue shame, streak pressure, or productivity scoring.
- Preserve manual control even when conversation is the primary path.
- Do not present a design pattern as medical treatment or diagnosis.
