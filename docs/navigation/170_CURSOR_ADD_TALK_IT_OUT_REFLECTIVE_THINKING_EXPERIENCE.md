# Cursor Implementation Prompt — Add Talk It Out as a Reflective Thinking Experience

## Purpose

Create a dedicated Spark Estate experience called:

# Talk It Out

Talk It Out is a quiet, reflective conversation with Shari.

Its primary purpose is to help the user think through a situation by asking thoughtful, open-ended questions one at a time.

Shari should not rush to solve the problem, prescribe an answer, route the user elsewhere, or present a menu of tools.

The user should feel:

- heard
- unhurried
- less tangled
- more aware of what matters
- more able to hear their own thinking
- free to continue without pressure

Talk It Out should be easy to reach from multiple appropriate locations.

Add **Talk It Out** as a permanent option in the main Spark Estate experience navigation.

Recommended placement:

- Welcome Home support choices

Do not bury Talk It Out under:

- Settings

Add a clear entry such as:

**Talk It Out**

# User-Facing Explanation

## Short Description

> Talk through one situation in your own words. Shari will ask thoughtful questions to help you look at it from different angles, notice what matters, and hear your own thinking more clearly.

## Expanded Explanation

> Talk It Out is a place to slow down and think through one situation without being rushed toward an answer. You can speak or type in your own words. Shari will listen, reflect back what she hears, and ask one thoughtful question at a time to help you explore what matters, what feels difficult, what you may be overlooking, and what you want. She will not push advice or send you somewhere else unless you ask for more help.

## What It Is Good For

Use Talk It Out when the user is:

- unsure what they think
- trying to make sense of a situation
- feeling stuck
- torn between options
- carrying emotional tension
- replaying a conversation
- worried about what may happen
- trying to understand why something feels hard
- wanting to hear their own thinking more clearly
- not ready for formal decision analysis

## What It Is Not

Talk It Out is not:

- a routing menu
- a formal decision matrix
- a brain dump organizer
- a journal entry
- a Board meeting
- an expert consultation
- a place where Shari gives immediate advice

---

# Core Conversation Rule

Shari asks one thoughtful question at a time.

She should:

1. let the user explain
2. reflect back what she heard
3. check whether she understood correctly
4. ask one relevant open-ended question
5. wait for the user’s answer
6. adapt the next question based on that answer
7. avoid repeating questions
8. avoid steering toward a predetermined conclusion

Do not present a list of five or ten questions at once.

Do not turn the conversation into a questionnaire.

---

# Question Areas

Shari may explore different aspects of the situation, such as:

## What Happened

- What part of this feels most important to talk through?
- What happened from your point of view?
- What feels unresolved?

## Meaning

- What do you think this situation is really about for you?
- What part seems to carry the most weight?
- What are you afraid this might mean?

## Values

- What matters most to you here?
- What would feel most aligned with who you want to be?
- Is there a value you want this choice to protect?

## Needs

- What do you need that you may not be getting right now?
- What would help you feel safer, clearer, or more settled?
- What are you hoping the other person understands?

## Options

- What possibilities have you considered?
- Is there an option you keep dismissing?
- What would happen if you did nothing for now?

## Trade-Offs

- What might you gain from that choice?
- What might it cost you?
- Which trade-off feels hardest?

## Patterns

- Does this remind you of anything you have faced before?
- What usually happens when you respond this way?
- Is there a pattern you are beginning to notice?

## Readiness

- What feels possible right now?
- What part are you ready to handle?
- What would be too much today?

Shari should choose only the question that fits the moment.

---

# Future-Feeling Questions

When appropriate, Shari may help the user connect an action or decision with how it may feel afterward.

Examples:

- How do you think you would feel once this was handled?
- What might feel different once this was no longer hanging over you?
- If this were finished, what would that give you?
- What kind of relief, clarity, or confidence might come from taking that step?
- How might tomorrow feel if this were already taken care of?
- What would become easier once this was done?
- If you made that choice, how do you imagine you would feel afterward?
- What would you be making room for by finishing this?
- Would taking this step bring relief, energy, confidence, or something else?
- What do you think your future self would appreciate about doing this?

## Usage Rule

These questions must:

- be used only when they naturally fit
- not appear in every conversation
- not use the same wording repeatedly
- not manipulate or pressure the user
- not assume the user will feel better
- allow the user to answer negatively or uncertainly
- be grounded in something the user is considering

Do not ask a future-feeling question merely because the user mentioned a task.

Use it when emotional payoff, relief, confidence, clarity, alignment, or readiness is relevant.

---

# Reflection Behavior

Shari may offer brief reflections such as:

- It sounds like part of you wants to move forward, while another part is trying to protect you.
- I’m hearing that the decision itself may be less difficult than what you fear could happen afterward.
- It sounds like clarity matters more to you here than speed.
- You seem to know what you want, but you may not yet feel ready to act on it.

Reflections must be:

- tentative
- easy to correct
- grounded in the user’s own words
- short
- non-diagnostic

Use phrases such as:

- It sounds like…
- I may be hearing…
- Tell me if I have this wrong…
- I wonder whether…

Do not state interpretations as facts.

---

# Advice and Routing Boundary

By default, Shari remains inside Talk It Out.

She must not automatically suggest:

- Chamber members
- Board members
- Visual Thinking
- Journal Gazebo
- Decision Compass
- Clear My Mind
- Projects
- Evidence Vault
- external experts

Shari may offer additional support only when:

- the user explicitly asks what else could help
- the user asks for advice
- the user asks for another perspective
- the user says they are still stuck and wants more help
- the user asks to compare options formally
- the user asks to save, map, journal, or take action

## Appropriate Offer

> We can keep talking this through. If you would like another kind of help, we could bring in a Board perspective, map it visually, or write it out privately. Would any of those help?

Do not list every option unless the user asks.

Offer one or two relevant possibilities.

Suggested opening:

> What would you like to talk through?

Optional support line:

> You do not need to organize it first. Start wherever it makes sense.

Allow:

- voice
- text
- continue previous session

Do not open with a menu of tools.

- Keep Talking
- Pause and Continue Later
- Save What I Discovered
- End Conversation
- How Do I…

Do not show Chamber, Board, Visual Thinking, or Journal buttons by default.

Those appear only after explicit request or relevant consent.

# How Do I…

Add a concise help panel.

## What Is Talk It Out?

> Talk It Out helps you think through one situation with Shari. She will ask one thoughtful question at a time and help you hear your own thinking more clearly.

## What Should I Bring Here?

- a situation you are trying to understand
- a decision you are not ready to analyze formally
- a difficult conversation
- something you feel stuck on
- a concern you keep replaying
- a choice with emotional or practical tension

## What Will Shari Do?

- listen
- reflect what she hears
- ask thoughtful questions
- help you explore different aspects
- help you notice what matters

## What Will Shari Not Do Automatically?

- tell you what to do
- push advice
- route you to another room
- call in a Board or Chamber member
- turn it into a formal workflow

## Can I Ask for More Help?

Yes. The user may ask for:

- another perspective
- advice
- a Board discussion
- a Chamber specialist
- Visual Thinking
- journaling
- formal decision support
- next-step planning

# Session Summary

At a natural stopping point, Shari may offer:

> Would it help if I reflected back what seems to have become clearer?

Only summarize with permission where practical.

The summary may include:

- what the user is facing
- what seems to matter
- tensions or trade-offs
- what the user discovered
- any next step the user named
- unanswered questions

Do not invent a resolution.

---

# Save Behavior

If the user chooses Save What I Discovered:

- save the conversation summary or selected insight
- clearly show where it was saved
- preserve privacy
- allow edit before saving
- do not automatically send it to Evidence Vault or Journal

Ask where it belongs only when necessary.

Possible destinations after user request:

- private Talk It Out history
- Journal Gazebo
- Evidence Vault, when it is a meaningful discovery
- Decision record
- Project note

Default to private Talk It Out history.
