# Discovery Note Voice Standards™

**Every Discovery should feel like a thoughtful note personally written for the member.**

| | |
|---|---|
| **Parent** | [Discovery CMS](./README.md) |
| **Runtime lint** | `lib/estateDiscovery/discoveryCms/voiceLint.ts` |

---

## Always sound

- **Warm** — like a friend who is glad you are here
- **Thoughtful** — one idea at a time; no essays
- **Encouraging** — hope without hype
- **Curious** — wonder, not interrogation
- **Elegant** — premium estate, not app copy
- **Human** — would Shari say this out loud?

---

## Never sound

- Salesy · Robotic · Overly enthusiastic · Generic · Pushy · Judgmental

---

## Banned patterns (unless member asked for structure)

- "Great question" · "Let's dive in" · "It's important to note"
- "In conclusion" · "Here's a breakdown" · "Unlock your potential"
- "Game-changer" · "Take your X to the next level"
- "Something about the way you said that makes me curious" (repetitive filler)
- Multiple exclamation marks
- ALL CAPS emphasis
- Numbered step lists in Discovery body (save for rare clarity)

---

## Field guidance

| Field | Voice |
|-------|-------|
| **Title** | Official name from Knowledge Base when naming a place or feature |
| **Subtitle** | Optional whisper — never a second headline |
| **Discovery text** | What was found — present tense or gentle past; scene, not brochure |
| **Why this matters** | Kind reason to care — never guilt or urgency |
| **Food for thought** | One open question — never homework |

---

## Personal discoveries

Must be **positive and supportive** — observations, not diagnoses.

Never: "You always…" · "You never…" · shame · surveillance language.

---

## Review checklist (Shari)

1. Would I say this to someone sitting across from me?
2. Does this sound like AI?
3. Does every name match the Estate Knowledge Base?
4. Is there exactly one gentle idea?
5. Would the member feel seen — not sold to?

If any answer fails → return to **Draft**.
