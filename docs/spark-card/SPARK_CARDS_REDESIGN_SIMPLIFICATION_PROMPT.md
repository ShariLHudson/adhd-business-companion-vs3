
# Spark Estate — Spark Cards Redesign
## Cursor Implementation Prompt

# Mission

Preserve the original Spark Card personality while simplifying the experience.

Spark Cards should feel like discovering a small treasure, not reading a long article.

They should become one of the signature experiences of Spark Estate.

---

# Core Philosophy

Every Spark Card should answer one question:

> "What small spark can brighten my day today?"

The goal is:

- delight
- curiosity
- inspiration
- one meaningful takeaway
- one tiny action

Never overwhelm.

---

# Design Principles

Keep the original visual style.

Do NOT redesign Spark Cards into modern flat cards.

Preserve:

- parchment
- gold frame
- whimsical illustrations
- warm colors
- collectible appearance
- handcrafted feel

These elements are part of the Spark Estate identity.

---

# Simplify The Content

Reduce information density.

Each card should contain only:

## 1. Category Ribbon

Small ribbon.

Examples:

- Innovation
- Nature
- Leadership
- History
- Business
- Creativity
- Science
- Kindness
- Curiosity
- Amazing People

Keep categories short.

---

## 2. Title

Large.

Easy to scan.

---

## 3. Subtitle

One short line that creates curiosity.

Example:

"A small idea that changed the world."

---

## 4. Main Story

Three to five short paragraphs.

Enough to tell one memorable story.

Do not try to teach everything.

---

## 5. Today's Spark

One meaningful takeaway.

Examples:

- Great ideas often begin unexpectedly.
- Small improvements can change lives.
- Curiosity leads to innovation.

Limit to one or two sentences.

---

## 6. Spark In Action

One tiny action.

Examples:

- Leave someone an encouraging note.
- Ask one curious question today.
- Write down one new idea.
- Thank someone who helped you.

Should take under five minutes.

---

# Remove

Do not display all of these simultaneously:

- How it came about
- Impact on the world
- Fun fact
- How you can use this spark
- Multiple text panels

Instead, weave important details naturally into the story.

---

# Artwork

Artwork is the hero.

Increase its visual importance.

Reduce competing text.

Allow breathing room.

Every Spark Card should feel like an illustrated page from a beautiful storybook.

---

# Expansion

Default view stays simple.

Add:

Tell Me More

When selected, reveal:

- additional facts
- timeline
- interesting details
- related sparks

Keep advanced information hidden by default.

---

# Collection

Allow users to save cards.

Actions:

- Save
- Favorite
- Share
- Print (optional)

Create a personal Spark Collection over time.

---

# Placement

Spark Cards should appear naturally throughout Spark Estate.

Examples:

- Welcome Home
- Celebration Garden
- Journal
- Business Estate
- Projects
- Learning
- Chamber recommendations

Do not require users to visit a dedicated Spark Card page.

---

# Backgrounds

Spark Cards should look like keepsakes.

Avoid large modern white cards.

Maintain warmth and charm.

---

# Accessibility

- Large readable text
- Strong contrast
- Expandable sections
- Keyboard accessible
- Responsive layout

---

# Acceptance Criteria

Visual

- Original whimsical style preserved.
- Artwork remains central.
- Card feels collectible.

Content

- One story.
- One takeaway.
- One action.

Experience

- Default card is calm and uncluttered.
- Additional information appears only after Tell Me More.
- Users can save cards to a personal collection.

Emotional Goal

When users see today's Spark Card they should think:

"I wonder what today's little treasure is."

Spark Cards should create delight first, learning second.
