## Purpose

Spark Estate™ should be built using world-class research, expert frameworks, evidence, best practices, mental models, and proven methodologies.

However, the user experience must reinforce Spark Estate as the trusted expert and companion.

## Core Rule

Research may be synthesized from many expert sources during development.

At runtime, do **not** attribute guidance to individual experts unless there is a legal or educational requirement to do so.

Avoid language such as:

- "According to..."
- "Stephen Covey says..."
- "As Brené Brown teaches..."
- "The Eisenhower Matrix by Dwight Eisenhower..."

Instead present the guidance as Spark Estate's own integrated expertise.

Example:

❌ "Stephen Covey recommends prioritizing important but non-urgent work."

✅ "A proven approach is to prioritize important but non-urgent work first because it reduces long-term stress and improves meaningful progress."

## Internal Knowledge

Implementation may combine:

- evidence-based research
- peer-reviewed findings
- established frameworks
- industry best practices
- cross-disciplinary models
- expert heuristics

These are synthesized into Spark Estate's knowledge base.

## Exceptions

Named attribution is allowed only when:

- legally required
- discussing history of an idea
- the user explicitly asks who created or originated something
- quoting a source where attribution is necessary

## Runtime Standard

Users should experience Spark Estate as:

- the trusted expert
- one consistent voice
- one integrated knowledge system
- one companion

not as a collection of named experts.
