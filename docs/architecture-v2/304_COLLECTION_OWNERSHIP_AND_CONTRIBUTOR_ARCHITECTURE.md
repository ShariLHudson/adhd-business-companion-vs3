# 304 — Collection Ownership and Contributor Architecture

## Purpose

Define how Spark Estate Collections organize coherent experiences across multiple Chamber Members without duplication, fragmentation, or user-facing complexity.

## Collection principle

A Collection is organized around a meaningful business need, not around internal platform structure.

Examples:

- Speaker Business
- Coach Business
- Handmade Business
- Product Launch
- Event Planning
- Client Experience
- Hiring
- Business Growth
- LinkedIn Growth
- Course Creation

A Collection may use many Members while presenting one coherent experience.

## Ownership model

Every Collection must have:

### One accountable owner

The owner is responsible for:

- collection mission
- boundaries
- coherence
- asset registry
- capability requirements
- contributor orchestration
- user experience
- certification
- maintenance

### Multiple contributors

Contributors provide certified capabilities.

Contributors do not:

- create parallel versions of the Collection
- replace the owner
- copy the full Collection into their Member
- control the user experience unless explicitly delegated

## Collection types

### Intelligence Collection

Organizes a major knowledge domain.

Example:

- Marketing Strategy

### Business-Model Collection

Organizes work for a type of business professional.

Examples:

- Speaker Business
- Coach Business
- Handmade Business
- Service Business
- LinkedIn Creator Business

### Industry Collection

Organizes domain-specific workflows.

Examples:

- Nonprofit
- Real Estate
- Church
- Legal
- Healthcare

### Lifecycle Collection

Organizes a recurring business phase.

Examples:

- Start
- Build
- Launch
- Grow
- Scale
- Simplify
- Exit

### Cross-Functional Collection

Organizes a business outcome requiring multiple Members.

Examples:

- Product Launch
- Client Onboarding
- Workshop
- Hiring
- Strategic Planning

## Collection manifest

Every Collection must declare:

- ID
- name
- type
- mission
- owner
- contributors
- target business identities
- target industries
- compatible stages
- required capabilities
- optional capabilities
- assets
- entry points
- outputs
- Work Types
- dependencies
- routing signals
- user adaptation rules
- certification
- maintenance owner

## Asset architecture

A Collection may contain:

- Blueprints
- Frameworks
- Playbooks
- Templates
- Guides
- Checklists
- Calculators
- Assessments
- Reports
- Decision Tools
- Operating Systems
- Guided Experiences
- conversation patterns

Assets must not become disconnected downloads.

Each asset should declare:

- what Work it creates or supports
- what capability it uses
- where it appears
- how it adapts
- what output it generates
- how it resumes
- how it is tested

## Business-profile adaptation

Collections should adapt based on:

- active business identity
- industry
- business stage
- revenue model
- user experience by capability
- confidence
- tools
- current goals
- current constraints
- current context

Example:

The Speaker Business Collection may adapt differently for:

- a first-time speaker
- an experienced corporate keynote speaker
- an author adding speaking
- a coach using speaking for lead generation
- a low-confidence expert with strong content
- a confident speaker with weak business operations

## Cross-Collection connection

Collections may connect through:

- shared capabilities
- related Work
- dependency
- lifecycle progression
- recommendation
- shared business identity
- shared metric
- shared audience
- linked Project
- Cartography relationship

No Collection may duplicate another Collection merely to create convenience.

Use views, aliases, and relationships instead.

## Collection routing

Routing should resolve:

1. user intent
2. active Work
3. business identity
4. likely Collection
5. likely asset
6. required capabilities
7. appropriate depth
8. best next action

The user should not need to select contributors manually.

## Decision-fatigue rules

A Collection must:

- prioritize relevant assets
- avoid displaying the entire library by default
- show a meaningful next step
- remember where the user left off
- support low-energy entry
- explain why something is recommended
- avoid asking users to configure the Collection before receiving value

## Collection certification

Required tests:

- owner exists
- contributors exist
- capabilities resolve
- no duplicated owner knowledge
- assets reference canonical Work
- routing works from all supported origins
- Business DNA adaptation works
- capability/confidence adaptation works
- low-energy behavior works
- accessibility passes
- outputs are coherent
- provenance is preserved
- contributor conflicts are handled
- maintenance ownership exists

Certification verdicts:

- COLLECTION PRODUCTION CERTIFIED
- COLLECTION CONDITIONALLY CERTIFIED
- COLLECTION NOT CERTIFIED
