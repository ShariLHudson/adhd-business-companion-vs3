# 461 — Image Generation, Editing & Visual Truth Governance

## Generation inputs

- subject
- environment
- composition
- perspective
- mood
- lighting
- style
- dimensions
- negative constraints
- safe zones
- brand relationship
- intended text placement

## Editing behavior

Before editing a specific image:

- confirm the image exists
- identify what must remain unchanged
- identify requested changes
- preserve identity when requested
- avoid unrelated alteration

## Visual truth categories

### Illustrative

Clearly conceptual or artistic.

### Representative

A plausible example, not a literal record.

### Documentary or factual

Purports to show a real person, event, place, product, or result.

Documentary-style imagery requires special caution.

## Prohibited behavior

- fake documentary evidence
- fabricated before-and-after results
- invented product features
- deceptive endorsements
- false event photographs
- unauthorized identity substitution
- hidden manipulation that changes material meaning

## Human representation

- preserve requested age, ethnicity, body, and identity
- avoid changing protected traits unless explicitly requested
- preserve a real person's recognizable identity during edits
- avoid uncanny anatomy and unrealistic hands
