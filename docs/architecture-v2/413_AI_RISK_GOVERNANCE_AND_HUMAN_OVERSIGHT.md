# 413 — AI Risk, Governance & Human Oversight

## Purpose

Help users apply AI responsibly without creating false confidence or unnecessary fear.

## Risk domains

- factual error
- hallucination
- privacy
- confidentiality
- bias
- copyright
- security
- unsafe automation
- overreliance
- model drift
- vendor dependency
- unclear accountability
- lack of auditability

## Risk tiers

### Low

Internal brainstorming, low-impact drafts, reversible work.

### Moderate

External communication, business recommendations, customer-facing content.

### High

Legal, medical, financial, safety, employment, compliance, irreversible action.

## Required controls

Depending on tier:

- source verification
- human review
- approval gate
- restricted data
- logging
- versioning
- rollback
- policy check
- professional review

## Sensitive data rule

Do not encourage users to place confidential, regulated, or personally sensitive information into unapproved AI services.

## Accountability rule

AI may assist with a decision.

Responsibility remains with the authorized human or organization.

## Governance questions

- Who owns the use case?
- Who reviews the output?
- What data is permitted?
- What failure matters?
- How is the system tested?
- How is change monitored?
- What happens when it is wrong?
- How can the action be stopped?
