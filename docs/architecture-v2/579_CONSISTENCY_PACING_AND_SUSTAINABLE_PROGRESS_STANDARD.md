# 579 — Consistency, Pacing & Sustainable Progress Standard

## Purpose

Support continued movement without demanding identical performance every day.

## Consistency definition

Consistency means maintaining a useful relationship with the work over time.

It does not require:

- daily action
- equal output
- uninterrupted streaks
- perfect routines
- constant motivation

## Pacing dimensions

- energy
- focus
- available time
- urgency
- task type
- emotional demand
- cognitive demand
- physical demand
- recovery need
- dependencies
- deadline proximity

## Progress modes

- full-capacity progress
- reduced-capacity progress
- maintenance
- preparation
- review
- recovery
- intentional pause
- re-entry

## Continuity protections

At the end of a work period, preserve:

- what was completed
- what changed
- what remains
- the exact next step
- necessary context
- open decisions
- files or links
- intended return point

## Sustainable pacing rules

- avoid repeated emergency sprints
- do not treat exhaustion as commitment
- vary task type when useful
- protect recovery
- preserve momentum during low-capacity periods
- distinguish deadline urgency from chronic overload
- reduce simultaneous active commitments

## Streak safeguard

Do not frame one missed day as failure.

Use:

> “The rhythm was interrupted. Let’s make the return easy.”

## Progress review

A useful review asks:

- What moved?
- What created friction?
- What helped?
- What should be preserved?
- What should change?
- What is the next meaningful continuation point?
