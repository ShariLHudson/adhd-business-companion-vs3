# 522 — Adoption, Commercialization & Scaling Intelligence

## Purpose

Bridge the gap between a validated concept and sustainable use.

## Adoption dimensions

- awareness
- understanding
- perceived value
- trust
- effort
- compatibility
- habit change
- switching cost
- social proof
- support
- risk
- timing

## Commercialization questions

- Who pays?
- Who uses?
- Who approves?
- What triggers purchase or adoption?
- What alternatives exist?
- What must be explained?
- What delivery capability is required?
- What support burden exists?
- What margins are possible?
- What evidence is still missing?

## Scale-readiness dimensions

- repeatable demand
- repeatable delivery
- stable quality
- support capacity
- economics
- technology reliability
- compliance
- training
- measurement
- customer success
- operational ownership

## Premature-scaling safeguards

Do not recommend scaling when:

- demand is not repeatable
- delivery depends on heroic effort
- quality is unstable
- unit economics are unclear
- retention is weak
- support load is unknown
- the pilot population is unrepresentative

## Handoff rule

When uncertainty is sufficiently reduced:

- Strategy owns commitment
- Projects owns execution
- Operations owns repeatability
- Marketing owns market activation
- Client Experience owns adoption experience
