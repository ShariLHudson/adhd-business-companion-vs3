# 576 — Momentum Canonical Ownership & Non-Duplication Contract

## Momentum owns

- activation
- initiation support
- friction diagnosis
- progress preservation
- continuity
- consistency
- pacing
- re-entry
- recovery from stalls
- momentum protection
- completion support
- sustainable motivation
- progress visibility
- small-win recognition
- celebration handoffs
- momentum pattern memory

## Projects owns

- project structure
- tasks
- subtasks
- milestones
- owners
- dates
- dependencies
- execution status

Projects answers: “What must be done?”
Momentum answers: “What will help this person begin, continue, or re-enter?”

## Learning owns

- skill acquisition
- understanding
- practice
- mastery
- retention
- transfer

Momentum may identify that a skill gap is blocking movement, but Learning owns the learning response.

## Strategy owns

- priorities
- tradeoffs
- business direction
- resource commitments
- what should be pursued

Momentum does not motivate users to pursue work that has not been strategically chosen.

## Shari owns

- whole-person context
- relational continuity
- supportive conversation
- reflective guidance
- platform-wide awareness

## Celebration Garden, Celebration Hall, and Hall of Accomplishments own

- celebration experiences
- ceremonial recognition
- preserved accomplishments
- optional celebration sounds and visual moments

Momentum identifies and qualifies the moment for celebration. The celebration destinations own the experience.

## Rhythms owns

- reminders
- recurring supports
- quiet hours
- snooze, pause, resume, skip, and completion mechanics
- temporal delivery

Momentum may recommend a rhythm. Rhythms owns scheduling and recurrence.

## Non-duplication rule

Momentum must not become:

- a task manager
- a project planner
- a rigid habit tracker
- a motivational quote engine
- a guilt-based accountability coach
- a mental health clinician
- a celebration destination
- a substitute for strategic prioritization
