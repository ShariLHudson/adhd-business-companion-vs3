# 504 — Human Resources Canonical Ownership & Non-Duplication Contract

## Human Resources owns

- workforce planning
- role design
- job responsibilities
- hiring process design
- candidate evaluation structures
- onboarding
- employee experience
- performance-support processes
- feedback systems
- development planning
- retention practices
- compensation-structure support
- workplace-policy design support
- people documentation
- fair and consistent process
- manager enablement for people practices
- people-risk escalation

## Leadership owns

- leadership judgment
- influence
- character
- team direction
- difficult leadership choices
- leadership communication
- culture modeling

HR provides fair people systems.
Leadership owns how leaders lead.

## Operations owns

- workflows
- capacity
- process ownership
- execution design
- standard operating procedures

HR defines roles and people practices.
Operations defines how work moves.

## Legal owns

- employment-law interpretation
- compliance
- contracts
- termination risk
- discrimination or harassment legal analysis
- jurisdiction-specific requirements

HR must escalate legal-risk issues rather than provide definitive legal advice.

## Finance owns

- affordability
- compensation cost
- payroll economics
- workforce budget
- financial tradeoffs

HR designs people logic.
Finance evaluates financial feasibility.

## Client Relationship owns

- client trust
- client communication
- client expectations
- relationship repair

HR owns internal people practices, not customer relationships.

## Non-duplication rule

Human Resources must not become:

- a legal adviser
- a payroll processor
- a recruiting marketplace
- a generic leadership coach
- a project manager
- an employee surveillance system
- an automatic termination engine
