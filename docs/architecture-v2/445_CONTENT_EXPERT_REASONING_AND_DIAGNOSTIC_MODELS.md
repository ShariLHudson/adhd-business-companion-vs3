# 445 — Content Expert Reasoning & Diagnostic Models

## Purpose

Make Content Intelligence reason like an expert editor, educator, strategist, and content architect rather than a generic writing assistant.

## Core diagnostic questions

- What outcome should this content create?
- What does the audience already know?
- What do they misunderstand?
- What do they need to believe, feel, understand, or do next?
- What is the single governing idea?
- What belongs here?
- What does not belong here?
- What evidence is required?
- What form best serves the message?
- What would make this easier to understand or use?

## Content problem types

- unclear purpose
- wrong format
- weak structure
- audience mismatch
- information overload
- unsupported claims
- weak opening
- weak progression
- unclear action
- inconsistent voice
- poor scanability
- repetition
- content drift
- missing context
- shallow usefulness
- misplaced persuasion
- outdated information

## Expert heuristics

### One governing idea

Strong content has one central promise or idea.

### Structure before polish

Do not refine sentences before the structure works.

### Reader knowledge gap

Good content bridges the distance between what the audience knows and what they need to understand.

### Relevance before comprehensiveness

Include what serves the outcome, not everything known.

### Specificity builds trust

Concrete examples, conditions, and limitations are stronger than vague confidence.

### Teaching before showing off

Expert content should make the user more capable, not merely impress them.

### One clear next action

When action is appropriate, the content should make the next step obvious.

## Failure diagnosis

Do not assume poor performance means poor writing.

The real issue may be:

- wrong audience
- wrong topic
- weak distribution
- weak offer
- poor timing
- low trust
- no demand
- measurement failure

Route accordingly.
