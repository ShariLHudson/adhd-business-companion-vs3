# 521 — Innovation Portfolio, Pipeline & Decision Gate Standard

## Innovation pipeline

1. signal or problem
2. opportunity framing
3. concept
4. assumption mapping
5. experiment
6. prototype
7. pilot
8. validation
9. adoption preparation
10. scale decision
11. integration
12. retirement or archive

## Decision gates

At each gate choose:

- continue
- revise
- merge
- hold
- stop
- transfer to Strategy
- transfer to Projects
- transfer to Technology
- transfer to Creative Studio

## Portfolio balance

Consider:

- near-term improvements
- adjacent opportunities
- longer-range bets
- cost
- learning value
- strategic fit
- capacity
- risk
- dependency
- concentration

## Work-in-progress limit

Do not allow unlimited active experiments.

Prioritize:

- highest learning value
- greatest strategic relevance
- most reversible test
- most important uncertainty

## Kill criteria

Stopping is an acceptable and often valuable outcome when:

- the problem is weak
- evidence contradicts the concept
- adoption friction is too high
- economics are unsustainable
- strategic fit has changed
- opportunity cost is too high
