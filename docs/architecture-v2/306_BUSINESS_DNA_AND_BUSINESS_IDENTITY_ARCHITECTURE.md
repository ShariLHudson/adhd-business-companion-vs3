# 306 — Business DNA and Business Identity Architecture

## Purpose

Define how Spark Estate understands who the user is professionally, how their business works, and which Collections, capabilities, and experiences are most relevant.

Business DNA must never reduce a person to one fixed label.

A user may have:

- multiple businesses
- multiple professional identities
- multiple revenue models
- different audiences
- different stages of development
- different capability levels in different areas

## Core principle

Spark Estate should not ask:

> Which Chamber Member do you want?

It should understand:

- what the user does
- how they make money
- who they serve
- what they are building
- what stage they are in
- what they are trying to accomplish now

Then it should quietly assemble the right Collections and capabilities.

- operating constraints

## Anti-assumptions

An identity must not automatically imply:

- business size
- income
- experience
- confidence
- preferred platform
- political or cultural beliefs
- technical skill
- team size
- goals
- desire to scale
- desire to hire
- willingness to use social media
- willingness to appear on camera

## Discovery approach

Business DNA should be built gradually.

Use:

- natural conversation
- existing Work
- Business Estate data
- confirmed Profile data
- Projects
- goals
- connected tools
- observed patterns
- direct user corrections

Do not require a long intake before delivering value.

## Confidence and consent

Every data point should be classified as:

- user confirmed
- strongly inferred
- weakly inferred
- stale
- contradicted
- unknown

Material inferences must be shown before they affect consequential recommendations.

## Multiple-business support

Spark Estate must allow users to:

- switch business context
- view all businesses
- connect Work across businesses
- keep confidential data separate
- share capabilities and preferences where appropriate
- preserve business-specific goals and metrics
- avoid applying one business's assumptions to another

## Required tests

- multiple identities per business
- multiple businesses per user
- stage by function
- revenue-model combinations
- anti-assumption enforcement
- confirmed versus inferred data
- stale data handling
- user correction
- Collection recommendation
- no forced onboarding
- privacy boundaries
- exact business-context resume
