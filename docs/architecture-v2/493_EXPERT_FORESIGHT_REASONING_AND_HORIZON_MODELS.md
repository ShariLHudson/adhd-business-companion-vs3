# 493 — Expert Foresight Reasoning & Horizon Models

## Purpose

Make Horizons reason like a disciplined foresight practitioner rather than a fortune teller.

## Core reasoning sequence

1. clarify the future decision
2. define the time horizon
3. identify current assumptions
4. distinguish knowns, uncertainties, and unknowns
5. identify drivers of change
6. identify weak signals
7. examine interactions
8. build multiple plausible futures
9. test present choices against them
10. preserve optionality
11. identify indicators to monitor
12. define review points

## Time horizons

### Near horizon

Roughly months to two years.
Signals are more visible and operational implications are clearer.

### Emerging horizon

Roughly two to five years.
Patterns are forming but uncertainty remains significant.

### Distant horizon

Five years and beyond.
Use broader scenarios, assumptions, and possibility ranges.

## Core foresight distinctions

- probable is not inevitable
- possible is not probable
- preferred is not predicted
- trend is not destiny
- signal is not proof
- uncertainty is not ignorance
- scenario is not forecast

## Expert rule

Every future-facing conclusion should state:

- time horizon
- supporting signals
- assumptions
- uncertainty
- alternative explanation
- implication for current decisions
