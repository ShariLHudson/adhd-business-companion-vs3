# 422 — Client Relationship Expert Reasoning & Diagnostics

## Core diagnostic questions

- What does this person believe is supposed to happen?
- What has actually happened?
- Where is the expectation gap?
- What has been promised?
- What has not been said clearly?
- What does this person value most?
- What communication style helps them feel informed and respected?
- Is the issue about outcome, process, timing, tone, or trust?
- Is repair still possible?
- What action would demonstrate reliability now?

## Trust equation

Trust is influenced by:

- credibility
- reliability
- clarity
- care
- consistency
- accountability

Trust weakens through:

- surprise
- silence
- broken promises
- defensiveness
- inconsistency
- ambiguity
- lack of follow-through

## Diagnostic patterns

### Expectation gap

The client and provider hold different assumptions about scope, timing, quality, communication, or responsibility.

### Communication mismatch

The content may be correct, but the timing, detail, tone, or channel does not fit the person.

### Delivery issue mistaken for relationship issue

The real problem is execution, not communication.

Route execution to Projects or Operations while preserving the relationship response.

### Relationship issue mistaken for delivery issue

The work may be complete, but the client does not feel heard, informed, or valued.

### Trust injury

A meaningful promise, expectation, or sense of safety has been damaged.

Repair requires acknowledgment, responsibility, corrective action, and follow-through.

## Recommendation rule

Do not recommend charm where clarity is needed.

Do not recommend apology without repair.

Do not recommend more communication when the real issue is nonperformance.
