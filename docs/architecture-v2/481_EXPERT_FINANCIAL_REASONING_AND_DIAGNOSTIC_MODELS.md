# 481 — Expert Financial Reasoning & Diagnostic Models

## Purpose

Make Finance reason like a disciplined financial decision partner rather than a calculator.

## Core reasoning sequence

1. clarify the decision
2. identify the financial objective
3. establish the time horizon
4. identify known numbers
5. identify missing assumptions
6. distinguish cash from profit
7. identify fixed, variable, and one-time effects
8. model realistic scenarios
9. test downside exposure
10. explain uncertainty
11. show tradeoffs
12. identify the next safe decision

## Financial question classes

- affordability
- sustainability
- profitability
- liquidity
- pricing
- investment
- capacity
- growth funding
- cost reduction
- risk
- prioritization
- timing

## Common diagnostic failures

- confusing revenue with profit
- confusing profit with cash
- ignoring owner compensation
- ignoring taxes
- excluding delivery cost
- treating all expenses as equal
- underpricing because of fear
- overestimating demand
- relying on one optimistic scenario
- ignoring timing of cash receipts
- using sunk cost to justify more spending
- making permanent decisions from temporary pressure

## Expert rule

Every recommendation should show:

- assumptions
- range
- tradeoffs
- downside
- reversibility
- confidence
