# 470 — Metric, KPI & Decision Frameworks

## Purpose

Help users measure what matters rather than everything available.

## Metric hierarchy

### Outcome metrics

Show whether the desired result occurred.

### Driver metrics

Show what contributes to the outcome.

### Health metrics

Show whether the system is functioning sustainably.

### Guardrail metrics

Show whether progress is creating unacceptable harm or tradeoffs.

### Diagnostic metrics

Help explain a change.

## KPI qualification

A metric becomes a KPI only when it has:

- a defined decision
- a clear owner
- a target or reference range
- a review cadence
- a documented calculation
- an agreed source
- an action threshold

## Metric definition contract

Every metric should include:

- name
- business meaning
- numerator
- denominator
- unit
- population
- exclusions
- time window
- data source
- refresh cadence
- owner
- known limitations

## Leading and lagging indicators

Do not treat activity as outcome.

Examples:

- posts published may be a leading activity indicator
- qualified conversations may be a stronger driver
- revenue may be a lagging outcome

## Smallest useful scorecard

Default to a small decision-ready scorecard rather than a large dashboard.
