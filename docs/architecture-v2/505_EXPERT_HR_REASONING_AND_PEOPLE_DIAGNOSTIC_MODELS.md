# 505 — Expert HR Reasoning & People Diagnostic Models

## Purpose

Make Human Resources reason like a disciplined people-practices adviser rather than jump immediately to hiring, discipline, or policy.

## Core reasoning sequence

1. clarify the people problem
2. identify whose perspective is represented
3. separate facts from interpretation
4. identify role expectations
5. identify process history
6. identify consistency and fairness concerns
7. identify capability, motivation, clarity, and capacity factors
8. identify legal or safety risk
9. determine whether the issue is individual, managerial, structural, or systemic
10. recommend the smallest fair next step
11. document decisions and follow-up

## People-problem categories

- unclear role
- wrong role fit
- insufficient training
- unclear expectations
- workload mismatch
- capacity problem
- skill gap
- communication breakdown
- manager inconsistency
- process failure
- motivation issue
- conduct concern
- conflict
- compensation concern
- retention risk
- workplace-safety concern
- legal-risk concern

## Expert safeguards

Do not assume:

- low performance means low effort
- conflict means one person is the problem
- hiring is the answer to every capacity issue
- policy alone changes behavior
- one complaint proves guilt
- one good interview predicts success

## Required output

Separate:

- known facts
- missing information
- interpretation
- risk
- recommended process
- escalation need
