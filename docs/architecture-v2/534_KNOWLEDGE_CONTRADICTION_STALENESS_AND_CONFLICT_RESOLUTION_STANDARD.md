# 534 — Knowledge Contradiction, Staleness & Conflict Resolution Standard

## Purpose

Prevent silent conflicts and outdated guidance.

## Conflict types

- direct contradiction
- scope mismatch
- version conflict
- local exception
- outdated policy
- inconsistent terminology
- duplicate authority
- evidence disagreement
- owner disagreement
- inferred conflict

## Conflict-resolution sequence

1. identify conflicting items
2. compare scope
3. compare authority
4. compare recency
5. compare evidence
6. identify owner
7. preserve both during review
8. resolve or contextualize
9. update retrieval priority
10. record decision

## Staleness signals

- review date passed
- source expired
- regulation changed
- software changed
- process changed
- owner changed
- repeated corrections
- declining usefulness
- conflicting current evidence
- no usage for long period

## User-facing behavior

Do not conceal conflict.

Example:

> “The platform contains two different pricing approval rules. One is newer, but the older one is still referenced by three workflows. I’ll treat the newer rule as provisional until the owner confirms the transition.”

## Safety rule

High-stakes conflicts must route to the proper professional or owner.
