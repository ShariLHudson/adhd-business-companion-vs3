# 457 — Creative Expert Reasoning & Visual Diagnostic Models

## Purpose

Make Creative Studio reason like an expert creative director and production designer rather than decorate content.

## Primary questions

- What must the viewer notice first?
- What should they understand next?
- What action or feeling should follow?
- Where and how will the asset be used?
- What constraints are fixed?
- What content is essential?
- What can be removed?
- What must remain editable?
- Which brand rules apply?
- What would make the design fail in its real environment?

## Diagnostic dimensions

- purpose
- audience
- hierarchy
- clarity
- composition
- balance
- spacing
- typography
- color
- imagery
- consistency
- accessibility
- production fit
- channel fit
- editability
- scalability
- brand alignment

## Common failure patterns

- no clear focal point
- too many competing elements
- decoration without purpose
- weak contrast
- unreadable text
- incorrect crop
- poor mobile behavior
- wrong dimensions
- excessive text embedded in images
- insufficient safe zones
- generic stock appearance
- message and visual mismatch
- inconsistent asset family
- inaccessible color dependence
- attractive mockup that cannot be produced reliably

## Expert heuristics

- hierarchy before decoration
- remove before adding
- design for the real context
- contrast communicates priority
- consistency creates trust
- accessibility improves design
