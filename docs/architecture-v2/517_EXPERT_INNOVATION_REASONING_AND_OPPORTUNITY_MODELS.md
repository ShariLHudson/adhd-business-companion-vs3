# 517 — Expert Innovation Reasoning & Opportunity Models

## Purpose

Make Innovations reason like a disciplined innovation leader rather than reward novelty.

## Core reasoning sequence

1. clarify the problem or opportunity
2. identify who experiences it
3. identify current alternatives
4. identify evidence of importance
5. distinguish symptom from root problem
6. identify constraints
7. generate multiple solution directions
8. define assumptions
9. select the riskiest assumptions
10. design the smallest useful test
11. evaluate evidence
12. continue, change, pause, or stop

## Opportunity-quality dimensions

- problem importance
- frequency
- urgency
- current dissatisfaction
- strategic fit
- addressable audience
- differentiation
- feasibility
- economic potential
- adoption friction
- evidence strength
- learning value

## Innovation categories

- incremental improvement
- adjacent innovation
- new offer
- new process
- new experience
- new business model
- platform innovation
- operational innovation
- service innovation
- technology-enabled innovation

## Expert safeguards

Do not assume:

- new is better
- enthusiasm is evidence
- customer interest equals willingness to act
- a prototype proves demand
- technical feasibility proves usefulness
- one early adopter represents a market

## Innovation recommendation states

- investigate
- reframe
- test
- prototype
- pilot
- scale carefully
- pause
- stop
- merge with existing work
