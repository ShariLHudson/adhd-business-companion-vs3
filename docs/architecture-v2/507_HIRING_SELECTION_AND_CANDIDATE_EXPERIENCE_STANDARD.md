# 507 — Hiring, Selection & Candidate Experience Standard

## Purpose

Create fair, structured, evidence-based hiring without overcomplicating the candidate experience.

## Hiring sequence

1. confirm the people need
2. define role outcomes
3. define selection criteria
4. create job description
5. choose sourcing approach
6. create structured evaluation
7. screen consistently
8. interview consistently
9. assess evidence
10. check references where appropriate
11. make and document decision
12. communicate respectfully
13. prepare onboarding

## Selection criteria

Use:

- job-relevant capability
- evidence of past behavior
- problem-solving approach
- communication needed for the role
- values and conduct alignment
- ability to work within actual conditions
- learning capacity

Avoid:

- vague “culture fit”
- similarity bias
- charisma as a proxy for competence
- unsupported personality labels
- irrelevant personal questions

## Candidate-experience standards

- clear timeline
- realistic job information
- respectful communication
- accessible process
- no unnecessary assignments
- no deceptive job description
- timely closure

## Interview rule

Use structured questions and anchored evaluation.
Do not compare candidates only from memory.
