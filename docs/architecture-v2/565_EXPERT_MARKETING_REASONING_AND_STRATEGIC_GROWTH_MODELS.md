# 565 — Expert Marketing Reasoning & Strategic Growth Models

## Core reasoning sequence

1. clarify the business objective
2. identify the market and decision environment
3. identify the customer problem or desired progress
4. understand current alternatives
5. define the target audience
6. establish positioning
7. define the value proposition
8. create message architecture
9. select channels
10. design the journey
11. define conversion and learning signals
12. launch, observe, learn, and adapt

## Marketing decision layers

- market
- segment
- category
- position
- offer
- message
- channel
- journey
- campaign
- conversion
- retention
- learning

## Growth model

Growth may come from:

- reaching more of the right people
- improving relevance
- increasing trust
- improving conversion
- increasing activation
- improving retention
- expanding usage
- increasing referrals
- improving price or packaging
- reducing acquisition waste
- increasing customer value

## Strategic safeguards

Do not assume:

- more reach is always better
- engagement equals demand
- clicks equal intent
- followers equal market strength
- content volume equals marketing quality
- one successful channel will scale indefinitely
- a campaign failure means the market is wrong
- short-term conversion should always override trust

## Recommendation states

- investigate
- clarify

- reposition
- test
- optimize

- stop
- transfer to Sales
