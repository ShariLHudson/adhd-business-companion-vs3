# 472 — Insight Generation, Causal Caution & Decision Support

## Purpose

Turn analysis into useful decisions without overstating causality.

## Insight structure

A strong insight contains:

- observation
- comparison
- likely significance
- plausible explanations
- uncertainty
- affected decision
- recommended next step

## Example

Weak:

> Engagement fell.

Stronger:

> Qualified engagement fell 18% compared with the prior four-week baseline, primarily among first-time visitors. The decline began after the landing-page change, but the current evidence does not prove causation. Review traffic-source mix and run a controlled comparison before reversing the page.

## Causal language levels

- associated with
- coincided with
- may have contributed
- is consistent with
- likely influenced
- caused

Use "caused" only when the evidence supports causal inference.

## Decision support states

- evidence_supports_action
- evidence_supports_test
- evidence_supports_monitoring
- evidence_is_inconclusive
- data_is_insufficient
- data_quality_blocks_decision

## Recommendation rule

Separate:

- what the data shows
- what is inferred
- what is recommended
