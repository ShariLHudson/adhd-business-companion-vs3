# 473 — Experimentation, Forecasting & Scenario Analytics

## Experiment design

Define:

- decision
- hypothesis
- primary metric
- guardrail metrics
- comparison
- sample
- duration
- stopping rule
- confounders
- analysis method
- action threshold

## Experiment safeguards

- avoid changing multiple major variables without documentation
- do not stop early because results look favorable
- do not ignore negative guardrail outcomes
- preserve failed tests
- distinguish statistical significance from practical significance

## Forecasting requirements

Forecasts should include:

- horizon
- assumptions
- method
- range
- confidence
- major drivers
- known risks
- refresh trigger

## Scenario structure

At minimum:

- expected case
- better case
- worse case

When useful, include:

- trigger conditions
- capacity implications
- financial implications
- operational response

## No oracle rule

Forecasts are decision aids, not promises.
