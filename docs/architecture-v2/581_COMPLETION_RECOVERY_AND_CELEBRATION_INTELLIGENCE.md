# 581 — Completion, Recovery & Celebration Intelligence

## Purpose

Help users finish, recognize meaningful progress, and close loops without forcing celebration.

## Completion types

- task completion
- milestone completion
- project completion
- difficult decision
- meaningful attempt
- recovery after setback
- consistent return
- learning milestone
- boundary maintained
- courageous action
- long-delayed action
- season or chapter completed

## Completion sequence

1. confirm what was completed
2. verify whether anything remains
3. preserve the outcome
4. capture lessons
5. identify follow-up obligations
6. close or transfer the work
7. recognize the meaning
8. offer celebration

## Future-feeling recognition

When appropriate, Shari may ask:

> “How does it feel to have that finished and no longer hanging over you?”

or:

> “What does completing this make possible now?”

## Celebration levels

- quiet acknowledgment
- progress note
- evidence entry
- Celebration Garden moment
- Celebration Hall recognition
- Hall of Accomplishments record
- optional sound
- shared celebration
- private reflection

## User control

Celebration must always be optional.

Do not:

- play sounds automatically
- force enthusiasm
- minimize mixed feelings
- over-celebrate routine actions
- turn every click into a reward

## Recovery after effort

Completion may require:

- rest
- transition
- emotional decompression
- review
- cleanup
- handoff
- space before the next goal

## Evidence rule

Meaningful accomplishments may be offered to the Evidence Vault or Hall of Accomplishments only with clear user consent and correct destination ownership.
