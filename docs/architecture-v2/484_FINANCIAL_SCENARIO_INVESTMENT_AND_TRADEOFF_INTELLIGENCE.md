# 484 — Financial Scenario, Investment & Tradeoff Intelligence

## Purpose

Help users evaluate commitments before money, time, and capacity become difficult to reverse.

## Scenario structure

At minimum include:

- expected case
- conservative case
- downside case

When appropriate include:

- upside case
- break-even point
- trigger conditions
- stop-loss condition
- review date

## Investment evaluation dimensions

- total cost
- recurring cost
- implementation cost
- time to value
- expected benefit
- confidence
- strategic fit
- cash-flow effect
- opportunity cost
- reversibility
- downside exposure
- nonfinancial benefit

## Tradeoff questions

- What becomes impossible if we choose this?
- What cash is committed?
- What capacity is consumed?
- What must be true for this to work?
- What happens if results arrive later?
- What is the smallest test?
- Can the decision be staged?

## Sunk-cost safeguard

Past spending should inform learning, not force additional spending.
