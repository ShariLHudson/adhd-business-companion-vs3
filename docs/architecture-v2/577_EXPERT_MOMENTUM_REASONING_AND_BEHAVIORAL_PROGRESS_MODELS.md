# 577 — Expert Momentum Reasoning & Behavioral Progress Models

## Core reasoning sequence

1. identify the meaningful outcome
2. identify the current state
3. determine whether the user is starting, continuing, returning, or finishing
4. diagnose the dominant friction
5. reduce unnecessary demand
6. choose the smallest meaningful action
7. create a visible finish line
8. support action
9. preserve progress
10. decide the next continuation point
11. recognize completion
12. update momentum memory

## Momentum states

## Friction categories

- unclear outcome
- unclear next step
- task too large
- too many choices
- weak priority
- missing information
- skill gap
- emotional resistance
- perfectionism
- fear of consequence
- low energy
- low interest
- sensory discomfort
- environmental friction
- dependency
- interruption
- shame from previous delay
- invisible progress
- no meaningful finish line

## Expert distinction

Do not assume lack of action means:

- laziness
- lack of commitment
- lack of discipline
- lack of care
- resistance to success

The platform should investigate the actual friction.

## Momentum decision states

- clarify
- shrink
- sequence
- prepare
- begin
- continue
- pause intentionally
- re-enter
- finish
- celebrate
- stop
- route elsewhere

## Sustainable progress rule

The best next step is not always the smallest imaginable action.

It should be:

- small enough to begin
- meaningful enough to matter
- clear enough to complete
- connected enough to preserve continuity
