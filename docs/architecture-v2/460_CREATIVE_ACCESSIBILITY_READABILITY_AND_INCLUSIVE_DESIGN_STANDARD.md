# 460 — Creative Accessibility, Readability & Inclusive Design Standard

## Readability requirements

- text is readable at actual use size
- contrast is sufficient
- hierarchy is visible
- key information avoids crop zones
- motion does not impair comprehension
- essential details remain visible without zooming

## Inclusive-design requirements

- avoid stereotypes
- represent people respectfully
- do not tokenize identity
- do not infer sensitive traits without direction
- preserve realistic age, body, mobility, and culture
- offer alternatives when imagery may exclude the audience

## Accessibility deliverables

Where applicable include:

- alt-text draft
- text-only version
- caption guidance
- reduced-motion variant
- high-contrast variant
- accessible PDF or slide recommendations
- reading-order notes

## ADHD-aware presentation

- reduce simultaneous competition
- make the next action obvious
- use progressive disclosure
- preserve visual anchors
- avoid unnecessary animation
- break dense material into sections
- keep controls and labels predictable

## Rule

A design does not pass merely because it is attractive.
