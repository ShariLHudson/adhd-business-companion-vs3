# 568 — Marketing Measurement, Attribution & Experimentation Standard

## Measurement hierarchy

### Business outcomes
- revenue
- qualified demand
- customer value
- retention
- profitability
- strategic market progress

### Journey outcomes
- awareness
- consideration
- activation
- conversion
- retention
- expansion
- advocacy

### Diagnostic signals
- reach
- impressions
- engagement
- click-through
- page behavior
- response
- cost
- frequency
- completion

Diagnostic signals are not automatically business outcomes.

## Measurement plan

Define before launch:

- objective
- primary metric
- supporting metrics
- guardrail metrics
- baseline
- target
- time window
- source
- segmentation
- decision threshold
- known limitations

## Attribution standard

Attribution is an estimate of influence, not perfect proof.

Record:

- attribution model
- data gaps
- tracking limitations
- offline influence
- multi-touch effects
- time lag
- confidence

## Experiment design

Define:

- hypothesis
- variable
- audience
- control or comparison
- expected outcome
- minimum useful evidence
- duration
- contamination risks
- stop condition
- next decision

## Invalid patterns

- claiming causation from correlation
- changing the primary metric after launch
- optimizing only for clicks
- ignoring customer quality
- declaring victory from tiny samples
- hiding negative segments
- scaling before operational readiness
