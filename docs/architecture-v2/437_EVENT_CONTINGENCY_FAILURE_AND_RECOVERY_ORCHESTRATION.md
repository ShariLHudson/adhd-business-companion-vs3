# 437 — Event Contingency, Failure & Recovery Orchestration

## Purpose

Prepare for disruption and support recovery without catastrophizing.

## Common disruptions

- speaker cancellation
- venue problem
- technology failure
- low registration
- overcapacity
- weather
- catering failure
- accessibility breakdown
- staffing absence
- delayed materials
- sponsor problem
- schedule overrun
- attendee conflict
- medical or safety incident

## Contingency structure

Every critical risk should define:

- trigger
- owner
- immediate response
- backup option
- communication
- attendee impact
- decision deadline
- recovery step
- documentation requirement

## Recovery priority order

1. safety
2. essential event outcome
3. participant clarity
4. continuity
5. experience quality
6. nonessential polish

## No false calm rule

Do not minimize serious risk.

## No panic amplification rule

Do not present every possible failure at once.

Surface the most relevant contingency based on current context.
